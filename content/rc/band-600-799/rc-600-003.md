# Customer Service Policy Revision

From: operations@grandviewretail.com
To: all-staff@grandviewretail.com
Date: February 20
Subject: Important Changes to Customer Service and Return Policies

Dear Team,

Following an extensive review of customer feedback collected throughout the past year, the Operations Department has finalized several revisions to our customer service and return policies. These changes will take effect on March 1 and are designed to improve customer satisfaction while maintaining operational efficiency.

First, the standard return window will be extended from 14 days to 30 days for all merchandise purchased in-store or online. Customers will be required to present a valid receipt or order confirmation number at the time of return. Items returned without proof of purchase will be eligible only for store credit at the current selling price.

Second, we are introducing a dedicated customer resolution hotline for handling escalated complaints. This line will be staffed by specially trained representatives who have the authority to issue refunds of up to $200 without requiring managerial approval. For amounts exceeding this threshold, standard escalation procedures will continue to apply.

Third, all customer-facing employees are required to complete an updated training module by March 15. The module covers the revised policies, conflict resolution techniques, and the new point-of-sale procedures associated with extended returns. Training schedules will be posted on the employee portal by February 25.

If you have any questions about the new policies, please contact Rachel Dunn in the Operations Department at extension 3187 or via email at r.dunn@grandviewretail.com.

Best regards,
Marcus Webb
Vice President of Operations

## Q137. What prompted the changes described in this email?
- A. A decline in quarterly revenue at all store locations
- B. A review of customer feedback gathered over the past year
- C. A request from the board of directors to reduce costs
- D. A regulatory requirement imposed by consumer protection authorities
- Answer: B
- Explanation: The email states the revisions were made "following an extensive review of customer feedback collected throughout the past year," identifying customer feedback as the driving factor.
