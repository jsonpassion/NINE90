# Regional Banking Trends: Digital Transformation Reshapes Customer Expectations

A report published by the Financial Services Advisory Group examines how digital transformation is reshaping customer expectations in regional banking. Based on survey data from 8,500 retail banking customers across the southeastern United States, the report highlights several key trends that are likely to influence strategic planning at community banks and regional institutions over the next five years.

The most striking finding is that 82 percent of respondents now prefer digital channels for routine transactions such as balance inquiries, fund transfers, and bill payments. However, for complex financial decisions including mortgage applications, investment planning, and business loans, 71 percent still prefer in-person consultations with a banking advisor. This dual preference creates a challenge for banks that must invest in both digital infrastructure and personalized in-branch services.

Customer satisfaction scores were highest among banks that offered a seamless experience across multiple channels. Specifically, customers who could initiate a process online and complete it in a branch, or vice versa, reported satisfaction rates 23 percent higher than customers limited to a single channel. The report suggests that this omnichannel capability should be a top priority for banks seeking to retain and attract customers.

The report also found that customers under 35 are twice as likely as those over 55 to switch banks based on digital capabilities alone. This generational divide underscores the urgency for traditional banks to accelerate their digital transformation efforts while maintaining the relationship-based service model that older customers value.
