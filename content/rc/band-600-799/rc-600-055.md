# Quarterly Performance Review: Grandview Hospitality Group

Grandview Hospitality Group reports solid third-quarter results across its portfolio of 38 hotels and resorts in the United States and Caribbean. Revenue per available room, the industry's key performance metric, reached $142, representing a 7 percent increase over the same quarter in 2024 and the highest level in the company's history.

Occupancy rates averaged 78 percent across the portfolio, up from 74 percent in the prior year. The improvement was driven by strong demand from both leisure and business travel segments. Business travel revenue, which had been the slowest segment to recover from pandemic-era declines, grew 15 percent year-over-year, supported by the return of group meetings and corporate events to pre-pandemic levels.

The luxury segment was the strongest performer. The company's six luxury properties achieved an average occupancy of 84 percent and an average daily rate of $380, with the Grandview Coral Bay Resort in the Caribbean achieving the highest rate in its history at $520 per night. Management attributes this performance to the completion of a $32 million renovation program that upgraded guest rooms, dining facilities, and wellness amenities at the resort.

Operating margins improved by 150 basis points to 28.3 percent, supported by revenue growth and the implementation of a workforce optimization program that has reduced overtime expenses by $4.2 million across the portfolio without affecting service quality scores. Technology investments, including a new revenue management system that uses dynamic pricing algorithms, contributed an estimated $6 million in incremental revenue during the quarter.

Looking ahead, advance bookings for the fourth quarter are 12 percent above the same point last year, providing confidence in continued strong performance through the holiday travel season.

- Answer: A
- Explanation: The report describes "Revenue per available room" as "the industry's key performance metric."
