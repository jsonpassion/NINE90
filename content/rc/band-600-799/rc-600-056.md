# Memorandum: Revised Procurement Authorization Levels

TO: All Department Heads and Purchasing Managers
FROM: Chief Procurement Officer
DATE: November 5, 2025
RE: Updated Procurement Authorization and Approval Framework

Effective December 1, 2025, revised procurement authorization levels will take effect across all divisions of Caldwell Industries. These changes result from a comprehensive review of our purchasing processes and are designed to accelerate routine procurement while maintaining appropriate financial controls for significant expenditures.

The updated authorization framework is as follows. Department managers may approve purchases up to $10,000 without additional authorization, increased from the previous limit of $5,000. This change recognizes that many routine office supplies, software licenses, and maintenance services fall within this range and have been unnecessarily delayed by multi-level approval requirements.

Division directors may approve purchases between $10,000 and $50,000. Purchases in this range must include a minimum of two competitive quotes from qualified vendors. The previous requirement for three quotes in this tier has been reduced to balance procurement rigor with processing efficiency.

Purchases between $50,000 and $250,000 require approval from the relevant vice president and the Chief Procurement Officer. A formal request for proposal must be issued to a minimum of three vendors, and the procurement team must complete a documented vendor evaluation using the standardized scoring matrix.

Any procurement exceeding $250,000 requires approval from the Executive Procurement Committee, which meets bi-weekly. Committee submissions must include a business case, vendor evaluation report, financial analysis, and contract terms review by the Legal department.

Emergency procurements that cannot follow standard procedures due to operational urgency may be approved by any vice president with post-authorization documentation submitted within five business days. Emergency authorizations are limited to $100,000 and are subject to quarterly audit review.

## Q1. What was the previous spending limit for department managers?
- A. $5,000
- B. $2,500
- C. $10,000
- D. $50,000
- Answer: A
- Explanation: The new limit is $10,000, "increased from the previous limit of $5,000."

## Q2. How many competitive quotes are required for purchases between $10,000 and $50,000?
- A. One
- B. Two
- C. Three
- D. Four
- Answer: B
- Explanation: "Purchases in this range must include a minimum of two competitive quotes," reduced from the previous three.

## Q3. What can be inferred about the motivation behind the changes?
- A. The company is trying to reduce total spending by restricting approvals.
- B. A procurement fraud incident required complete overhaul of all policies.
- C. The previous framework caused unnecessary delays for routine purchases.
- D. Government regulations mandated the specific authorization levels.
- Answer: C
- Explanation: The changes aim to "accelerate routine procurement" and the increased manager limit addresses items that "have been unnecessarily delayed by multi-level approval requirements."

## Q4. What is the maximum amount an emergency procurement can cover?
- A. $10,000
- B. $100,000
- C. $50,000
- D. $250,000
- Answer: B
- Explanation: "Emergency authorizations are limited to $100,000."
