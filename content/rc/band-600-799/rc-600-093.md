# Cybersecurity Incident Response Plan Update

To: IT Staff and Department Managers
From: Alan Cooper, Chief Information Security Officer
Date: September 14

In response to the increasing frequency of cybersecurity threats targeting organizations in our sector, we have revised the company's incident response plan. The updated plan, which takes effect immediately, introduces new procedures for identifying, containing, and reporting security incidents. All IT staff and department managers must familiarize themselves with the changes outlined below.

The most critical update is the establishment of a tiered classification system for security incidents. Tier 1 incidents, such as phishing attempts and minor malware detections, should be reported to the IT security team within four hours and can typically be resolved through standard procedures. Tier 2 incidents, including unauthorized access to internal systems or significant data breaches, must be escalated to the Chief Information Security Officer within one hour and reported to affected stakeholders within 24 hours.

Additionally, all department managers are now required to designate a cybersecurity liaison within their team. This individual will serve as the primary point of contact between the department and the IT security team during an incident. Liaisons will receive specialized training during the fourth quarter, covering threat identification, initial containment measures, and communication protocols.

The IT security team will conduct quarterly tabletop exercises simulating various attack scenarios to test the effectiveness of the updated plan. The first exercise is scheduled for October 22. Participation from all designated liaisons is mandatory.
