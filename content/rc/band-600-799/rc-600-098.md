# Corporate Training Budgets Shift Toward Digital Platforms

According to a recent survey by the Workforce Development Institute, 73 percent of large companies now allocate more than half of their training budgets to digital learning platforms, up from 41 percent just three years ago. The shift reflects a growing recognition that digital training offers significant advantages in terms of scalability, consistency, and cost efficiency.

The survey, which included responses from 500 companies with more than 1,000 employees each, found that the average annual training expenditure per employee has risen to $1,850, a 15 percent increase from the previous year. Companies in the technology and financial services sectors reported the highest per-employee spending, averaging $2,400 and $2,200 respectively.

Despite the shift toward digital delivery, the survey revealed that blended learning approaches, which combine online modules with in-person workshops, remain the most effective format. Eighty-one percent of training managers rated blended learning as "highly effective" compared to 64 percent for purely digital programs and 55 percent for purely in-person sessions.

The report also identified emerging priorities for corporate training programs. Data literacy, artificial intelligence fundamentals, and change management were cited as the top three skills areas expected to receive increased investment over the next two years. Communication and leadership development, while still important, have plateaued in terms of budget allocation.
