# Environmental Compliance Report

Annual Environmental Compliance and Sustainability Report — Fiscal Year 2025

Atlas Industrial Group — Regulatory Affairs Division

Atlas Industrial Group maintained full regulatory compliance across all 14 manufacturing facilities during fiscal year 2025, with zero significant environmental violations for the third consecutive year. This report summarizes our environmental performance, highlights key achievements, and outlines commitments for the coming year.

Air emissions across all facilities were reduced by 16 percent year-over-year, driven by the completion of the catalytic converter upgrade program at our three largest plants. Total greenhouse gas emissions decreased from 284,000 metric tons of CO2 equivalent in FY2024 to 247,000 metric tons in FY2025, a 13 percent reduction. Water consumption per unit of production decreased by 9 percent through the deployment of closed-loop cooling systems at five facilities, resulting in the conservation of approximately 120 million gallons of water.

Waste management performance showed continued improvement. The company's overall waste diversion rate — the percentage of waste diverted from landfills through recycling, composting, and energy recovery — increased from 74 percent to 81 percent. Hazardous waste generation decreased by 11 percent following the reformulation of three chemical processes at the Riverside and Millbrook facilities.

Capital expenditures on environmental projects totaled $28 million in FY2025, representing 5.6 percent of total capital spending. The largest single investment was the $12 million wastewater treatment plant upgrade at the Clearwater facility, which now produces effluent quality that exceeds regulatory requirements by 40 percent.

For FY2026, we have committed to the following targets: a further 10 percent reduction in greenhouse gas emissions, achievement of a 90 percent waste diversion rate, and completion of ISO 14001 certification for the four facilities that have not yet achieved this standard. The estimated capital requirement for these initiatives is $32 million.
