# HR Analytics Report: Employee Retention and Engagement Trends

Prepared by: People Analytics Team, Thornfield Corporation
Reporting Period: January 2025 — December 2025

This report examines workforce retention and engagement metrics across Thornfield Corporation's eight operating divisions, encompassing approximately 14,000 employees globally. Data was collected through quarterly engagement surveys, exit interview analyses, and human resource information system records.

Overall voluntary turnover for 2025 was 13.8 percent, a reduction from 16.2 percent in 2024. The improvement is attributed primarily to the comprehensive benefits restructuring implemented in Q2 2025, which introduced flexible work arrangements, enhanced parental leave, and a student loan repayment assistance program. Divisions that fully adopted all three initiatives experienced turnover rates averaging 10.4 percent, while those that implemented only one or two measures saw rates of 14.9 percent.

Employee engagement scores, measured on a five-point scale, rose from 3.4 to 3.8 during the reporting period. The most significant drivers of improved engagement were manager effectiveness, career development opportunities, and organizational transparency regarding strategic decisions. Notably, employees who participated in the company's mentorship program reported engagement scores of 4.2, compared to 3.5 for non-participants.

However, the report identifies areas requiring attention. The Technology and Engineering division recorded the highest turnover at 18.1 percent, largely attributable to aggressive external recruitment by competing firms offering substantially higher compensation packages. Additionally, engagement scores among employees with five to ten years of tenure declined by 0.3 points, suggesting a mid-career disengagement pattern that warrants further investigation.

The People Analytics Team recommends targeted retention bonuses for high-risk technology roles, expanded mid-career development programs, and the introduction of internal mobility pathways to address these specific challenges.

## Q1. What was the primary cause of the improvement in voluntary turnover?
- A. A company-wide salary increase of 15 percent
- B. The restructuring of employee benefits including flexible work and enhanced leave
- C. The hiring of additional managers across all divisions
- D. A reduction in the total number of employees
- Answer: B
- Explanation: The improvement is attributed to "the comprehensive benefits restructuring implemented in Q2 2025, which introduced flexible work arrangements, enhanced parental leave, and a student loan repayment assistance program."

## Q2. What does the data suggest about the mentorship program's impact?
- A. It had no measurable effect on employee satisfaction.
- B. Participants reported notably higher engagement than non-participants.
- C. It was only available to employees in the Technology division.
- D. It increased turnover among senior employees.
- Answer: B
- Explanation: Mentorship program participants reported engagement scores of 4.2 compared to 3.5 for non-participants, indicating a notably positive impact.

## Q3. What can be inferred about divisions that only partially adopted the new benefits?
- A. They experienced better retention than fully adopting divisions.
- B. They were unable to implement any changes due to budget constraints.
- C. Their retention improvements were less pronounced than those of fully adopting divisions.
- D. They received additional funding to compensate for lower adoption rates.
- Answer: C
- Explanation: Divisions implementing only one or two measures had turnover of 14.9% versus 10.4% for full adopters, showing partial adoption yielded less improvement.

## Q4. Why does the report recommend targeted retention bonuses for technology roles?
- A. Technology employees are the least productive in the company.
- B. The Technology division faces high turnover due to competitors offering better pay.
- C. Technology staff have expressed dissatisfaction with the mentorship program.
- D. Retention bonuses are required by new labor regulations.
- Answer: B
- Explanation: The Technology and Engineering division had the highest turnover at 18.1%, "largely attributable to aggressive external recruitment by competing firms offering substantially higher compensation packages."
