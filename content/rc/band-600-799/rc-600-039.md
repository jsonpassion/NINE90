# Proposal to Establish a Customer Experience Analytics Department

This proposal recommends the creation of a dedicated Customer Experience Analytics department within Cornerstone Financial Services. The department would consolidate existing customer data analysis functions currently dispersed across the marketing, operations, and technology divisions into a single, strategically focused unit.

At present, our organization collects customer interaction data from 14 different channels, including branch visits, mobile application usage, call center contacts, website activity, and social media engagements. However, this data is analyzed in isolation by separate teams within different departments, resulting in a fragmented understanding of the overall customer experience. Customer satisfaction scores have stagnated at 73 percent for three consecutive quarters despite significant investments in individual channel improvements.

The proposed department would employ a team of 12 data analysts and 3 senior strategists under the leadership of a Vice President of Customer Experience, reporting directly to the Chief Operating Officer. The team's primary mission would be to integrate data from all customer touchpoints to create comprehensive journey maps, identify friction points across channels, and develop data-driven recommendations for experience improvements.

Expected outcomes include a unified view of customer behavior across all channels, faster identification and resolution of service issues, and more targeted resource allocation for improvement initiatives. Based on industry benchmarks from comparable financial services firms that have implemented similar functions, we project a 12 to 15 percentage point improvement in customer satisfaction within 24 months and a 20 percent reduction in customer attrition.

The estimated annual operating cost for the department is $3.2 million, including salaries, technology tools, and overhead. Given that a one percentage point improvement in customer retention is estimated to generate $4.5 million in annual revenue, the investment is expected to yield a substantial positive return.

## Q1. What is the main problem the proposal aims to solve?
- A. The company has too few customer interaction channels.
- B. Customer data is analyzed in isolation across departments, creating a fragmented view.
- C. The technology division is overstaffed and needs to reduce headcount.
- D. Customer satisfaction scores have never been measured before.
- Answer: B
- Explanation: The proposal states data "is analyzed in isolation by separate teams within different departments, resulting in a fragmented understanding of the overall customer experience."

## Q2. How long have customer satisfaction scores been stagnant?
- A. One quarter
- B. Two quarters
- C. Three consecutive quarters
- D. Twelve months
- Answer: C
- Explanation: "Customer satisfaction scores have stagnated at 73 percent for three consecutive quarters."

## Q3. What can be inferred about the projected return on investment?
- A. The department will not generate any measurable financial benefit.
- B. The return depends entirely on reducing staff in other departments.
- C. The investment will only break even after five years.
- D. The financial return is expected to significantly exceed the department's operating cost.
- Answer: D
- Explanation: The department costs $3.2 million annually, while each percentage point of retention improvement generates $4.5 million. With a projected 12-15 point satisfaction improvement and 20 percent retention reduction, the return substantially exceeds the cost.

## Q4. To whom would the new Vice President of Customer Experience report?
- A. The Chief Marketing Officer
- B. The Chief Technology Officer
- C. The Chief Financial Officer
- D. The Chief Operating Officer
- Answer: D
- Explanation: The proposal states the VP would be "reporting directly to the Chief Operating Officer."
