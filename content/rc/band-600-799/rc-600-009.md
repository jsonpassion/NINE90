# Annual Sustainability Report: Calder Manufacturing Group

Calder Manufacturing Group is pleased to present its 2025 Annual Sustainability Report, which documents the company's progress toward its environmental, social, and governance objectives. This report has been prepared in accordance with the Global Reporting Initiative Standards and has been independently verified by Prescott Assurance Partners.

In the area of carbon emissions, the company achieved a 22 percent reduction in Scope 1 and Scope 2 emissions compared to 2023 baseline levels. This was accomplished primarily through the conversion of three manufacturing plants to renewable energy sources and the installation of high-efficiency thermal recovery systems. The company remains on track to meet its target of a 40 percent reduction by 2030.

Water stewardship continued to be a priority, with total water consumption declining by 15 percent across all facilities. The implementation of closed-loop cooling systems at the Birmingham and Osaka plants accounted for the majority of these savings. Additionally, zero-liquid-discharge technology was piloted at the Hamburg facility, with plans for broader adoption pending successful evaluation.

On the social front, Calder expanded its workforce development programs, investing $8.5 million in employee training and upskilling initiatives. The company also increased the representation of women in senior management positions from 24 percent to 31 percent, surpassing its interim target of 28 percent. Community engagement spending rose to $3.2 million, funding educational partnerships and vocational training in regions where the company operates.

Governance improvements included the establishment of an independent sustainability oversight committee and the adoption of enhanced supply chain due diligence protocols to address human rights risks.
