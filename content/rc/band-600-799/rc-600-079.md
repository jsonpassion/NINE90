# Digital Marketing Performance Report

Digital Marketing Performance Report — Q1 2026

Prepared by the Marketing Analytics Division for the Chief Marketing Officer

This report provides a comprehensive analysis of digital marketing performance for the first quarter of 2026, benchmarked against the same period in 2025 and evaluated against annual targets established in the marketing plan.

Total digital marketing spend in Q1 was $3.8 million, representing 62 percent of the total marketing budget, up from 54 percent in Q1 2025. This increased allocation reflects the continued shift from traditional channels based on superior measurability and return on investment. Overall digital channel revenue attribution for the quarter was $31.2 million, yielding a blended return on ad spend of 8.2x, compared to 7.1x in the prior year.

Performance varied significantly across channels. Paid search remained the largest contributor at $12.4 million in attributed revenue on spend of $1.3 million, producing a return of 9.5x. Social media advertising generated $8.7 million in revenue on $1.1 million in spend, a return of 7.9x, with video content on short-form platforms driving a 45 percent increase in engagement year-over-year. Email marketing continued to deliver the highest efficiency, producing $6.8 million in revenue at a cost of only $180,000, though its growth potential is constrained by list size limitations.

The weakest performer was display advertising, which generated $3.3 million in revenue on $1.2 million in spend for a return of 2.8x. This underperformance is attributed to increasing ad-blocker adoption, which now affects an estimated 38 percent of our target audience, and declining click-through rates on standard banner formats. We recommend reallocating 40 percent of the display budget to programmatic native advertising, which has demonstrated 3x higher engagement rates in our testing.

Customer acquisition cost decreased by 14 percent year-over-year to $42 per new customer, driven primarily by improved targeting algorithms and the growing contribution of organic search traffic, which increased 28 percent following the website redesign launched in January.

## Q1. What was the overall return on ad spend for Q1 2026?
- A. 2.8x
- B. 7.1x
- C. 8.2x
- D. 9.5x
- Answer: C
- Explanation: The report states 'a blended return on ad spend of 8.2x.'

## Q2. Which channel delivered the highest efficiency?

- B. Social media
- C. Email marketing
- D. Display advertising
- Answer: C
- Explanation: Email marketing produced '$6.8 million in revenue at a cost of only $180,000,' the highest return ratio.
