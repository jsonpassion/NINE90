# Digital Banking Adoption Accelerates Among Older Consumers

A demographic shift in digital banking usage is challenging long-held assumptions about technology adoption among older adults. According to research published by the Financial Services Consumer Institute, 61 percent of consumers aged 65 and older now regularly use mobile banking applications, a dramatic increase from 23 percent in 2020.

The study, which surveyed 4,500 banking customers across all age groups, found that the pandemic period served as a catalyst for initial adoption, but continued usage has been sustained by genuine preference rather than necessity. Among older digital banking users, 74 percent reported that they now prefer mobile and online channels over visiting physical branches for routine transactions such as checking balances, transferring funds, and paying bills.

Fidelity Community Bank, which has a customer base with a median age of 58, invested $6.8 million in redesigning its mobile application specifically to address the needs of older users. Changes included larger text and button sizes, simplified navigation with fewer menu levels, and a prominent one-touch connection to live customer support. The bank also established a network of 120 volunteer digital coaches — existing customers who assist peers in learning to use the mobile platform.

The redesigned application was launched in April 2025, and early adoption metrics have exceeded expectations. Active mobile users among customers over 65 increased by 42 percent in the first five months, while call center volume for routine inquiries decreased by 18 percent. Customer satisfaction scores for the mobile application improved from 3.2 to 4.1 on a five-point scale.

Industry analysts note that financial institutions that fail to accommodate older digital users risk losing market share to competitors that offer more accessible digital experiences. With the over-65 demographic controlling an estimated 53 percent of total household wealth in the United States, the economic incentive for inclusive digital design is substantial.
