# Internal Audit Report: Procurement Process Review

Audit Reference: IA-2026-017
Prepared by: Internal Audit Division, Orion Manufacturing Corp.
Period Under Review: July 2025 — December 2025
Rating: Requires Improvement

The Internal Audit Division conducted a comprehensive review of procurement processes across Orion Manufacturing Corp.'s four operating regions. The audit evaluated compliance with the company's Procurement Policy Manual, effectiveness of internal controls, and adherence to competitive bidding requirements for contracts exceeding $50,000.

The audit identified several areas of concern. Of the 286 contracts reviewed, 41 contracts totaling $8.7 million were awarded without the requisite three competitive bids, representing a non-compliance rate of 14.3 percent. While sole-source justification documentation was provided for 18 of these contracts, the remaining 23 lacked adequate documentation to support the waiver of competitive bidding procedures. The Asia-Pacific region accounted for the highest concentration of exceptions, with 15 of the 23 undocumented cases originating from that region.

Additionally, the audit revealed that vendor performance evaluations were completed for only 52 percent of active contracts, despite the policy requirement of 100 percent completion. This gap limits the organization's ability to hold suppliers accountable for delivery timelines, quality standards, and pricing commitments. The audit team also identified $1.2 million in potential cost savings that could have been realized through consolidated purchasing agreements for commonly procured materials across regions.

The following corrective actions are recommended: first, mandatory procurement training for all regional purchasing managers, to be completed within sixty days; second, implementation of an automated workflow system that prevents contract approval without verified competitive bids or approved sole-source justification; third, quarterly vendor performance reviews with standardized evaluation criteria; and fourth, establishment of a centralized procurement analytics function to identify consolidation and cost-saving opportunities.

Management has accepted all recommendations and committed to an implementation timeline concluding by September 30, 2026.

- Explanation: The audit found 41 contracts awarded without three competitive bids, and 23 of those lacked adequate sole-source justification documentation, making this the central compliance finding.
