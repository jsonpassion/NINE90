# Mergers and Acquisitions Analysis

Post-Acquisition Integration Assessment — Synergy Realization Update

Prepared by the Corporate Development Office for the Board of Directors

This report provides an 18-month assessment of the integration of Cascade Analytics, which was acquired in September 2024 for $280 million. The acquisition was predicated on three strategic rationales: expanding our data analytics capabilities, accessing Cascade's client base of 340 enterprise customers, and achieving $38 million in annual cost synergies within 24 months.

Revenue synergies have exceeded initial projections. Cross-selling Cascade's analytics products to our existing customer base generated $22 million in incremental revenue during the first 18 months, compared to the pre-acquisition forecast of $15 million. The stronger-than-anticipated cross-selling performance reflects the high degree of product complementarity and the effectiveness of joint sales teams established within the first quarter post-acquisition. Client retention among Cascade's pre-existing customer base stands at 94 percent, exceeding the 90 percent target.

Cost synergy realization has been mixed. The elimination of redundant corporate functions, including overlapping finance, human resources, and legal teams, delivered $18 million in annualized savings, on track with projections. However, technology platform consolidation has encountered significant delays. The planned migration of Cascade's products to our cloud infrastructure, originally scheduled for completion in March 2026, has been pushed to September 2026 due to unexpected architectural incompatibilities. As a result, the company is currently maintaining two parallel technology environments at an incremental cost of $4.2 million per year.

Cultural integration presents an ongoing challenge. Employee engagement survey scores among former Cascade employees have declined by 15 points since the acquisition, and voluntary turnover among Cascade's technical staff has reached 18 percent, nearly double the pre-acquisition rate. Exit interviews indicate that concerns about reduced autonomy and changes to the product development methodology are the primary drivers.

## Q1. How did revenue synergies compare to projections?
- A. They fell significantly short of projections.
- B. They met projections exactly.
- C. They exceeded projections by $7 million.
- D. They exceeded projections by $22 million.
- Answer: C
- Explanation: Cross-selling generated $22 million versus the forecasted $15 million, exceeding projections by $7 million.

## Q2. What caused the delay in technology platform consolidation?
- A. Budget constraints
- B. Regulatory approval requirements
- C. Unexpected architectural incompatibilities
- D. Resistance from the sales team
- Answer: C
- Explanation: The delay was 'due to unexpected architectural incompatibilities' between the platforms.
