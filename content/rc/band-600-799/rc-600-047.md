# Proposal for Digital Marketing Strategy Overhaul

This proposal recommends a comprehensive overhaul of Belmont Health Products' digital marketing strategy. Our current approach, which relies heavily on paid search advertising and email campaigns, has yielded diminishing returns over the past 18 months. Customer acquisition costs have increased by 38 percent while conversion rates have declined by 15 percent, indicating that our current channels are approaching saturation.

We propose a three-phase transformation. Phase one focuses on content marketing. We recommend developing an authoritative health and wellness content platform featuring articles, videos, and interactive tools written and reviewed by licensed health professionals. Research indicates that brands investing in educational content experience 45 percent higher customer trust scores and 30 percent lower acquisition costs compared with brands relying primarily on direct advertising.

Phase two involves building a social media community strategy. Rather than using social platforms solely for promotional messaging, we propose establishing dedicated community spaces where customers can share experiences, ask questions, and receive guidance from our product specialists. This approach has proven effective for comparable health and wellness brands, with community-engaged customers demonstrating 2.5 times higher lifetime purchase value.

Phase three addresses data analytics and personalization. We recommend implementing an advanced customer data platform that integrates purchase history, website behavior, and engagement data to deliver personalized product recommendations and content. Companies that have implemented similar personalization capabilities report conversion rate improvements of 20 to 35 percent.

The total investment for the three-phase program is estimated at $1.8 million over 18 months, with phase one requiring $600,000, phase two requiring $480,000, and phase three requiring $720,000. We project that the program will reduce customer acquisition costs by 25 percent and increase average customer lifetime value by 40 percent within two years of full implementation.

## Q1. What problem does this proposal aim to address?
- A. A complete absence of any digital marketing activities
- B. Rising customer acquisition costs and declining conversion rates
- C. The company's decision to exit the health products market
- D. A data breach that compromised customer marketing databases
- Answer: B
- Explanation: The proposal identifies "Customer acquisition costs have increased by 38 percent while conversion rates have declined by 15 percent."

## Q2. Which phase requires the largest financial investment?
- A. Phase one — Content marketing
- B. Phase two — Social media community
- C. All three phases require equal investment
- D. Phase three — Data analytics and personalization
- Answer: D
- Explanation: Phase three requires $720,000, compared with $600,000 for phase one and $480,000 for phase two.
