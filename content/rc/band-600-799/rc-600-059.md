# Proposal for Comprehensive Employee Wellness Program

This proposal recommends that Stratton Industries implement a comprehensive employee wellness program to address rising healthcare costs and declining workforce health indicators. Analysis of our health insurance claims data reveals that healthcare expenditures per employee have increased 28 percent over the past three years, significantly outpacing the industry average increase of 18 percent.

Contributing factors include a 35 percent increase in claims related to musculoskeletal disorders, a 22 percent rise in stress-related conditions, and a 15 percent increase in metabolic health issues such as diabetes and hypertension. These trends correlate with employee survey data showing that 47 percent of staff report high levels of work-related stress and 62 percent describe their daily physical activity level as insufficient.

The proposed wellness program comprises four components. First, an on-site fitness facility at our headquarters campus, equipped with cardio and strength training equipment, group exercise spaces, and shower facilities. The estimated construction and equipment cost is $1.8 million, with annual operating costs of $240,000. Second, a mental health support program providing all employees with access to 12 confidential counseling sessions per year through a network of licensed therapists, at an estimated annual cost of $480,000.

Third, a preventive health screening program offering annual comprehensive health assessments at no cost to employees, including biometric screenings, cardiovascular risk evaluations, and nutrition consultations. The estimated annual cost is $320,000. Fourth, a wellness incentive program that rewards employees who participate in health-promoting activities with reduced insurance premiums and additional paid time off.

Industry research indicates that well-designed corporate wellness programs generate an average return of $3.27 for every dollar invested, primarily through reduced healthcare costs and decreased absenteeism. Based on our current expenditure levels, we project annual savings of $2.8 million within three years of full program implementation.

## Q1. What is the primary driver behind the wellness program proposal?
- A. A government mandate requiring all companies to offer wellness programs
- B. A desire to reduce the company's overall headcount
- C. Employee demands for a company gym as a recruitment incentive
- D. Rising healthcare costs that exceed industry averages and declining workforce health
- Answer: D
- Explanation: Healthcare costs "increased 28 percent over the past three years, significantly outpacing the industry average increase of 18 percent."

## Q2. Which component of the proposed program is the most expensive to operate annually?
- A. The on-site fitness facility
- B. The wellness incentive program
- C. The preventive health screening program
- D. The mental health support program
- Answer: D
- Explanation: The mental health program costs $480,000 annually, compared with $240,000 for the fitness facility and $320,000 for health screenings.

## Q3. What can be inferred about the link between employee health and company costs?
- A. Employee health has no measurable impact on company finances.
- B. Only employees who exercise regularly generate insurance claims.
- C. Poor employee health directly increases healthcare expenditures and likely reduces productivity.
- D. The company's insurance provider is responsible for all health-related costs.
- Answer: C
- Explanation: The data shows increased claims from musculoskeletal, stress, and metabolic conditions correlating with self-reported insufficient activity and high stress, driving 28 percent cost increases.

- A. $1.50 for every dollar invested
