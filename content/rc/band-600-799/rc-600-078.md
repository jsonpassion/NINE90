# Logistics Partnership Proposal

Proposal for Strategic Logistics Partnership — Last-Mile Delivery Optimization

Submitted by SwiftRoute Logistics to Greenleaf Consumer Products

SwiftRoute Logistics is pleased to present this proposal for a strategic partnership to manage last-mile delivery operations for Greenleaf Consumer Products across the Northeast region. Based on our analysis of Greenleaf's current delivery network, we believe this partnership can reduce last-mile delivery costs by 22 to 28 percent while improving delivery speed and customer satisfaction.

Greenleaf's current last-mile operations rely on three separate regional carriers, resulting in inconsistent service quality, fragmented tracking systems, and limited delivery window flexibility. Our analysis of 12 months of delivery data provided by Greenleaf identified the following issues: an on-time delivery rate of 84 percent against an industry benchmark of 93 percent, an average delivery window of six hours compared to the increasingly standard two-hour window, and a customer complaint rate of 3.2 percent, which is nearly double the industry average of 1.7 percent.

SwiftRoute proposes deploying our AI-optimized routing platform, which dynamically assigns delivery routes based on real-time traffic, weather, and package density data. Combined with our fleet of 450 vehicles and a network of 12 micro-distribution centers strategically located within a 15-mile radius of major population centers, we can offer guaranteed two-hour delivery windows for 90 percent of Greenleaf's Northeast customers.

The proposed partnership structure is a three-year agreement with performance-based pricing. The base rate per delivery is $8.50, compared to Greenleaf's current blended rate of $11.20 per delivery. Incentive bonuses of $0.25 per delivery apply when on-time rates exceed 95 percent, and penalties of $0.50 per delivery apply when rates fall below 90 percent. Based on Greenleaf's projected volume of 2.4 million deliveries per year, the estimated annual savings range from $5.3 million to $6.5 million.

We propose a 60-day pilot program covering two markets before full regional deployment, allowing both parties to validate performance assumptions and refine operational processes.
