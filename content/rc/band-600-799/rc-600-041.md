# Fleet Electrification Progress Report — Crestline Logistics

Crestline Logistics is pleased to report on the progress of its commercial fleet electrification program, now entering its second full year of implementation. The program aims to convert 40 percent of the company's 2,800-vehicle delivery fleet to electric vehicles by 2028 and achieve full electrification by 2032.

As of September 2025, 312 electric delivery vans and 48 electric medium-duty trucks have been deployed across 15 distribution centers in the eastern United States. These vehicles have collectively traveled 8.4 million miles since deployment, resulting in an estimated reduction of 6,200 metric tons of carbon dioxide emissions compared with the diesel vehicles they replaced.

Operational cost data has been favorable. Electric vehicles in the fleet are averaging $0.04 per mile in energy costs, compared with $0.28 per mile for equivalent diesel vehicles. Maintenance costs are also lower, averaging $0.06 per mile for electric vehicles versus $0.14 per mile for diesel. However, the higher upfront purchase price of electric vehicles — approximately $45,000 more per unit than comparable diesel models — means that total cost of ownership parity is not achieved until approximately the fourth year of service.

Charging infrastructure development has been the most significant operational challenge. Installation of Level 3 fast-charging stations at distribution centers has been delayed at four locations due to electrical grid capacity limitations that required utility upgrades costing an additional $2.3 million beyond the original budget. We are working with regional utility providers to develop pre-assessment protocols that will prevent similar delays at future sites.

Driver feedback has been largely positive. In a survey of 280 drivers who have operated electric vehicles, 82 percent reported satisfaction with vehicle performance, though 35 percent expressed concern about range limitations during winter months when battery efficiency decreases.

## Q1. What is Crestline's fleet electrification target for 2028?
- A. Full electrification of all 2,800 vehicles
- B. Deployment of 312 electric delivery vans only
- C. Conversion of 40 percent of the fleet to electric vehicles
- D. Replacement of all diesel medium-duty trucks
- Answer: C
- Explanation: The program "aims to convert 40 percent of the company's 2,800-vehicle delivery fleet to electric vehicles by 2028."

## Q2. What is the most significant challenge in the electrification program?
- A. Charging infrastructure delays due to electrical grid limitations
- B. Insufficient range for all delivery routes
- C. Driver resistance to operating electric vehicles
- D. The lack of available electric vehicle models for commercial use
- Answer: A
- Explanation: The report identifies "Charging infrastructure development" as "the most significant operational challenge," specifically grid capacity limitations causing delays.
