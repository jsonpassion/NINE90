# Annual Diversity and Inclusion Progress Report

Sterling Global Partners is committed to building a workforce that reflects the diversity of the communities and clients we serve. This report summarizes our progress toward the diversity and inclusion goals established in 2023 and outlines priorities for the coming year.

Overall workforce diversity continued to improve in 2025. Representation of women in management positions increased from 34 percent to 38 percent, advancing toward our 2027 target of 42 percent. At the senior leadership level, women now hold 28 percent of vice president and above positions, up from 24 percent in 2024. The appointment of three women to director-level roles in the technology and finance divisions contributed meaningfully to this progress.

Ethnic and racial diversity metrics also showed gains. Underrepresented groups now constitute 31 percent of total employees, compared with 27 percent two years ago. Our targeted recruitment partnerships with 15 universities that serve diverse student populations generated 180 summer intern placements in 2025, of which 72 percent received and accepted full-time employment offers upon graduation.

Employee resource groups continued to serve as important platforms for community building and professional development. Participation in ERGs grew by 24 percent, reaching 1,400 active members across eight groups. Feedback surveys indicate that ERG members report 15 percent higher engagement scores than non-members, suggesting these groups contribute positively to overall workplace satisfaction.

Areas requiring additional focus include representation in technical roles, where diversity metrics have shown slower progress, and geographic diversity in our international offices. The London and Singapore offices currently lag behind our domestic locations in meeting inclusion benchmarks. For 2026, we will invest $2.4 million in expanded recruitment programs, unconscious bias training for all hiring managers, and the development of a formal sponsorship program pairing senior leaders with high-potential employees from underrepresented backgrounds.

## Q1. What progress has been made in women's representation in management?
- A. It increased from 34 percent to 38 percent, moving toward a 42 percent target.
- B. It decreased from 38 percent to 34 percent.
- C. It reached the 2027 target of 42 percent ahead of schedule.
- D. No change was recorded from the previous year.
- Answer: A
- Explanation: "Representation of women in management positions increased from 34 percent to 38 percent, advancing toward our 2027 target of 42 percent."

## Q2. What is the conversion rate from intern to full-time employee?
- A. 15 percent
- B. 24 percent
- C. 31 percent
- D. 72 percent
- Answer: D
- Explanation: Of 180 intern placements, "72 percent received and accepted full-time employment offers."

## Q3. What can be inferred about the effectiveness of employee resource groups?
- A. They are primarily social clubs with no measurable business impact.
- B. Only senior leaders are permitted to participate.
- C. They are being discontinued due to declining participation.
- D. They positively influence employee engagement and satisfaction.
- Answer: D
- Explanation: ERG members report "15 percent higher engagement scores than non-members," and participation grew 24 percent, indicating positive impact.

## Q4. Which areas still need improvement according to the report?
- A. Overall workforce diversity and management representation
- B. Technical role diversity and international office inclusion benchmarks
- C. Summer internship programs and university partnerships
- D. Employee resource group participation and engagement surveys
- Answer: B
- Explanation: The report identifies "representation in technical roles" and "geographic diversity in our international offices" (London and Singapore) as areas requiring additional focus.
