# Urban Development Proposal

Proposal for the Redevelopment of the Waterfront District — Executive Summary

Submitted by Pinnacle Urban Partners to the City of Lakewood Planning Commission

Pinnacle Urban Partners respectfully submits this proposal for the comprehensive redevelopment of the 28-acre Waterfront District, which has been designated for revitalization under the city's 2025 Master Plan. Our proposed development, named Harbor Point, represents a $420 million mixed-use investment designed to transform the currently underutilized industrial waterfront into a vibrant live-work-play destination.

The development plan encompasses four phases spanning seven years. Phase 1, scheduled for completion within 24 months, includes 320 residential units in two mid-rise buildings, 45,000 square feet of ground-floor retail space, and a public waterfront promenade extending 1.2 miles along the harbor. Phase 2 adds a 180-room boutique hotel and a 60,000-square-foot conference center. Phase 3 delivers 150,000 square feet of Class A office space across two buildings, targeting technology and creative industry tenants. Phase 4 completes the project with an additional 200 residential units, a community health center, and a 3-acre public park.

We project that Harbor Point will generate approximately 2,800 permanent jobs upon full completion, contribute an estimated $18 million annually in property tax revenue, and attract over 500,000 visitors per year to the waterfront area. Our financial model assumes a public-private partnership structure in which the city provides infrastructure improvements — including road access, utility connections, and a new transit stop — valued at approximately $55 million, with the remaining $365 million funded through private equity and construction financing.

Pinnacle brings 22 years of experience in urban mixed-use developments, having successfully completed projects totaling over $3 billion in six metropolitan areas. We are committed to incorporating sustainable design standards, with a target of LEED Gold certification for all commercial buildings and a minimum of 15 percent affordable housing units within the residential components.
