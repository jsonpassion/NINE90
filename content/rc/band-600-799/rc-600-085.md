# Corporate Credit Card Policy Revision

To: All Cardholders
From: Steven Park, Controller
Date: August 8

The Finance Department is implementing several important changes to the corporate credit card policy, effective September 1. These revisions are intended to strengthen internal controls, improve expense tracking accuracy, and ensure compliance with updated audit requirements.

First, the single-transaction spending limit for all cardholders has been raised from $2,000 to $3,000 to accommodate inflation-adjusted costs for common business expenses such as travel and client entertainment. However, any individual transaction exceeding $1,000 will now require a brief written justification to be entered in the expense tracking system within five business days.

Second, personal use of corporate credit cards is strictly prohibited under all circumstances. Previous policy versions allowed incidental personal charges with the expectation that they would be reimbursed promptly. This exception has been eliminated. Any personal charges discovered during monthly audits will be deducted from the cardholder's next paycheck without prior notice.

Third, receipts for all transactions, regardless of amount, must now be uploaded digitally through the expense management app within 72 hours of the purchase. Paper receipt submissions will no longer be accepted. Cardholders who fail to submit receipts within the required timeframe for three or more transactions in a single quarter may have their card privileges suspended.

A mandatory briefing for all current cardholders will be held on August 25. Department administrators will receive the updated policy manual by August 15.
