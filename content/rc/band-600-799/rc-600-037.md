# Quality Assurance Audit Report — Harmon Pharmaceutical Manufacturing

This report presents the findings of the annual quality assurance audit conducted at Harmon Pharmaceutical's Sterling Heights manufacturing facility from August 12 to August 23, 2025. The audit was performed in accordance with current Good Manufacturing Practice regulations and covered all production lines, quality control laboratories, and documentation systems.

Overall, the facility demonstrated a satisfactory level of compliance with applicable regulatory standards. Of the 186 audit criteria evaluated, 168 received satisfactory ratings, 14 were classified as requiring minor corrective actions, and 4 were identified as requiring major corrective actions. This represents an improvement over the previous year's audit, which identified 7 major findings.

The four major findings are summarized below. First, temperature monitoring records for Warehouse B showed gaps during three separate periods in June and July, totaling 11 days of incomplete data. Regulations require continuous temperature monitoring for all storage areas containing temperature-sensitive pharmaceutical products. Second, deviation investigation reports for two production batches did not include root cause analysis documentation, as required by the facility's standard operating procedures.

Third, calibration records for four analytical instruments in the microbiology laboratory were overdue by periods ranging from 12 to 28 days. All affected instruments have since been recalibrated and returned to service. Fourth, cleaning validation protocols for the tablet compression line had not been updated to reflect equipment modifications made in April 2025.

Corrective action plans for all major findings must be submitted to the Quality Assurance department within 30 days of this report's issuance. Implementation of corrective actions will be verified through targeted follow-up audits scheduled for November 2025. Failure to address major findings within the specified timeframe may result in a referral to the facility's regulatory affairs team for further evaluation.
