# Green Building Certification Drives Premium Rents in Commercial Market

A new study by the National Commercial Real Estate Association found that office buildings with green certification command rental premiums of 12 to 18 percent compared to conventional buildings in the same markets. The study analyzed lease data from over 3,000 commercial properties across 25 metropolitan areas over a five-year period.

Buildings that achieved the highest level of environmental certification showed the greatest rental advantages, with tenants willing to pay an average of $6.50 more per square foot annually. Researchers attributed the premium to several factors, including lower utility costs for tenants, improved indoor air quality, and the growing importance of corporate sustainability commitments in tenant decision-making.

The study also found that green-certified buildings experienced significantly lower vacancy rates, averaging 8 percent compared to 14 percent for non-certified properties. Tenant retention rates were higher as well, with an average lease renewal rate of 72 percent versus 58 percent for conventional buildings.

However, the report cautioned that the initial cost of achieving green certification can be substantial, typically adding 5 to 10 percent to construction costs. For existing buildings, retrofit costs vary widely depending on the building's age and condition. Despite these upfront expenses, the researchers concluded that green certification generally pays for itself within four to six years through higher rents and reduced vacancies.
