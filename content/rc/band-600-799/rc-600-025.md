# Compliance Memo: Updated Data Privacy Requirements

TO: All Department Heads and Data Custodians
FROM: Office of the Chief Compliance Officer
DATE: November 1, 2025
RE: Implementation of Enhanced Data Privacy Standards

This memorandum outlines mandatory changes to data handling procedures required under the recently enacted Consumer Data Protection Act (CDPA), which takes effect on January 15, 2026. All business units must achieve full compliance by that date to avoid potential penalties of up to $500,000 per violation.

The CDPA introduces three principal requirements that affect our operations. First, all customer personal data must be classified according to a three-tier sensitivity framework: Standard (name, address, phone), Sensitive (financial records, health information), and Restricted (government identification numbers, biometric data). Each tier carries specific storage, access, and retention obligations.

Second, the Act requires that customers be provided with a clear and accessible mechanism to request the deletion of their personal data within 30 business days of such a request. Our current systems do not support automated data deletion workflows, and the Information Technology department has been authorized to procure and implement the necessary software tools at an estimated cost of $380,000.

Third, any transfer of personal data to third-party service providers must be governed by updated contractual provisions that explicitly require the receiving party to maintain equivalent data protection standards. The Legal department is currently revising all relevant vendor agreements to incorporate these provisions, with a target completion date of December 15.

To support these changes, mandatory training sessions will be conducted for all employees who handle customer data. The first session is scheduled for November 15, with additional sessions available through December. Attendance will be tracked and reported to senior management. Employees who do not complete the training by January 1 will have their data system access suspended until the requirement is fulfilled.
