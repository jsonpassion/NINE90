# Response to Client Complaint Regarding Service Delivery

Dear Ms. Fontaine,

Thank you for your letter dated October 3, in which you described your experience with our facilities management services at the Lakeview Business Park complex. I want to assure you that we take your concerns very seriously and have conducted a thorough review of the matters you raised.

After investigating, I can confirm that the heating system maintenance scheduled for September was indeed delayed by two weeks due to a parts shortage from our primary equipment supplier. This delay was unacceptable, and I acknowledge that our team failed to communicate the revised timeline to your property management office in a timely manner. We have since implemented a notification protocol requiring our field supervisors to inform clients within 24 hours of any schedule changes.

Regarding the landscaping concerns, our site inspection confirmed that the grounds maintenance during August and September fell below the standards specified in our service agreement. The crew assigned to your property was temporarily reduced from four members to two during that period because of staffing shortages. We have now restored the full crew complement and conducted a comprehensive grounds restoration, which was completed on October 10.

As a gesture of goodwill and in recognition of the service shortfalls you experienced, we are applying a credit of $4,800 to your account, representing 15 percent of the quarterly service fee. This credit will appear on your November invoice.

I have also arranged for our Regional Operations Director, Mark Sullivan, to serve as your dedicated point of contact going forward. Mr. Sullivan can be reached directly at extension 2240. We value our partnership with Lakeview Business Park and are committed to restoring your confidence in our services.

Sincerely,
Diana Hargrove
Vice President of Client Relations, Pinnacle Facilities Group

## Q1. Why was the heating system maintenance delayed?
- A. The client cancelled the appointment.
- B. The maintenance crew was reassigned to another property.
- C. A parts shortage from the equipment supplier caused the delay.
- D. Severe weather conditions prevented the work from being performed.
- Answer: C
- Explanation: The letter states the maintenance "was delayed by two weeks due to a parts shortage from our primary equipment supplier."

## Q2. What can be inferred about Pinnacle Facilities Group's response to the complaint?
- A. The company dismissed the concerns as unfounded.
- B. The company acknowledged the failures and took concrete corrective actions.
- C. The company terminated the service agreement immediately.
- D. The company blamed the client for the service problems.
- Answer: B
- Explanation: The company acknowledged both the maintenance delay and landscaping shortfalls, implemented new protocols, restored staffing, provided a financial credit, and assigned a dedicated contact — all concrete corrective actions.

## Q3. What was the cause of the landscaping quality decline?
- A. Unusually harsh weather damaged the equipment.
- B. The client requested a reduced service level.
- C. A new landscaping contractor was hired mid-contract.
- D. The grounds crew was temporarily reduced from four to two members.
- Answer: D
- Explanation: The letter explains that "the crew assigned to your property was temporarily reduced from four members to two during that period because of staffing shortages."

## Q4. How much is the account credit being offered?
- A. 15 percent of the annual service fee
- B. $2,400 applied over two months
- C. $4,800, representing 15 percent of the quarterly fee
- D. A full refund of the September service charge
- Answer: C
- Explanation: The letter states "a credit of $4,800 to your account, representing 15 percent of the quarterly service fee."
