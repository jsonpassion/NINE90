# Letter Regarding Commercial Lease Modification Request

Dear Mr. Ramirez,

I am writing on behalf of Westbrook Design Associates to request a modification to the terms of our commercial lease for Suite 400 at Broadmoor Office Tower. Our current lease, executed on March 1, 2022, runs through February 28, 2027, with a monthly rent of $18,500.

Since the lease was signed, our firm has grown from 28 to 46 employees, and we are experiencing significant space constraints. Suite 400 comprises 4,200 square feet, and our current staff density exceeds recommended workspace guidelines, which is affecting productivity and employee satisfaction.

We are aware that Suite 410, the adjacent unit of approximately 2,100 square feet, will become available on February 1, 2026, when the current tenant's lease expires. We would like to propose the following arrangement: expansion of our leased premises to include both Suite 400 and Suite 410, for a combined area of approximately 6,300 square feet; a new five-year lease term commencing February 1, 2026, replacing the remaining term of the existing lease; and a negotiated rent reflecting a blended rate for the combined space.

Given the current market conditions and the strength of our tenancy — we have maintained an impeccable payment record over the past three and a half years and have invested approximately $85,000 in improvements to our current space — we believe a rate of $27 per square foot annually for the combined premises is fair and competitive.

We are also open to discussing a longer lease term of seven years in exchange for a reduced rate or a contribution toward the build-out costs of Suite 410, which we estimate at $120,000. We see our long-term future at Broadmoor Office Tower and hope to reach terms that are mutually beneficial.

I welcome the opportunity to meet and discuss this proposal at your earliest convenience.

Sincerely,
Rachel Westbrook
Principal, Westbrook Design Associates

## Q1. Why is Westbrook Design requesting a lease modification?
- A. They have outgrown their current space due to employee growth from 28 to 46 staff.
- B. They want to reduce their space and lower monthly payments.
- C. The building is scheduled for demolition and they need to relocate.
- D. Their current suite has structural damage requiring immediate repair.
- Answer: A
- Explanation: The firm "has grown from 28 to 46 employees" and is "experiencing significant space constraints" in their 4,200 square foot space.

## Q2. What leverage does Westbrook cite in the negotiation?
- A. An impeccable payment record and significant tenant improvements
- B. A threat to file a complaint with the building management authority
- C. A competing offer from another building at a lower rate
- D. A legal obligation for the landlord to accommodate tenant growth
- Answer: A
- Explanation: Westbrook cites "an impeccable payment record over the past three and a half years" and "$85,000 in improvements to our current space."

## Q3. What can be inferred about Westbrook's negotiation strategy?
- A. They are offering flexibility and multiple options to reach a mutually beneficial agreement.
- B. They are issuing an ultimatum with no room for discussion.
- C. They plan to terminate the lease if their exact terms are not met.
- D. They have already signed a lease at a competing building.
- Answer: A
- Explanation: Westbrook proposes specific terms but also states openness to "a longer lease term of seven years in exchange for a reduced rate or a contribution toward build-out costs."

## Q4. What is the estimated cost to build out Suite 410?
- A. $18,500
- B. $27,000
- C. $120,000
- D. $85,000
- Answer: C
- Explanation: The letter states "build-out costs of Suite 410, which we estimate at $120,000."
