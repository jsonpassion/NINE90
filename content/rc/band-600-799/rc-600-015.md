# Budget Proposal: Fiscal Year 2027 Marketing Operations

Submitted to: Executive Budget Committee
Prepared by: Victoria Chen, Vice President of Marketing
Date: March 12, 2026

The Marketing Operations division requests a total budget allocation of $18.5 million for fiscal year 2027, representing a 12 percent increase over the current year's approved budget of $16.5 million. This increase is necessitated by three strategic imperatives: the planned expansion into two new international markets, the modernization of our digital marketing technology stack, and the launch of three new product lines scheduled for Q2 and Q3.

The proposed allocation is structured as follows. Digital marketing channels, including search engine optimization, paid advertising, and social media management, would receive $7.2 million, reflecting a 20 percent increase to support aggressive customer acquisition targets in the new markets. Brand and creative services would be allocated $4.1 million to fund the development of localized campaigns for the European and Latin American markets. Events and trade shows would receive $2.8 million, a modest increase to accommodate participation in six additional industry conferences. The marketing technology upgrade, encompassing a new customer data platform and marketing automation suite, would require a one-time investment of $3.0 million. The remaining $1.4 million would cover personnel costs associated with hiring four additional team members: two regional marketing managers, one data analyst, and one content strategist.

Without this investment, the division will be unable to adequately support the international expansion, which is projected to generate $45 million in incremental revenue within three years. Furthermore, our current marketing technology infrastructure is approaching end-of-life, and failure to upgrade will result in diminished campaign efficiency and increased operational risk. We respectfully request the committee's approval by April 15 to ensure timely vendor procurement and recruitment processes.

## Q1. What is the total budget increase being requested compared to the current year?
- A. $1.5 million
- B. $2.0 million
- C. $3.0 million
- D. $4.5 million
- Answer: B
- Explanation: The current budget is $16.5 million and the requested budget is $18.5 million, a difference of $2.0 million (12 percent increase).

## Q2. Which budget category receives the largest allocation in the proposal?
- A. Brand and creative services
- B. Events and trade shows
- C. Digital marketing channels
- D. Marketing technology upgrade
- Answer: C
- Explanation: Digital marketing channels would receive $7.2 million, the largest single allocation among all categories listed.

## Q3. What can be inferred about the company's current marketing technology?
- A. It was recently installed and is functioning optimally.
- B. It is outdated and poses risks if not replaced.
- C. It was donated by a third-party technology partner.
- D. It only supports domestic marketing campaigns.
- Answer: B
- Explanation: The proposal states the "current marketing technology infrastructure is approaching end-of-life" and that failure to upgrade will cause "diminished campaign efficiency and increased operational risk."

## Q4. Why does the proposal emphasize the April 15 approval deadline?
- A. The fiscal year begins on April 15.
- B. International markets will close to new entrants after that date.
- C. Timely approval is needed to begin procurement and hiring on schedule.
- D. The executive committee disbands after April 15.
- Answer: C
- Explanation: The proposal requests approval by April 15 "to ensure timely vendor procurement and recruitment processes," indicating that delays would affect implementation schedules.
