# Project Status Report: Lakewood Mixed-Use Development

This monthly status report covers the Lakewood Mixed-Use Development project for the period ending September 30, 2025. The project comprises a 14-story residential tower with 220 units, 35,000 square feet of ground-floor retail space, and a 450-space underground parking structure. The total project budget is $142 million, with a scheduled completion date of August 2026.

Construction is currently 58 percent complete, which is two percentage points behind the original schedule. The delay is attributable to an unexpected geological condition encountered during foundation work in July. Excavation revealed an underground water channel that required installation of additional waterproofing systems and structural reinforcement, adding 18 days to the foundation phase and $1.4 million in unplanned costs.

Despite this setback, the structural steel framework has been erected through the ninth floor, and the project team has implemented an accelerated construction schedule for floors 10 through 14 that is expected to recover approximately 12 of the 18 lost days. The mechanical, electrical, and plumbing rough-in work is proceeding on the lower floors concurrently with the structural work above.

Budget performance remains within acceptable parameters. Total expenditures to date are $83.6 million against a planned $82.1 million, representing a variance of 1.8 percent. The $1.4 million foundation cost overrun has been partially offset by $900,000 in savings achieved through a renegotiated concrete supply contract. The project contingency reserve of $7.1 million remains available to address any additional unforeseen conditions.

Safety performance has been exemplary, with zero lost-time incidents recorded in over 180,000 worker-hours during the reporting period. This achievement reflects the effectiveness of the daily safety briefings and weekly hazard inspections that were instituted at project commencement.

## Q1. What caused the construction delay?
- A. A labor strike by construction workers
- B. An underground water channel discovered during foundation work
- C. Late delivery of structural steel components
- D. A change in the building design requested by the client
- Answer: B
- Explanation: "Excavation revealed an underground water channel that required installation of additional waterproofing systems and structural reinforcement."

## Q2. How much of the 18-day delay does the team expect to recover?
