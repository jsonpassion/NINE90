# Anti-Money Laundering Compliance Review Summary

This report summarizes the findings of the annual anti-money laundering compliance review conducted for Harrington National Bank. The review, performed by the internal audit department with support from external compliance consultants, covered all branch locations, commercial lending operations, and private banking services for the period January through September 2025.

The bank's AML compliance framework received an overall rating of "satisfactory with recommendations." The transaction monitoring system correctly flagged 94 percent of test scenarios designed to simulate suspicious activity patterns, representing a meaningful improvement from the 86 percent detection rate observed in the 2024 review. This improvement is attributed to the upgrade of the monitoring software to version 4.2, which incorporates enhanced pattern recognition algorithms.

However, the review identified several areas requiring attention. Customer due diligence files for 23 of the 200 commercial accounts sampled were found to be incomplete, missing either updated beneficial ownership information or current business verification documents. While the missing documentation does not indicate actual suspicious activity, it represents a procedural compliance gap that could attract regulatory criticism during an examination.

The review also found that suspicious activity report filing timelines exceeded the 30-day regulatory requirement in 8 of 62 cases examined, with average filing time of 38 days for those delayed reports. The primary cause was an insufficient number of trained analysts in the financial intelligence unit, which currently has 12 positions but only 9 are filled.

Recommendations include completing the documentation remediation for identified deficient accounts within 60 days, filling the three vacant analyst positions by year-end, and conducting refresher training for all relationship managers on customer due diligence requirements. The Chief Compliance Officer has acknowledged these findings and committed to an action plan addressing all recommendations.

## Q1. How did the transaction monitoring system's performance change from 2024 to 2025?
- A. It declined from 94 percent to 86 percent detection rate.
- B. It remained unchanged at 86 percent.
- C. It improved from 86 percent to 94 percent detection rate.
- D. The monitoring system was not tested in 2024.
- Answer: C
- Explanation: The system "correctly flagged 94 percent of test scenarios," improved from "the 86 percent detection rate observed in the 2024 review."

## Q2. What caused delays in suspicious activity report filings?
- A. The bank's software system crashed and lost filing data.
- B. Bank management deliberately delayed filings to protect clients.
- C. Insufficient staffing in the financial intelligence unit.
- D. The regulatory filing system was offline for maintenance.
- Answer: C
- Explanation: "The primary cause was an insufficient number of trained analysts in the financial intelligence unit, which currently has 12 positions but only 9 are filled."

## Q3. What can be inferred about the incomplete customer due diligence files?

- Answer: C
- Explanation: The missing documentation "does not indicate actual suspicious activity" but "represents a procedural compliance gap that could attract regulatory criticism."
