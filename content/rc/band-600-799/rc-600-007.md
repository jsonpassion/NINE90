# Re: Breach of Non-Disclosure Agreement — Case No. 2026-LT-4418

Dear Mr. Thornton,

This letter is written on behalf of our client, Avalon Pharmaceuticals Inc., regarding the unauthorized disclosure of proprietary research data that occurred on or about February 14, 2026. Our investigation has confirmed that confidential information pertaining to Avalon's Phase III clinical trial for compound AV-7720 was shared with an external party in violation of the Non-Disclosure Agreement executed on September 3, 2024.

Specifically, our forensic analysis of electronic communications revealed that detailed efficacy data and projected regulatory timelines were transmitted to a representative of Nextera BioSciences via an unsecured personal email account. This information constitutes trade secrets under applicable state and federal law, and its unauthorized disclosure has caused significant competitive harm to our client.

Pursuant to Section 8.2 of the NDA, Avalon Pharmaceuticals is entitled to seek injunctive relief and monetary damages for any breach. We have been instructed to demand the following: first, immediate cessation of any further dissemination of Avalon's confidential information; second, return or certified destruction of all materials containing proprietary data within fourteen business days; and third, a written confirmation of compliance addressed to our offices.

Should these demands not be satisfied by March 31, 2026, Avalon Pharmaceuticals reserves the right to pursue all available legal remedies, including but not limited to filing for a temporary restraining order and initiating litigation for compensatory and punitive damages. We strongly advise you to consult with legal counsel regarding this matter.

We remain open to resolving this dispute through mediation, provided that good-faith efforts are demonstrated promptly.

Sincerely,
Rebecca Langford, Esq.
Senior Partner, Langford & Associates LLP

- Explanation: The letter informs Mr. Thornton of the confirmed NDA breach and lists specific demands for compliance, making notification and corrective action its primary purpose.
