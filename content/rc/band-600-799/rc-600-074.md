# Workplace Redesign Proposal

Proposal for Activity-Based Workplace Redesign — Corporate Headquarters

Submitted by the Workplace Strategy Task Force

The Workplace Strategy Task Force recommends a comprehensive redesign of the corporate headquarters from a traditional assigned-desk model to an activity-based working environment. This proposal responds to utilization data showing that 42 percent of assigned workstations are unoccupied on any given day, a figure that has increased from 28 percent following the implementation of our hybrid work policy in 2024.

The activity-based design philosophy replaces individual assigned desks with a variety of purpose-built work settings. The proposed layout includes 120 focus pods for individual concentrated work, 80 collaboration zones with writable walls and video conferencing equipment, 15 project rooms for extended team work, 40 quiet library-style seats for reading and analysis, and a rejuvenation area with comfortable seating and natural elements for informal conversations and mental breaks. A smart booking system will allow employees to reserve spaces via a mobile app, while walk-in availability will be displayed on screens at each floor's entrance.

The total redesign cost is estimated at $4.2 million, which includes furniture procurement, technology infrastructure, acoustic treatments, and architectural modifications. We project annual savings of $1.8 million through the consolidation of two leased floors that will no longer be needed, reducing the company's office footprint from six floors to four while accommodating the same workforce. The payback period is therefore approximately 2.3 years.

Employee surveys indicate that 67 percent of staff support the transition, 22 percent are neutral, and 11 percent oppose it. The primary concerns among opponents relate to the loss of personal space and storage. To address these concerns, we propose providing each employee with a personal locker and a guaranteed minimum of two reserved days per week in their preferred work zone during the six-month transition period.
