# Cybersecurity Assessment Report

Enterprise Cybersecurity Posture Assessment — Executive Summary

Prepared by CyberGuard Solutions for Westbrook Financial Group

This executive summary presents the findings of the comprehensive cybersecurity assessment conducted for Westbrook Financial Group between January 15 and February 28, 2026. The assessment evaluated network infrastructure, application security, access management controls, and incident response capabilities across all three operational facilities.

The assessment identified a total of 142 security findings, categorized as follows: 8 critical, 27 high, 56 medium, and 51 low-severity items. Of particular concern are three findings that represent immediate risk to the organization. First, the email gateway system is running firmware that is 14 months past its end-of-support date, leaving it without security patches for 23 known vulnerabilities. Second, privileged access to the core banking system is shared among 12 administrators using a common credential rather than individual accounts, preventing meaningful audit tracking. Third, the disaster recovery environment has not been tested since August 2024, and our analysis indicates that the documented recovery procedures contain configuration references that no longer match the production environment.

On a positive note, the assessment confirmed that Westbrook's endpoint protection platform is current and effectively managed, with a 98 percent deployment rate across all corporate devices. Network segmentation between customer-facing systems and internal operations is properly implemented, and the security operations center demonstrated competent handling of simulated threat scenarios during the assessment period.

We recommend immediate remediation of the three critical findings within 30 days, followed by a structured 90-day program to address high-severity items. The estimated cost for the complete remediation program, including hardware upgrades, software licensing, and professional services, is $1.85 million. We further recommend conducting a follow-up assessment in six months to verify that remediation actions have been effectively implemented.
