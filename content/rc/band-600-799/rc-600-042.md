# Memo: Workplace Ergonomics Assessment Findings and Recommendations

TO: All Department Managers
FROM: Environmental Health and Safety Division
DATE: September 8, 2025
RE: Results of Comprehensive Workplace Ergonomics Assessment

The Environmental Health and Safety Division recently completed a comprehensive ergonomics assessment of all office facilities operated by Pinnacle Financial Group. The assessment, conducted by certified ergonomics specialists from Carter Workplace Solutions, evaluated 840 individual workstations across our four office locations over a six-week period.

The assessment identified ergonomic risk factors at 62 percent of evaluated workstations. The most common issues were improper monitor height and distance (found at 48 percent of workstations), chairs with insufficient lumbar support or incorrect height adjustment (41 percent), and keyboard and mouse positioning that encourages repetitive strain (29 percent). These findings correlate with our workers' compensation data, which shows a 23 percent increase in musculoskeletal disorder claims over the past two years.

Immediate corrective actions have already been initiated for the 84 workstations classified as high risk, defined as those with three or more concurrent ergonomic deficiencies. Adjustable monitor arms, ergonomic keyboards, and replacement chairs have been ordered for these workstations, with delivery expected within three weeks.

For the remaining workstations with identified issues, we recommend a phased remediation program. Phase one, covering workstations with two deficiencies, would be completed by December 2025 at an estimated cost of $185,000. Phase two, addressing single-deficiency workstations, would be completed by March 2026 at an estimated cost of $120,000.

Additionally, we strongly recommend implementing a mandatory ergonomics awareness training program for all employees. Research indicates that even properly configured workstations can contribute to injury if employees do not understand correct posture and work habits. Training sessions of 90 minutes are proposed for all staff, with annual refresher modules thereafter.

## Q1. What percentage of workstations had ergonomic risk factors?
- A. 29 percent
- B. 41 percent
- C. 62 percent
- D. 48 percent
- Answer: C
- Explanation: "The assessment identified ergonomic risk factors at 62 percent of evaluated workstations."

- B. A workstation older than five years

- D. A workstation used by an employee with a prior injury claim
- Answer: A
- Explanation: High risk is "defined as those with three or more concurrent ergonomic deficiencies."
