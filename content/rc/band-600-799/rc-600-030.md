# Proposal for Regional Office Relocation

This proposal recommends relocating the Southeast Regional Office of Whitfield Consulting Group from its current location at 1450 Commerce Boulevard, Atlanta, to a new facility at Peachtree Innovation Center, also in Atlanta. The move is proposed for completion by March 2026.

The current office space, which has been occupied since 2014, presents several operational limitations. The building's aging HVAC system requires increasingly frequent repairs, with maintenance costs having risen 40 percent over the past three years. The available floor area of 18,000 square feet can no longer accommodate the regional staff of 145 employees comfortably, resulting in overcrowded meeting rooms and insufficient individual workspace.

Additionally, the building lacks modern technological infrastructure. Retrofitting the existing space with fiber optic connectivity, enhanced power capacity for a server room, and updated conference room video systems would cost an estimated $620,000 according to our facilities engineering assessment.

The proposed new location at Peachtree Innovation Center offers 26,000 square feet of recently renovated office space with modern building systems already in place, including high-speed fiber connectivity, energy-efficient climate control, and fully equipped conference facilities. The annual lease cost of $780,000 represents a 15 percent premium over the current lease; however, this increase would be substantially offset by the elimination of $180,000 in annual maintenance and repair expenditures and the avoidance of the $620,000 retrofit investment.

The new location is also better positioned for employee recruitment and client accessibility. It is situated adjacent to a major transit hub, reducing average employee commute times by an estimated 20 minutes, and is within walking distance of the city's financial and legal districts.

We recommend that the executive committee authorize the relocation and approve a moving budget of $290,000 to cover furniture procurement, technology migration, and temporary operational disruptions during the transition.

## Q1. What is the main reason for proposing the office relocation?
- A. The current facility has operational limitations that are costly to address.
- B. The company is downsizing its Southeast regional operations.
- C. A government mandate requires all businesses to relocate to newer buildings.
- D. The current lease has been terminated by the building owner.
- Answer: A
- Explanation: The proposal cites aging HVAC, insufficient space for 145 employees, and lack of modern infrastructure, with costly retrofit requirements.

## Q2. How would the higher lease cost at the new location be offset?
- A. By eliminating current maintenance costs and avoiding retrofit expenses
- B. By receiving a government subsidy for the relocation
- C. By reducing the number of employees in the regional office
- D. By subletting unused portions of the new office space
- Answer: A
- Explanation: The 15 percent lease premium would be "substantially offset by the elimination of $180,000 in annual maintenance and repair expenditures and the avoidance of the $620,000 retrofit investment."

## Q3. What can be inferred about the current office's effect on employee recruitment?
- A. The location actively drives candidates away from the company.
- B. The current location is less attractive to potential employees than the proposed site.
- C. Employee recruitment is not affected by office location.
- D. The company has stopped hiring for the regional office.
- Answer: B
- Explanation: The proposal highlights the new location as "better positioned for employee recruitment," with transit access and reduced commute times, implying the current location is less attractive.

## Q4. How much has the current building's HVAC maintenance cost increased?
- A. 15 percent over the past year
- B. 40 percent over the past three years
- C. 20 percent since 2014
- D. $620,000 in total
- Answer: B
- Explanation: The proposal states "maintenance costs having risen 40 percent over the past three years."
