# Quarterly Earnings Report Summary

Meridian Technologies — Third Quarter Earnings Summary

Meridian Technologies reported third-quarter revenue of $2.18 billion, representing an 11 percent year-over-year increase and exceeding analyst consensus estimates by approximately $90 million. The growth was primarily driven by strong demand in the company's cloud infrastructure division, which posted revenue of $840 million, a 24 percent increase from the same period last year. The enterprise software segment generated $720 million in revenue, up 8 percent, while the legacy hardware division continued its anticipated decline, falling 6 percent to $620 million.

Gross margin expanded by 180 basis points to 42.3 percent, reflecting a favorable shift in revenue mix toward higher-margin cloud services and the benefits of ongoing cost optimization in manufacturing operations. Operating expenses rose 7 percent to $610 million, driven largely by increased investment in research and development, which now accounts for 14 percent of total revenue. Net income for the quarter was $328 million, or $2.45 per diluted share, compared to $271 million, or $2.03 per diluted share, in the year-ago quarter.

The company raised its full-year revenue guidance to a range of $8.5 billion to $8.7 billion, up from the prior range of $8.2 billion to $8.5 billion. Management attributed the upward revision to an accelerating pipeline of large enterprise deals and the successful launch of the CloudVault 3.0 platform in September, which has already secured commitments from 35 new enterprise customers.

Cash and short-term investments stood at $3.1 billion as of the quarter-end. The board of directors authorized an additional $500 million share repurchase program, bringing the total authorized buyback to $1.2 billion.

## Q1. Which division drove the strongest revenue growth?
