# Internal Memo: Updated Regulatory Compliance Requirements

To: All Department Heads and Regional Managers
From: Office of the Chief Compliance Officer
Date: March 10, 2026
Subject: Implementation of Revised Data Protection Regulations

This memorandum serves to inform all relevant personnel of the mandatory compliance actions required under the recently enacted Digital Privacy Enhancement Act (DPEA), which takes effect on June 1, 2026. The regulation introduces stricter requirements for the collection, storage, and processing of personal data, and non-compliance may result in penalties of up to 4 percent of annual global revenue.

Effective immediately, the following measures must be implemented across all business units. First, all customer-facing applications must incorporate explicit consent mechanisms that meet the DPEA's "informed and unambiguous" standard. Legacy systems that rely on pre-checked consent boxes or implied consent through continued use will no longer satisfy regulatory requirements. Second, data retention periods must be reviewed and, where necessary, shortened to align with the principle of data minimization. Any personal data that is no longer essential for the purpose for which it was collected must be securely deleted within ninety days.

Third, each department is required to designate a Data Protection Liaison who will serve as the primary point of contact for compliance inquiries and incident reporting. These liaisons must complete the certification program administered by the Legal and Compliance division no later than May 15, 2026. Fourth, all third-party data processors must be re-evaluated against the DPEA's enhanced due diligence criteria. Contracts with vendors that do not meet the new standards must be renegotiated or terminated before the enforcement date.

The compliance team will conduct readiness assessments beginning April 1. Department heads are expected to submit preliminary compliance action plans by March 28.
