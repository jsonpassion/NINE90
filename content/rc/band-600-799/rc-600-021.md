# Quarterly Sustainability Progress Report

Meridian Consumer Products is pleased to release its sustainability progress report for the third quarter of 2025. This document provides stakeholders with an update on our environmental initiatives, workforce development programs, and community engagement activities.

During the quarter, we achieved a 12 percent reduction in carbon emissions across our manufacturing facilities compared with the same period last year. This improvement was primarily driven by the installation of solar panel arrays at our Henderson and Oakville plants, which now generate approximately 35 percent of those facilities' electricity requirements. Additionally, our transition to recycled packaging materials reached 68 percent completion, exceeding the year-end target of 60 percent.

Water conservation efforts also showed meaningful progress. Our Memphis production site implemented a closed-loop water recycling system that reduced freshwater consumption by 1.8 million gallons per quarter. The system, which required a capital investment of $4.2 million, is expected to achieve full payback within three years through reduced utility costs and wastewater treatment fees.

In workforce development, we expanded our technician apprenticeship program to three additional sites, bringing total enrollment to 340 participants. The program has demonstrated strong retention outcomes, with 92 percent of graduates remaining with the company after two years. We also launched a leadership development initiative for mid-level managers, with 85 participants completing the first cohort.

Community engagement expenditures totaled $1.3 million for the quarter, directed primarily toward educational partnerships with local school districts near our manufacturing facilities and funding for vocational training programs.
