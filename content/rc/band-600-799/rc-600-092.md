# Telemedicine Adoption Rates Remain High Post-Pandemic

A comprehensive survey conducted by the National Health Services Research Institute reveals that telemedicine usage among adults has stabilized at approximately 35 percent, far exceeding the pre-pandemic level of 8 percent. The survey, which polled 12,000 adults across the country, provides valuable insights into how patients and healthcare providers are navigating the long-term integration of virtual care.

Among the key findings, patients aged 25 to 44 were the most frequent users of telemedicine services, with 48 percent reporting at least one virtual consultation in the past year. Patients over 65, while initially slower to adopt the technology, have seen a notable increase in usage, rising from 12 percent to 29 percent over the past two years. Convenience and reduced travel time were cited as the top reasons for choosing telemedicine across all age groups.

Healthcare providers expressed mixed sentiments about the sustained adoption. While 76 percent of surveyed physicians acknowledged that telemedicine improved access to care for patients in rural and underserved areas, 62 percent raised concerns about diagnostic limitations when physical examinations cannot be performed. Dermatology, psychiatry, and general follow-up consultations were identified as the specialties most suited to virtual delivery.

The report recommends that healthcare systems invest in hybrid care models that combine in-person and virtual visits, training programs to improve providers' telemedicine skills, and upgraded digital infrastructure to ensure equitable access across different demographic groups.
