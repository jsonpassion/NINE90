# Annual Employee Engagement Survey Results Summary

The Human Resources department is pleased to present the results of the 2025 Annual Employee Engagement Survey, conducted during September across all divisions of Thornton Enterprises. A total of 3,840 employees participated, representing a response rate of 82 percent, which is consistent with prior years and exceeds the industry benchmark of 70 percent.

Overall engagement scores improved modestly, rising to 71 percent from 68 percent in 2024. The strongest improvement was observed in the area of professional development opportunities, where satisfaction increased by 8 percentage points to 74 percent. This gain likely reflects the launch of the company's tuition reimbursement program in January 2025, which has enrolled 420 employees to date.

Communication from senior leadership also showed improvement, with 66 percent of respondents reporting satisfaction, up from 59 percent in 2024. This increase is attributed to the introduction of monthly town hall meetings and the CEO's weekly video updates, both initiatives that were recommended in last year's survey action plan.

However, several areas require continued attention. Compensation satisfaction remained the lowest-rated category at 52 percent, declining two points from the prior year. Exit interview data supports this finding, with 38 percent of departing employees citing compensation as a primary factor in their decision to leave. Work-life balance scores were also relatively low at 58 percent, with employees in the operations division reporting the most significant concerns.

Based on these findings, the HR department recommends three priority actions for 2026: conducting a comprehensive market compensation study to identify and address salary gaps, expanding flexible work arrangements to additional departments, and creating a dedicated well-being program focused on workload management and stress reduction.
