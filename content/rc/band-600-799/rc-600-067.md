# Talent Acquisition Strategy Report

Strategic Talent Acquisition Report — Technology Division

Prepared by the Talent Strategy Team, Alder Consulting Group

The technology division's talent acquisition challenges have intensified over the past 18 months, with the average time-to-fill for critical engineering positions increasing from 45 days to 72 days and offer acceptance rates declining from 82 percent to 68 percent. This report analyzes the underlying causes and proposes a multi-faceted response strategy.

Market analysis reveals that competition for qualified software engineers, data scientists, and cybersecurity professionals has reached unprecedented levels. The regional talent pool has contracted by approximately 15 percent as remote work opportunities have enabled professionals to accept positions with companies headquartered in higher-compensation markets. Simultaneously, the technology division's base salary offerings for senior engineers fall approximately 12 percent below the current market median, a gap that has widened from 5 percent two years ago.

Exit interview analysis of the 47 voluntary departures over the past year provides additional insight. Compensation was cited as the primary reason for leaving by 42 percent of departing employees. However, 31 percent cited limited career advancement opportunities, and 18 percent pointed to dissatisfaction with the company's technology stack, which they described as outdated relative to industry standards.

We recommend the following integrated strategy. First, adjust base salaries for critical technical roles to within 5 percent of market median, requiring an estimated annual investment of $2.4 million. Second, introduce a retention bonus program offering 15 percent of annual salary after three years of continuous service for positions designated as high-attrition. Third, establish a technology modernization fund of $800,000 annually to enable teams to adopt contemporary frameworks and tools. Fourth, create a transparent career progression framework with clearly defined technical and managerial advancement tracks, including quarterly development conversations between managers and their direct reports.
