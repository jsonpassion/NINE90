# Memorandum: Fiscal Year 2026 Budget Planning Guidelines

TO: All Division Presidents and Budget Managers
FROM: Office of the Chief Financial Officer
DATE: October 20, 2025
RE: Budget Submission Guidelines for Fiscal Year 2026

As we begin the annual budget planning cycle for fiscal year 2026, this memorandum outlines the key parameters, assumptions, and submission requirements that all divisions must follow. Completed budget packages are due to the Corporate Finance department no later than November 30, 2025.

Revenue Assumptions: All divisions should model revenue growth within a range of 3 to 6 percent over fiscal year 2025 actual results, consistent with our macroeconomic outlook and market position analysis. Divisions projecting growth rates outside this range must provide supporting documentation, including market research, competitive analysis, and identified risk factors.

Expense Management: Operating expense budgets should target a maximum increase of 4 percent over current-year actuals. Inflationary pressures on wages and materials are acknowledged; however, divisions are expected to identify offsetting efficiency improvements. Headcount additions beyond approved replacements require a business case approved by the division president and the Chief Human Resources Officer.

Capital Expenditures: The corporate capital allocation for FY2026 is set at $45 million, a 10 percent increase over the current year. Divisions must prioritize projects with the shortest payback periods and strongest strategic alignment. All capital requests exceeding $2 million require a detailed financial analysis, including net present value and internal rate of return calculations.

Contingency Planning: Each division must include a scenario analysis showing budget performance under both favorable and unfavorable conditions. The unfavorable scenario should model a 10 percent revenue shortfall and identify discretionary expenditures that could be reduced or deferred to maintain profitability targets.

Budget review sessions with the executive leadership team will be scheduled during the first two weeks of December.
