# Flexible Work Arrangements Become Standard in Professional Services

The professional services industry has undergone a permanent shift in workplace practices, with flexible work arrangements now established as a standard operating model rather than a temporary accommodation. A comprehensive survey by the Professional Services Workforce Institute found that 84 percent of firms in consulting, accounting, and legal services now offer hybrid work options, up from 31 percent before the pandemic.

The survey, which collected responses from 620 firms, revealed significant variation in implementation approaches. Approximately 45 percent of responding firms require employees to be in the office a minimum of three days per week, while 30 percent mandate only two days. The remaining 25 percent allow employees and their managers to determine office attendance schedules on a team-by-team basis.

Client expectations have played a central role in shaping these policies. Among firms that maintain higher in-office requirements, 72 percent cited client demand for in-person meetings and on-site collaboration as the primary driver. In contrast, firms with more flexible arrangements reported that their clients had largely adapted to virtual service delivery and did not require regular physical presence.

Employee recruitment and retention have also influenced policy decisions. The survey found that firms offering greater workplace flexibility experienced 18 percent lower voluntary turnover compared with firms requiring full-time office attendance. Additionally, 63 percent of firms reported that flexible work options had expanded their ability to recruit talent from a wider geographic area.

However, managing hybrid teams presents ongoing challenges. Forty percent of firm leaders expressed concern about maintaining organizational culture and junior employee development in a partially remote environment. Several firms have responded by creating structured mentoring programs and designated in-person collaboration days to address these concerns. The survey authors concluded that firms investing in intentional culture-building practices report higher satisfaction among both partners and junior staff.

## Q1. What percentage of professional services firms offered hybrid work before the pandemic?
- A. 25 percent
- B. 45 percent
- C. 31 percent
- D. 84 percent
- Answer: C
- Explanation: The article states flexible work options are now offered by 84 percent of firms, "up from 31 percent before the pandemic."

## Q2. What is the most common in-office attendance requirement?
- A. Full-time office attendance five days per week
- B. A minimum of three days per week in the office
- C. Two mandatory office days per week
- D. Fully remote with no office attendance required
- Answer: B
- Explanation: "Approximately 45 percent of responding firms require employees to be in the office a minimum of three days per week," making it the most common policy.

## Q3. What can be inferred about firms that resist flexible work arrangements?
- A. They are likely to experience advantages in recruiting talent from diverse locations.
- B. Their clients universally prefer virtual service delivery.
- C. They may face higher employee turnover compared to more flexible competitors.
- D. They have fully resolved all concerns about organizational culture.
- Answer: C
- Explanation: The survey found firms with greater flexibility experienced "18 percent lower voluntary turnover," implying less flexible firms face higher turnover.

## Q4. The phrase "intentional culture-building practices" most likely refers to:
- A. Accidental improvements in workplace atmosphere
- B. Deliberate, planned activities designed to strengthen organizational cohesion
- C. Government regulations requiring companies to promote diversity
- D. The natural social interactions that occur in an office environment
- Answer: B
- Explanation: "Intentional" indicates deliberate planning, and the context describes structured programs (mentoring, collaboration days) designed to maintain organizational culture.
