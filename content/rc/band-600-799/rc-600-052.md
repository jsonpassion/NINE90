# Customer Retention Analysis: Silverline Telecommunications

This report presents findings from the customer retention analysis conducted for Silverline Telecommunications' consumer wireless division. The analysis covers the 12-month period ending September 2025 and examines patterns in customer attrition, satisfaction drivers, and the effectiveness of retention initiatives.

During the analysis period, the consumer wireless division lost 142,000 subscribers, representing a monthly churn rate of 1.8 percent. This compares unfavorably with the industry average of 1.4 percent and represents a deterioration from Silverline's own historical rate of 1.5 percent. If sustained, the current churn rate would result in the loss of approximately $86 million in annual recurring revenue.

Exit survey analysis of departing customers identified three primary drivers of attrition. The most frequently cited reason was network coverage and reliability (mentioned by 42 percent of respondents), followed by pricing relative to competitors (33 percent), and customer service experience (25 percent). Notably, coverage complaints were concentrated in rural and suburban markets where Silverline's network density lags behind its two largest competitors.

The company's existing retention program, which offers discounted plans and device upgrades to customers who contact the cancellation department, has achieved a 28 percent save rate. However, this reactive approach engages customers only after they have already decided to leave. Analysis of customer behavior data shows that declining usage patterns and increased contact with customer service can predict churn risk 60 to 90 days before a customer initiates cancellation.

We recommend transitioning to a proactive retention model that identifies at-risk customers through predictive analytics and initiates personalized outreach before cancellation intent forms. Industry peers that have implemented similar models report save rates of 40 to 50 percent, which at Silverline's scale would prevent the loss of approximately 17,000 to 31,000 additional subscribers annually.

## Q1. How does Silverline's churn rate compare to the industry average?
- A. It is lower than the industry average, indicating strong performance.
- B. It matches the industry average of 1.4 percent exactly.
- C. It is higher than both the industry average and the company's historical rate.
- D. Industry data is not available for comparison.
- Answer: C
- Explanation: Silverline's 1.8 percent churn "compares unfavorably with the industry average of 1.4 percent" and is worse than its own historical 1.5 percent.

## Q2. What is the most common reason customers leave Silverline?
- A. Customer service experience
- B. Network coverage and reliability
- C. Pricing relative to competitors
- D. Lack of device options
- Answer: B
- Explanation: Network coverage was "the most frequently cited reason," mentioned by 42 percent of respondents.

## Q3. What can be inferred about the current retention program's limitations?
- A. It is too expensive to operate effectively.
- B. It has been discontinued due to poor results.
- C. It offers incentives that are too generous to be financially sustainable.
- D. It only engages customers after they have already decided to leave, missing earlier opportunities.
- Answer: D
- Explanation: The current program "engages customers only after they have already decided to leave," while data shows churn can be predicted 60-90 days earlier.
