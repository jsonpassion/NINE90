# Automotive Parts Manufacturer Invests in Robotic Assembly Line

Precision Auto Components, a leading manufacturer of braking systems and suspension parts, announced a $28 million investment to install a fully robotic assembly line at its primary production facility in Canton, Ohio. The project, which began in March and is expected to be completed by August, represents the company's largest single capital expenditure in its 40-year history.

The new assembly line will feature 24 industrial robots capable of performing tasks including welding, component fitting, quality inspection, and packaging. According to CEO Maria Delgado, the robotics initiative will increase production capacity by 35 percent while reducing the error rate to near zero. She emphasized that the investment was necessary to meet growing demand from major automotive manufacturers that have placed larger orders in anticipation of rising vehicle production volumes.

Concerns about potential job losses have been addressed by the company's commitment to a comprehensive workforce transition plan. Approximately 120 workers currently assigned to manual assembly tasks will be retrained for roles in robotics oversight, maintenance, and programming. The retraining program, developed in partnership with a local technical college, will take six months to complete and will be fully funded by the company.

Industry observers note that the investment reflects a broader trend among mid-sized manufacturers toward automation as a strategy for remaining competitive in an industry increasingly dominated by cost pressures and quality demands from global supply chains.
