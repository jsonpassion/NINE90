# Retail Chain Adopts AI-Powered Inventory Management

Sterling Department Stores, a national retail chain with 85 locations, announced last week that it has completed a company-wide rollout of an artificial intelligence-based inventory management system. The system, developed by logistics technology firm Apex Analytics, uses machine learning algorithms to predict consumer demand and automatically adjust stock levels across all store locations.

According to Sterling's Chief Operating Officer, David Kwon, early results from a pilot program conducted at 12 stores over the past six months have been highly promising. Stockout incidents, which occur when a product is unavailable despite customer demand, decreased by 28 percent. At the same time, overstock situations, where excess inventory ties up capital and storage space, were reduced by 22 percent.

The system analyzes a wide range of data points including historical sales patterns, seasonal trends, local demographic information, weather forecasts, and social media sentiment to generate demand predictions. These predictions are updated in real time and shared with both store managers and the central distribution team, enabling faster and more informed restocking decisions.

Kwon noted that the company expects to save approximately $14 million annually in reduced waste, lower storage costs, and improved sales from better product availability. The technology investment totaled $8.5 million, meaning the company anticipates a full return on investment within the first year.
