# Hotel Chain Launches Sustainable Tourism Program

Grandview Hotels International has introduced a new sustainability initiative called "Green Stay," which aims to reduce the environmental footprint of its 120 properties across 30 countries. The program, which officially launched last week, combines operational efficiency improvements with guest engagement strategies to promote environmentally responsible travel.

Key operational changes include the replacement of single-use plastic amenities with refillable dispensers in all guest rooms, the installation of energy-efficient LED lighting throughout all properties, and the implementation of a linen reuse program that allows guests to opt out of daily towel and sheet changes. Grandview estimates that these measures alone will reduce plastic waste by 65 percent and cut energy consumption by 20 percent annually.

On the guest engagement side, the Green Stay program offers loyalty program members bonus points for choosing sustainable options during their stay. Guests who decline daily housekeeping, use digital check-in instead of paper, or participate in the hotel's carbon offset program will earn double loyalty points. The carbon offset option adds $3 per night to the room rate, with proceeds directed to verified reforestation projects.

CEO Amanda Fischer noted that the initiative aligns with growing consumer demand for sustainable hospitality. "Our research shows that 67 percent of travelers consider a hotel's environmental practices when making booking decisions," she said. "Green Stay is not just the right thing to do—it's also smart business."
