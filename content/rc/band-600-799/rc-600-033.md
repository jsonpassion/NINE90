# Mid-Year Financial Review: Eastwood Consumer Brands

Eastwood Consumer Brands reports mixed financial results for the first half of 2025. Total revenue reached $1.42 billion, representing a 4 percent increase over the same period in 2024. While the top-line performance was in line with management expectations, profitability was adversely affected by several factors that are expected to moderate in the second half of the year.

Gross profit margin contracted by 180 basis points to 32.6 percent, primarily due to elevated raw material costs in the packaged foods division. Soybean and wheat prices rose 16 percent and 11 percent respectively during the period, adding approximately $48 million to production costs. The company's hedging program, which covers approximately 60 percent of commodity exposure, partially mitigated the impact but was insufficient to prevent margin compression entirely.

Operating expenses increased 7 percent to $312 million, driven by a $22 million investment in a new enterprise resource planning system and $15 million in costs associated with the integration of Marlow Natural Foods, which was acquired in December 2024. The ERP implementation is on track for completion in Q4 2025 and is expected to generate annual efficiency savings of $8 million beginning in 2026.

Segment performance diverged notably. The personal care division delivered revenue growth of 11 percent and maintained stable margins, benefiting from the successful launch of the Bloom skincare line, which generated $38 million in first-half sales. In contrast, the packaged foods division saw revenue growth of only 1 percent, constrained by competitive pricing pressure and the commodity cost headwinds described above.

Net income for the period was $89 million, down 12 percent from $101 million in the prior year, reflecting the combined impact of higher input costs and integration expenses.

## Q1. What was the primary cause of gross margin decline?
- A. A decrease in total revenue across all divisions
- B. Higher raw material costs in the packaged foods division
- C. The failure of the Bloom skincare product launch
- D. Excessive spending on advertising and marketing
- Answer: B
- Explanation: The report states gross margin contraction was "primarily due to elevated raw material costs in the packaged foods division," specifically soybean and wheat price increases.

## Q2. What can be inferred about the Bloom skincare line's contribution to overall performance?
- A. It has not yet been launched and revenue is projected for next year.
- B. It performed below expectations and contributed to the revenue decline.
- C. It was a significant positive factor that helped offset weakness in other areas.
- D. It was discontinued due to poor consumer reception.
- Answer: C
- Explanation: The personal care division grew 11 percent with stable margins, "benefiting from the successful launch of the Bloom skincare line" with $38 million in sales, helping offset the packaged foods division's weakness.

- B. Three years after implementation
- C. During the second half of 2025
- D. Beginning in 2026
- Answer: D
- Explanation: The report states the ERP system "is expected to generate annual efficiency savings of $8 million beginning in 2026."
