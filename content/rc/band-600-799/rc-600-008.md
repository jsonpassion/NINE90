# Project Proposal: Centralized Inventory Management System

Submitted by: Operations Strategy Division
Date: March 15, 2026

The Operations Strategy Division respectfully submits this proposal for the development and implementation of a Centralized Inventory Management System (CIMS) across all seven distribution centers operated by Grandview Logistics. The current decentralized approach, in which each facility maintains its own inventory tracking software, has resulted in persistent discrepancies, delayed order fulfillment, and an estimated $2.8 million in annual losses due to overstocking and stockouts.

The proposed CIMS would integrate real-time tracking, automated reorder triggers, and predictive demand analytics into a unified platform accessible by all facility managers. Phase one, spanning months one through four, would involve requirements gathering, vendor evaluation, and system architecture design. Phase two, covering months five through nine, would encompass software development, integration testing, and pilot deployment at the Atlanta and Denver facilities. Phase three, from months ten through fourteen, would extend the rollout to the remaining five locations with comprehensive staff training.

The total projected cost is $4.6 million, which includes software licensing, hardware upgrades, consulting fees, and training expenses. Based on conservative estimates, the system is expected to generate annual savings of $3.1 million through reduced waste, optimized stock levels, and improved order accuracy. Accordingly, the return on investment would be realized within approximately eighteen months of full deployment.

We recommend assembling a cross-functional steering committee comprising representatives from IT, operations, finance, and procurement to oversee the project. Executive sponsorship from the Chief Operating Officer is requested to ensure organizational alignment and resource prioritization.

## Q1. What is the main problem the proposal aims to solve?
- A. A shortage of qualified warehouse personnel
- B. Inconsistencies caused by separate inventory systems at each facility
- C. Declining revenue from international shipping operations
- D. Excessive spending on marketing for logistics services
- Answer: B
- Explanation: The proposal identifies the "decentralized approach, in which each facility maintains its own inventory tracking software" as causing discrepancies, delays, and financial losses.

## Q2. During which phase would the system first be tested in actual facilities?
- A. Phase one
- B. Phase two
- C. Phase three
- D. A pre-project assessment phase
- Answer: B
- Explanation: Phase two includes "pilot deployment at the Atlanta and Denver facilities," making it the first phase with real facility testing.

## Q3. What can be inferred about the expected financial outcome of the project?

- Answer: B
- Explanation: With a $4.6 million cost and $3.1 million in annual savings, the ROI would be achieved within approximately eighteen months, meaning savings exceed total investment well within two years.
