# Corporate Renewable Energy Procurement Reaches Record Levels

Corporate purchases of renewable energy reached unprecedented levels in 2025, with companies worldwide contracting for a combined 52 gigawatts of clean power capacity through long-term purchase agreements. This represents a 23 percent increase over the 2024 figure and more than triple the volume recorded in 2020.

The technology sector continued to dominate corporate renewable energy procurement, accounting for 38 percent of total contracted capacity. Data center operators, whose electricity consumption has surged due to the expansion of artificial intelligence computing workloads, were the largest single category of buyers. Nextera Analytics, a cloud computing firm, alone signed agreements for 4.2 gigawatts of solar and wind power across six states.

The manufacturing sector showed the fastest growth rate, with procurement volumes increasing 45 percent year-over-year. This acceleration reflects tightening emissions regulations in the European Union and mounting pressure from institutional investors for companies to demonstrate progress toward carbon neutrality commitments. Several major automakers signed their first renewable energy agreements during the year as they seek to decarbonize not only their vehicle fleets but also their manufacturing operations.

Despite the overall growth trend, market participants identified several challenges that could constrain future procurement. Transmission infrastructure has not expanded at the pace needed to connect new renewable generation projects to corporate buyers in many regions. Permitting timelines for large-scale solar and wind installations have lengthened in some jurisdictions due to increased environmental review requirements and local opposition.

Financing conditions also tightened during the year. Higher interest rates increased the cost of capital for project developers, leading to a 10 to 15 percent increase in the prices offered under new power purchase agreements compared with contracts signed in 2023.

- A. Technology
- B. Financial services

- D. Healthcare
- Answer: C

- A. Prices will continue to decline rapidly as technology improves.
- B. Higher interest rates are likely to keep prices elevated compared to earlier contracts.
- C. Government subsidies will eliminate all costs for corporate buyers.
- D. Prices will be unaffected by financing conditions.
- Answer: B
- Explanation: The article notes higher interest rates led to "a 10 to 15 percent increase in the prices offered under new power purchase agreements," suggesting elevated costs will persist.
