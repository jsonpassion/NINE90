# Notice of Service Agreement Non-Renewal

Dear Mr. Blackwell,

I am writing to formally notify you that Ashford & Associates will not be renewing our information technology support agreement with DataBridge Solutions upon its expiration on January 31, 2026. This decision was not made lightly, and I want to explain the factors that led to it, as well as outline the transition process we envision.

Over the past two years, we have experienced a pattern of service quality issues that, despite repeated discussions, have not been satisfactorily resolved. System response times during peak business hours have consistently exceeded the 200-millisecond threshold specified in our service level agreement, averaging 340 milliseconds during the third quarter of 2025. Additionally, there have been four unplanned service outages in the past six months, each lasting between two and five hours. Our agreement stipulates a maximum of two such outages per year.

We also have concerns about the timeliness of support ticket resolution. The agreed-upon resolution time for critical tickets is four hours; however, our records indicate that the average resolution time during the past quarter was 7.2 hours. This delay has directly impacted our ability to serve our own clients, resulting in at least three instances where we were unable to meet contractual deadlines.

To ensure an orderly transition, we request that DataBridge provide complete documentation of our system architecture, network configurations, and access credentials no later than December 15, 2025. We also require that your team cooperate fully with our incoming service provider during a 30-day parallel operations period beginning January 1, 2026.

We are prepared to fulfill all of our financial obligations under the current agreement through its expiration date. We wish DataBridge well and hope that the feedback provided in this letter is constructive.

Sincerely,
Margaret Harrington
Managing Partner, Ashford & Associates

## Q1. What is the primary reason for the non-renewal decision?
- A. Ashford & Associates is going out of business.
- B. Ashford decided to bring all IT support functions in-house.
- C. DataBridge Solutions raised its prices beyond what Ashford could afford.
- D. A persistent pattern of service quality issues that were not resolved.
- Answer: D
- Explanation: The letter cites "a pattern of service quality issues that, despite repeated discussions, have not been satisfactorily resolved."

## Q2. How do actual system response times compare to the agreed-upon standard?
- A. They meet the standard of 200 milliseconds consistently.
- B. The service agreement does not specify response time requirements.
- C. They are below the standard at 150 milliseconds.
- D. They exceed the standard by 70 percent, averaging 340 milliseconds.
- Answer: D
- Explanation: The SLA specifies 200 milliseconds, but actual times averaged 340 milliseconds — 70 percent above the threshold.

## Q3. What can be inferred about Ashford's approach to ending the relationship?
- A. Ashford is acting impulsively without any prior attempt to resolve issues.
- B. Ashford intends to withhold final payments as a penalty.
- C. Ashford is taking a professional, structured approach with clear transition requirements.
- D. Ashford expects DataBridge to find a replacement client immediately.
- Answer: C
- Explanation: The letter provides detailed transition requirements, offers to fulfill financial obligations, and provides constructive feedback — all indicating a professional approach.

## Q4. What is the purpose of the 30-day parallel operations period?
- A. To allow DataBridge to train new employees
- B. To enable a smooth transition between the outgoing and incoming service providers
- C. To give Ashford time to renegotiate better terms with DataBridge
- D. To conduct an audit of DataBridge's financial records
- Answer: B
- Explanation: The parallel operations period requires DataBridge to "cooperate fully with our incoming service provider," enabling a smooth handover.
