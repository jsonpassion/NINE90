# Memorandum: Revised Travel and Expense Policy

TO: All Employees
FROM: Chief Financial Officer
DATE: October 15, 2025
RE: Updates to Corporate Travel and Expense Reimbursement Policy — Effective November 1, 2025

Following a comprehensive review of travel spending patterns and industry benchmarking data, the finance department has revised the corporate travel and expense policy. These changes are designed to maintain appropriate cost controls while providing employees with reasonable flexibility when traveling on company business.

Airfare: Economy class remains the standard for all domestic flights. However, premium economy seating is now approved for domestic flights exceeding five hours and for all international flights. Business class travel requires advance written approval from a vice president or above and is generally limited to international flights exceeding eight hours. The previous policy required CEO approval for any premium cabin travel, which created unnecessary administrative burden and frequent processing delays.

Hotel Accommodations: Daily lodging allowances have been updated to reflect current market rates. The maximum nightly rate for major metropolitan areas (New York, San Francisco, Boston, Washington D.C., Los Angeles, Chicago) has been increased from $225 to $275. All other domestic locations retain the existing $175 cap. International lodging rates will follow the State Department per diem schedule published annually.

Meals: The daily meal allowance has been increased from $60 to $75 for domestic travel, reflecting inflation-adjusted costs. This allowance covers all meals and incidental expenses. Receipts are required for any individual meal exceeding $30.

Ground Transportation: Ride-sharing services are now an approved transportation option and will be reimbursed at the same rates as taxi services. Rental cars are approved when public transportation or ride-sharing is impractical, with a preference for compact or mid-size vehicles unless traveling with three or more colleagues.

All expense reports must be submitted within 14 business days of trip completion. Late submissions may result in delayed reimbursement.

## Q1. What change was made to the business class approval process?
- A. Business class travel is now prohibited for all employees.
- B. Only international travelers may now fly business class.
- C. Approval authority was moved from the CEO to vice presidents, reducing administrative delays.
- D. The CEO must still approve all premium cabin travel requests.
- Answer: C
- Explanation: The previous policy "required CEO approval for any premium cabin travel," which the memo describes as creating "unnecessary administrative burden." The revised policy allows VP-level approval.

## Q2. Under the new policy, what is the maximum hotel rate in San Francisco?
- A. $175 per night
- B. $275 per night
- C. $225 per night
- D. Based on the State Department schedule
- Answer: B
- Explanation: San Francisco is listed among major metropolitan areas, for which "the maximum nightly rate has been increased from $225 to $275."

## Q3. What can be inferred about the previous travel policy?
- A. It had not kept pace with inflation and current market conditions.
- B. It was too generous and allowed excessive spending.
- C. It was only recently implemented for the first time.
- D. It did not include any reimbursement for meals.
- Answer: A
- Explanation: The revisions increase meal allowances "reflecting inflation-adjusted costs" and hotel rates to "reflect current market rates," suggesting the previous policy was outdated.

## Q4. When must expense reports be submitted?
- A. Before the business trip begins
- B. Within 7 business days of trip completion
- C. Within 14 business days of trip completion
- D. By the end of each fiscal quarter
- Answer: C
- Explanation: The memo states "All expense reports must be submitted within 14 business days of trip completion."
