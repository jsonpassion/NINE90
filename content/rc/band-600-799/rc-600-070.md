# Employee Relocation Program

Proposal for Enhanced Employee Relocation Assistance Program

Submitted by the Human Resources Division to the Executive Leadership Team

The current employee relocation program, last updated in 2019, no longer meets the needs of our organization or the expectations of the talent market. An analysis of relocation-related offer declines over the past two years reveals that 38 percent of candidates who rejected offers requiring relocation cited insufficient relocation support as a primary factor. Among internal transfer candidates, 29 percent declined transfers for the same reason. This proposal outlines an enhanced program designed to reduce these barriers and support our geographic expansion strategy.

The proposed program introduces three tiers of relocation assistance based on role level. Tier 1, for individual contributors, provides up to $15,000 in relocation expenses, 30 days of temporary housing, and two house-hunting trips. Tier 2, for managers and senior specialists, increases the benefit to $30,000 in expenses, 60 days of temporary housing, spousal career assistance through a partnered placement agency, and a cost-of-living adjustment for the first 12 months in markets where living costs exceed the origin location by more than 10 percent. Tier 3, for directors and above, provides up to $50,000 in expenses, 90 days of temporary housing, full spousal career support, cost-of-living adjustment, and a one-time home sale assistance benefit covering up to 3 percent of the sale price.

The estimated annual cost increase for the enhanced program is $1.2 million, based on projected relocation volume of 85 employees per year. However, by reducing relocation-related offer declines by even half, we estimate savings of $2.8 million in avoided recruitment costs, reduced time-to-fill, and preserved institutional knowledge. The net benefit is therefore projected at $1.6 million annually.

We recommend implementing the enhanced program effective July 1, with a formal review after 18 months to assess effectiveness and adjust benefit levels as needed.

## Q1. What percentage of candidates declined offers due to insufficient relocation support?
- A. 29 percent
- B. 38 percent
- C. 50 percent
- D. 85 percent
- Answer: B
- Explanation: The proposal states '38 percent of candidates who rejected offers requiring relocation cited insufficient relocation support.'

## Q2. What is unique to the Tier 2 benefit?
- A. Home sale assistance
- B. 90 days of temporary housing
- C. Spousal career assistance and cost-of-living adjustment
- D. $50,000 in relocation expenses
- Answer: C
- Explanation: Tier 2 adds 'spousal career assistance through a partnered placement agency, and a cost-of-living adjustment,' which are not in Tier 1. Tier 3 features are different.

- Explanation: The cost increase is $1.2 million and estimated savings are $2.8 million, yielding 'a net benefit of $1.6 million annually.'
