# Letter to Shareholders: Annual Strategic Outlook

Dear Shareholders,

As we conclude fiscal year 2025, I am writing to share both our accomplishments and our strategic vision for the years ahead. Crestline Holdings delivered record revenue of $9.4 billion, a 9 percent increase over the prior year, while adjusted operating income grew 14 percent to $1.6 billion. These results were achieved despite significant headwinds, including foreign currency fluctuations that reduced reported earnings by approximately $180 million and persistent inflationary pressures on raw material costs.

Our performance was underpinned by the disciplined execution of our three-pillar growth strategy. The first pillar, organic expansion, contributed $520 million in incremental revenue through market share gains in our core infrastructure services business, particularly in the renewable energy and data center construction segments. The second pillar, strategic acquisitions, was advanced through the completion of two transactions: the purchase of Bellingham Engineering for $780 million and the acquisition of a controlling stake in Solaris Digital Solutions for $340 million. These additions expand our capabilities in structural engineering and smart building technologies, respectively.

The third pillar, operational excellence, delivered $290 million in productivity improvements through the deployment of advanced project management systems, workforce optimization, and renegotiated supplier agreements. Our gross margin expanded by 120 basis points to 28.4 percent, reflecting the cumulative impact of these efficiency initiatives.

Looking forward, we are entering fiscal year 2026 with a robust backlog of $14.2 billion, the highest in our history. Government infrastructure spending programs in North America and Europe are expected to sustain demand for our services over the next several years. We are also investing $200 million in research and development to advance our capabilities in modular construction and sustainable building materials.

I extend my gratitude to our 52,000 employees, whose dedication and expertise make these results possible, and to you, our shareholders, for your continued confidence.

Respectfully,
James Whitfield
Chairman and Chief Executive Officer, Crestline Holdings

## Q1. What was Crestline Holdings' revenue growth rate in fiscal year 2025?
- A. 14 percent
- B. 9 percent
- C. 28.4 percent
- D. 120 basis points
- Answer: B
- Explanation: The letter states the company delivered "record revenue of $9.4 billion, a 9 percent increase over the prior year."

## Q2. How did foreign currency fluctuations affect the company's results?
- A. They increased reported earnings by $180 million.
- B. They had no measurable impact on financial performance.
- C. They reduced reported earnings by approximately $180 million.
- D. They caused the company to miss its revenue target entirely.
- Answer: C
- Explanation: The letter notes "foreign currency fluctuations that reduced reported earnings by approximately $180 million" as a significant headwind.

## Q3. What can be inferred about Crestline's outlook for fiscal year 2026?
- A. The company expects a significant decline in government spending.
- B. Management is cautiously optimistic, supported by a record project backlog.
- C. The company plans to divest its infrastructure services business.
- D. Revenue is expected to decline due to increased competition.
- Answer: B
- Explanation: The company enters FY2026 with "a robust backlog of $14.2 billion, the highest in our history," and expects sustained demand from government infrastructure programs, indicating a positive but measured outlook.

## Q4. Which growth pillar contributed the most to incremental revenue?
- A. Organic expansion through market share gains
- B. Strategic acquisitions of two companies
- C. Operational excellence and productivity improvements
- D. Research and development investments
- Answer: A
- Explanation: Organic expansion contributed $520 million in incremental revenue, while operational excellence delivered $290 million in productivity improvements. Acquisitions are reported by purchase price ($780M + $340M) rather than revenue contribution, making organic expansion the clearest revenue contributor.
