# Hospital Implements New Patient Discharge Protocol

To: All Medical Staff
From: Dr. Angela Morris, Chief Medical Officer
Date: March 5

Effective April 1, Riverside General Hospital will implement a revised patient discharge protocol designed to reduce readmission rates and improve post-discharge outcomes. The new protocol was developed in collaboration with our nursing staff, social workers, and patient advocacy groups following a comprehensive six-month review of our discharge practices.

The most significant change involves the introduction of a structured discharge planning process that begins within 24 hours of a patient's admission. A designated discharge coordinator will assess each patient's post-hospital needs, including medication management, follow-up appointments, home care services, and transportation arrangements. This early planning approach replaces the current practice of initiating discharge planning only when a patient is medically cleared to leave.

Additionally, all patients will receive a personalized discharge summary written in clear, non-technical language. The summary will include medication instructions with visual aids, warning signs that should prompt a return visit, and contact information for the patient's care team. Patients will also receive a follow-up phone call within 48 hours of discharge to address any questions or concerns.

Training sessions for medical staff on the new protocol will be conducted during the last two weeks of March. Attendance is mandatory for all physicians, nurses, and discharge coordinators. Session schedules are posted on the hospital intranet.

- A. To reduce the length of hospital stays
- B. To lower readmission rates and improve outcomes
- C. To decrease the number of nursing staff required
- D. To increase the hospital's patient capacity
- Answer: B
- Explanation: The memo says the protocol is "designed to reduce readmission rates and improve post-discharge outcomes."
