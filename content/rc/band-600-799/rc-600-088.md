# Updated Data Privacy Compliance Requirements

To: All Department Managers
From: Kevin Marsh, Chief Compliance Officer
Date: October 20

As many of you are aware, new data privacy regulations will take effect on January 1. These regulations impose stricter requirements on how companies collect, store, and process personal information of customers and employees. Failure to comply could result in substantial fines and reputational damage.

To ensure our organization meets all regulatory requirements by the deadline, we are implementing the following measures. First, all departments that handle personal data must conduct a comprehensive data audit by November 30. This audit should identify all databases, spreadsheets, and file systems that contain personal information, as well as document who has access to this data and for what purpose.

Second, a new consent management system will be integrated into our customer-facing platforms by December 15. This system will require explicit opt-in consent before any personal data is collected. Our marketing team should be aware that this change may initially affect customer data collection rates, and alternative strategies should be prepared accordingly.

Third, mandatory data privacy training will be rolled out to all employees during November. The training, which takes approximately 90 minutes to complete, will be available as an online module through the learning management system. Employees who handle sensitive data will be required to complete an additional advanced module lasting 45 minutes. Completion records will be tracked and reported to the compliance office.

Department managers should schedule time during their next team meeting to discuss these changes and address any concerns.
