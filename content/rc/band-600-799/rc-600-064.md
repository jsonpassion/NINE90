# Supply Chain Optimization Report

Annual Supply Chain Performance Review — Fiscal Year 2025

Prepared by the Operations Excellence Division, Hartwell Manufacturing Group

Hartwell Manufacturing Group's supply chain operations achieved notable improvements in fiscal year 2025, though several areas require continued attention. This report summarizes key performance metrics, identifies root causes of operational disruptions, and outlines strategic recommendations for the coming fiscal year.

Total supply chain costs as a percentage of revenue decreased from 23.4 percent in FY2024 to 21.8 percent in FY2025, representing savings of approximately $14.6 million. This improvement was driven primarily by the consolidation of our supplier base from 340 vendors to 275 vendors, which enhanced our negotiating leverage and simplified procurement processes. Average order-to-delivery cycle time was reduced from 18 days to 14.5 days through the implementation of an AI-powered demand forecasting system that improved inventory positioning accuracy by 28 percent.

Despite these gains, the organization experienced 23 significant supply disruption events during the year, compared to 17 in FY2024. Analysis reveals that 65 percent of these disruptions originated from three semiconductor component suppliers located in a single geographic region, highlighting a concentration risk that must be addressed. The remaining disruptions were attributed to transportation delays caused by port congestion and severe weather events.

We recommend the following strategic initiatives for FY2026. First, geographic diversification of the semiconductor supply base by qualifying at least two alternative suppliers in different regions by September 2026. Second, investment of $3.2 million in a real-time supply chain visibility platform that provides end-to-end tracking of all shipments. Third, establishment of strategic safety stock for the 50 highest-risk components, increasing inventory holding costs by an estimated $1.8 million but reducing projected disruption-related losses by $7.5 million annually.
