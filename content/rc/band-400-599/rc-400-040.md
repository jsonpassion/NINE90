# Workplace Harassment Prevention Policy

MEMORANDUM: WORKPLACE HARASSMENT PREVENTION POLICY UPDATE

To: All Employees
From: Amanda Clarke, VP of Human Resources
Date: March 20, 2026
Subject: Updated Harassment Prevention Policy and Training Requirement

In accordance with recent changes to state employment law, Pacific Ridge Corporation is updating its Workplace Harassment Prevention Policy, effective April 1, 2026. All employees are required to read the updated policy, which is available on the HR portal, and complete the associated training by May 15, 2026.

The updated policy expands the definition of workplace harassment to include conduct occurring in virtual environments such as video meetings, chat platforms, and work-related social media interactions. It also introduces a new provision requiring managers to report any harassment complaints they receive or witness within 24 hours, even if the affected party has not filed a formal complaint.

Training sessions will be offered in two formats: an in-person workshop of 2 hours, scheduled for April 8, April 15, and April 22, or a self-paced online module accessible through the Learning Portal. The online module takes approximately 90 minutes to complete. Both options fulfill the training requirement.

Employees who do not complete the training by May 15 will be required to attend a makeup session and may face disciplinary action. The company has also engaged an independent ombudsperson, Dr. Rachel Nguyen, who will be available starting April 1 as a confidential resource for employees who wish to discuss concerns outside of the formal HR complaint process.

Questions about the policy should be directed to the HR Compliance team at compliance@pacificridge.com.

## Q1. What does the updated policy now cover that it did not before?
- A. Harassment during business travel
- B. Third-party vendor interactions
- C. Conduct in virtual environments
- D. Off-duty personal conduct
- Answer: C
- Explanation: The policy expands to include conduct in virtual environments such as video meetings and chat platforms.

## Q2. How quickly must managers report harassment complaints?
- A. Within 24 hours
- B. Immediately
- C. Within 48 hours
- D. Within one week
- Answer: A
- Explanation: Managers must report complaints within 24 hours.
