# Coworking Spaces See Rapid Growth in Suburban Areas

A recent industry report shows that coworking spaces in suburban areas have grown by 60 percent over the past two years, driven largely by the shift toward remote and hybrid work arrangements. The report, published by Commercial Real Estate Trends, surveyed 200 coworking operators across the country.

Suburban coworking spaces typically charge between $250 and $450 per month for a dedicated desk, compared to $500 to $800 in major urban centers. Many operators also offer day passes ranging from $25 to $40, making them attractive to freelancers and remote workers who need occasional office access.

The most popular amenities cited by members include high-speed internet, meeting rooms, free coffee, and printing services. However, the report notes that networking opportunities and community events are becoming increasingly important factors in members' decisions to join.

Industry analysts expect the trend to continue as companies reduce their traditional office footprints and encourage employees to work from locations closer to their homes. Several major coworking brands have announced plans to open new locations in suburban markets over the next 18 months.
