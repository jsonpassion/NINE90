# Local Retailer Expands to Three New Locations

Brightway Home Goods, a popular home furnishing retailer based in Portland, announced plans to open three new stores in the Pacific Northwest region by the end of this year. The company, which currently operates five locations, reported a 22 percent increase in revenue over the past fiscal year.

According to CEO Maria Gonzalez, the expansion will create approximately 120 new jobs across the three locations. The new stores will be located in Seattle, Vancouver, and Eugene. Construction on the Seattle location has already begun, with a planned opening date in August. The Vancouver and Eugene stores are expected to open in October and November, respectively.

The expansion is being funded through a combination of company profits and a $3 million loan from First National Bank. Gonzalez noted that the company plans to maintain its focus on locally sourced products, which account for roughly 40 percent of its inventory.

Industry analysts view the expansion positively, citing the growing demand for home improvement products in the region. However, some experts caution that increased competition from online retailers could present challenges for brick-and-mortar stores in the coming years.
