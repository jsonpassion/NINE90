# Workplace Safety Incident Reporting

WORKPLACE SAFETY INCIDENT REPORTING PROCEDURE

To: All Employees and Contractors
From: Michael Torres, Director of Environmental Health and Safety
Date: March 20, 2026
Subject: Updated Incident Reporting Requirements

In alignment with OSHA regulatory updates and our commitment to maintaining a safe work environment, the following incident reporting procedures have been revised and are effective immediately.

All workplace incidents, including near-misses, must be reported within 2 hours of occurrence using the Incident Report Form available on the Safety Portal or from your floor safety coordinator. A near-miss is defined as any unplanned event that did not result in injury but had the potential to do so. Reporting near-misses is critical, as research indicates that for every serious injury, there are approximately 30 minor injuries and 300 near-misses.

For incidents involving physical injury: Seek immediate medical attention at the on-site first aid station or call emergency services if needed. Notify your supervisor immediately. Complete the Incident Report Form within 2 hours. Do not disturb the incident scene until photographs have been taken and the safety team has completed its initial assessment.

The Safety Department will conduct an investigation within 48 hours of receiving a report. Root cause analysis will be completed within 10 business days, and corrective actions will be documented and tracked through the Safety Management System. Monthly safety performance reports will be shared with all departments.

Employees will not face disciplinary action for reporting incidents or near-misses in good faith. This protection is essential to maintaining an open reporting culture. Supervisors who discourage reporting or fail to forward reports to the Safety Department will face disciplinary measures.

For emergencies, call the Safety Hotline at ext. 9111. Non-emergency questions can be directed to safety@company.com.

## Q1. How quickly must incidents be reported?
- A. Within 2 hours
- B. Within 1 hour
- C. Within 30 minutes
- D. Within 24 hours
- Answer: A
- Explanation: All incidents must be reported within 2 hours of occurrence.

## Q2. What is the timeline for completing a root cause analysis?
- A. 48 hours
- B. 10 business days
- C. 5 business days
- D. 30 days
- Answer: B
- Explanation: Root cause analysis will be completed within 10 business days.

## Q3. What protection is given to employees who report incidents?
- A. A financial reward
- B. No disciplinary action for good-faith reporting
- C. Automatic time off
- D. Anonymity in all cases
- Answer: B
- Explanation: Employees will not face disciplinary action for reporting incidents in good faith.
