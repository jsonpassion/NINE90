# Local College Expands Online Course Offerings

Millbrook Community College announced yesterday that it will offer 35 new online courses beginning in the fall semester. The expansion is part of the college's plan to make higher education more accessible to working professionals and students in rural areas.

The new courses will cover subjects including business management, information technology, graphic design, and healthcare administration. Most courses will be entirely asynchronous, meaning students can complete coursework at their own pace within weekly deadlines. A few courses will include live virtual sessions on weekday evenings.

Tuition for online courses is the same as for on-campus courses: $185 per credit hour for in-state residents and $340 per credit hour for out-of-state students. Financial aid and payment plans are available for eligible students.

Registration for the fall semester opens on July 1. Prospective students can browse the full course catalog and apply online at www.millbrookcc.edu. An online orientation session for new students will be held on August 20.

## Q1. Why is the college expanding its online courses?
- A. To reduce campus operating costs
- B. To make education more accessible
- C. To replace all in-person classes
- D. To attract international students
- Answer: B
- Explanation: The article says the expansion is "part of the college's plan to make higher education more accessible to working professionals and students in rural areas."
