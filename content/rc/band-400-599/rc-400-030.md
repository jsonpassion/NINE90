# Remote Work Policy Update

MEMORANDUM: UPDATED REMOTE WORK POLICY

To: All Employees
From: Jennifer Walsh, VP of Human Resources
Date: March 20, 2026
Subject: Hybrid Work Model — Effective May 1, 2026

After careful consideration of employee feedback and productivity data gathered over the past year, the company is transitioning to a structured hybrid work model. This policy replaces the temporary flexible arrangement that has been in place since January 2025.

Under the new model, employees in eligible positions may work remotely up to three days per week. However, all team members must be present in the office on Tuesdays and Thursdays, which have been designated as "collaboration days." Department managers may require additional in-office days based on project needs, with at least one week's advance notice.

To maintain eligibility for remote work, employees must demonstrate consistent performance ratings of "meets expectations" or higher, maintain a reliable internet connection capable of supporting video conferencing, be available during core business hours of 9:00 AM to 3:00 PM regardless of location, and complete the updated Remote Work Agreement form by April 15.

The company will provide a one-time $500 stipend for home office equipment to employees who have not previously received such support. Ongoing expenses such as internet service remain the employee's responsibility. Employees who prefer to work in the office full-time are welcome to do so and will have priority access to dedicated desk assignments.

Please direct questions to your HR business partner or email remotework@company.com.

## Q1. How many days per week can eligible employees work remotely?
- A. One day
- B. Two days
- C. Four days
- D. Three days
- Answer: D
- Explanation: Employees in eligible positions may work remotely up to three days per week.

## Q2. Which days are designated as collaboration days?

- D. Monday and Friday
- Answer: C
- Explanation: All team members must be present on Tuesdays and Thursdays.
