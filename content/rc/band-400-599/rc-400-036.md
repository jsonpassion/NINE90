# Warehouse Inventory Discrepancy

From: Carlos Mendez, Warehouse Manager
To: Helen Park, Supply Chain Director
Date: March 22, 2026
Subject: Q1 Inventory Audit — Discrepancies Found

Dear Helen,

I am writing to report the findings of our Q1 physical inventory audit conducted on March 18-19. Unfortunately, we identified significant discrepancies between our inventory management system records and the actual stock count.

Out of 4,200 SKUs audited, 287 items (approximately 6.8 percent) showed variances exceeding the acceptable 2 percent threshold. The most affected categories were electronic components (42 items with an average shortage of 12 units each) and packaging materials (38 items with an average surplus of 25 units each). The total value of unaccounted inventory is estimated at $34,500.

After preliminary investigation, we believe the primary causes are: incorrect data entry during the receiving process, failure to record product returns in the system on the same day they arrive, and a software glitch in the barcode scanning system that occasionally assigns items to the wrong location code.

I recommend the following immediate actions: implement a mandatory dual-verification process for all incoming shipments, require same-day entry of all product returns, and schedule an IT review of the barcode scanning software. I have already spoken with the IT department, and they can begin the software review on April 7.

I would like to schedule a meeting with you next week to discuss these findings and the proposed corrective actions in detail.

Best regards,
Carlos

## Q1. What percentage of items showed unacceptable variances?

- Answer: D

- C. $34,500

- Answer: C

## Q3. What is one proposed solution to address the discrepancies?
- A. Hiring additional warehouse staff
- B. Implementing dual-verification for incoming shipments
- C. Replacing all barcode scanners
