# Mid-Year Performance Feedback

From: Karen Yoshida, Senior Director
To: Michael Santos, Marketing Analyst
Date: March 16, 2026
Subject: Mid-Year Performance Review Summary

Dear Michael,

Thank you for meeting with me earlier today to discuss your performance over the first half of the year. I wanted to follow up with a written summary of our conversation for your records.

Overall, your performance has been strong. Your work on the social media analytics project was particularly impressive, resulting in a 28 percent increase in audience engagement across our main platforms. Your ability to present complex data in clear, visual formats has been recognized by several members of the leadership team.

There are two areas where I would like to see continued growth. First, I encourage you to take a more active role in team meetings by sharing your insights and recommendations. Your analysis is excellent, but your ideas deserve a wider audience. Second, time management on longer projects could be improved. The Q1 campaign report, for example, was submitted three days past the original deadline.

For the second half of the year, I suggest setting a goal to lead at least one cross-department presentation. I also recommend enrolling in the project management workshop offered by the training department in May. These steps will position you well for advancement opportunities.

## Q1. What was the result of Michael's social media analytics project?
- A. A 15 percent increase in sales
- B. A 28 percent increase in audience engagement
- C. A 35 percent reduction in marketing costs
- D. A 20 percent increase in website traffic
- Answer: B
- Explanation: The email states Michael's work "resulted in a 28 percent increase in audience engagement across our main platforms."

## Q2. What issue was identified with Michael's time management?
- A. He frequently arrived late to work.
- B. He spent too much time in meetings.
- C. The Q1 campaign report was submitted three days late.
- D. He missed two important project deadlines.
- Answer: C
- Explanation: The email notes "the Q1 campaign report was submitted three days past the original deadline" as an example of time management needing improvement.

## Q3. What does Karen suggest Michael do in the second half of the year?
- A. Transfer to a different department
- B. Reduce his workload to focus on fewer projects
- C. Lead at least one cross-department presentation
- D. Take a leave of absence for professional development
- Answer: C
- Explanation: Karen suggests "setting a goal to lead at least one cross-department presentation" and enrolling in a project management workshop.
