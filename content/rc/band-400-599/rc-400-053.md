# Software License Audit

To: All Department Managers
From: Kevin O'Brien, IT Director
Date: March 21, 2026
Subject: Software License Compliance Audit — Action Required

The IT department has completed its semi-annual software license audit, and the results indicate several areas of concern that require immediate attention from all department managers.

Our audit found that the company currently has 1,240 installed instances of licensed software across all departments. However, we hold valid licenses for only 1,085 of those installations, leaving us with 155 unlicensed installations — a compliance gap of 12.5 percent. The primary offenders are Adobe Creative Cloud (47 excess installations), Microsoft Project (38 excess installations), and Tableau Desktop (31 excess installations).

Non-compliance with software licensing agreements carries significant financial and legal risks. Software publishers conduct their own audits, and penalties for unlicensed software can range from one to three times the retail price per installation. Based on current pricing, our potential liability if audited by these vendors could exceed $175,000.

To resolve this issue, I need each department manager to complete the Software Inventory Form by April 4, identifying which installations are actively being used and which can be removed. The IT team will then uninstall unused software and procure additional licenses where needed. The estimated cost of bringing all software into compliance is approximately $62,000, which will be allocated across departments proportionally.

Going forward, all software installations must be requested through the IT Service Portal to prevent future compliance issues. Unauthorized installations will be flagged and removed during monthly automated scans.

Please treat this as a high priority.
