# Supply Contract Negotiation

From: Rachel Kim, Procurement Manager
To: Thomas Weber, Atlas Supply Co.
Date: March 17, 2026
Subject: Contract Renewal — Proposed Terms

Dear Mr. Weber,

Thank you for your prompt response regarding the renewal of our annual supply contract. We have carefully reviewed your proposed pricing for the upcoming year, and I would like to discuss a few points before we finalize the agreement.

First, while we appreciate the 3 percent discount offered on bulk orders over 500 units, we believe a 5 percent discount would better reflect our long-standing partnership and the volume of orders we have placed over the past four years. Last year alone, our total order volume exceeded 8,000 units.

Second, we would like to request a change to the delivery schedule. Currently, shipments arrive every two weeks. Given our updated inventory management system, we would prefer weekly deliveries of smaller quantities. This adjustment would help us reduce warehouse storage costs and improve stock rotation.

Finally, we are interested in adding your new eco-friendly packaging option to our contract. Could you provide pricing details for this service? Our company has committed to reducing packaging waste by 30 percent this year, and this option would support that goal.

I look forward to your response and hope we can reach an agreement that benefits both parties.

## Q1. What discount does Rachel Kim propose for bulk orders?
- A. 3 percent
- B. 5 percent
- C. 8 percent
- D. 10 percent
- Answer: B
- Explanation: Rachel states "we believe a 5 percent discount would better reflect our long-standing partnership," compared to the 3 percent originally offered.

## Q2. How does Rachel want to change the delivery schedule?
- A. From weekly to biweekly
- B. From monthly to biweekly
- C. From biweekly to weekly
- D. From weekly to monthly
- Answer: C
- Explanation: The email states "currently, shipments arrive every two weeks" and Rachel requests "weekly deliveries of smaller quantities."

## Q3. Why is the company interested in eco-friendly packaging?
- A. It is required by new government regulations.
- B. Customers have demanded it.
- C. The company aims to reduce packaging waste by 30 percent.
- D. It is less expensive than standard packaging.
- Answer: C
- Explanation: Rachel explains "our company has committed to reducing packaging waste by 30 percent this year, and this option would support that goal."
