# Inventory Management Update

From: Lisa Chen, Warehouse Manager
To: Purchasing Department
Date: March 23, 2026
Subject: Monthly Inventory Report — March 2026

Dear Purchasing Team,

Please find below a summary of the March inventory status for our main distribution center. Overall inventory levels have decreased by 12 percent compared to February, primarily due to strong seasonal demand for outdoor products.

Several items have reached critically low stock levels and require immediate reorder. Outdoor furniture sets currently have only 45 units remaining, against an average monthly demand of 120 units. Garden tool kits are also running low at 60 units, compared to a typical monthly demand of 90 units. Conversely, indoor lighting products are overstocked at 850 units, nearly double the average monthly sales of 450 units.

I recommend placing an expedited order for outdoor furniture and garden tools by March 28 to avoid stockouts before the April sales peak. For indoor lighting, I suggest reducing the next scheduled order by 50 percent and running a promotional discount to clear excess inventory.

The full inventory report with item-by-item details is available in the shared system under Warehouse > Reports > March 2026. Please confirm the reorder quantities by March 26 so we can coordinate delivery schedules.

Thank you for your prompt attention to this matter.

## Q1. By how much did inventory levels decrease in March?
- A. 5 percent
- B. 8 percent
- C. 12 percent
- D. 15 percent
- Answer: C
- Explanation: The email states 'Overall inventory levels have decreased by 12 percent compared to February.'

## Q2. What is the main concern regarding outdoor furniture?
- A. The quality is below standard.
- B. Current stock is far below average monthly demand.
- C. Customers have been returning the items.
- D. The supplier has discontinued the product.
- Answer: B
- Explanation: The email says only 45 units remain 'against an average monthly demand of 120 units.'

## Q3. What does Lisa recommend for indoor lighting products?
- A. Return the excess to the supplier
- B. Move them to a different warehouse
- C. Reduce the next order and run a promotional discount
- D. Donate them to a charity
- Answer: C
- Explanation: Lisa suggests 'reducing the next scheduled order by 50 percent and running a promotional discount to clear excess inventory.'
