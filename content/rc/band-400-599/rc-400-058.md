# Fleet Management Optimization

To: Operations Team
From: Derek Lawson, Fleet Manager
Date: March 21, 2026
Subject: Fleet Optimization Recommendations for Q2-Q3

After completing our annual fleet assessment covering all 85 company vehicles, I have identified several opportunities to reduce costs and improve efficiency. Our current annual fleet operating cost is $1.4 million, and I believe we can achieve savings of approximately $180,000 through the following measures.

First, I recommend replacing 12 older vehicles (model year 2019 and earlier) with fuel-efficient hybrid models. These vehicles currently average 22 miles per gallon, while replacement hybrids average 48 miles per gallon. Based on our average annual mileage of 18,000 miles per vehicle, the fuel savings would be approximately $1,600 per vehicle per year, totaling $19,200 annually for the 12 vehicles. The trade-in value of the old vehicles is estimated at $96,000, which would offset a significant portion of the $240,000 purchase cost.

Second, implementing a GPS-based fleet tracking system would cost $15,000 for installation and $350 per vehicle per month. However, route optimization data from similar implementations has shown a typical reduction of 15 percent in total miles driven, which would save an estimated $42,000 per year in fuel and maintenance.

Third, consolidating our maintenance contracts with a single national provider instead of using local shops at each office location would save approximately $35,000 per year through volume discounts.

I have attached detailed cost-benefit analyses for each recommendation. I would appreciate the opportunity to present these findings at the April operations meeting.

Best regards,
Derek
