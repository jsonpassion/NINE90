# Changes to Employee Non-Disclosure Agreement

To: All Employees
From: Patricia Dunn, General Counsel
Date: November 3

The Legal Department has updated the company's Non-Disclosure Agreement (NDA) to reflect recent changes in data protection regulations. All current employees are required to review and sign the updated agreement by November 30.

The key changes are as follows: The definition of confidential information has been expanded to include customer data, internal communications, and proprietary software tools. Additionally, the non-disclosure obligation now extends for two years after an employee leaves the company, increased from the previous one-year period.

A summary of the changes has been emailed to all employees. The full agreement is available for download on the Legal Department's page on the company intranet. Employees who have questions about specific provisions are encouraged to schedule a consultation with a member of the legal team.

Signed agreements should be submitted to your department's HR representative. Employees who do not sign by the deadline may be subject to restricted access to certain company systems until the agreement is completed.

## Q1. Why was the NDA updated?
- A. Because of a data breach incident
- B. To reflect changes in data protection regulations
- C. Due to a merger with another company
- D. To simplify the language
- Answer: B
- Explanation: The letter says the NDA was updated "to reflect recent changes in data protection regulations."

## Q2. How long does the non-disclosure obligation last after leaving the company?
- A. Two years
- B. One year
- C. Six months
- D. Five years
- Answer: A
- Explanation: The letter states "the non-disclosure obligation now extends for two years after an employee leaves the company."

## Q3. What may happen to employees who miss the signing deadline?
- A. They will be terminated
- B. They will receive a pay reduction
- C. They may have restricted system access
- D. They will be transferred to another department
- Answer: C
- Explanation: The letter says employees "may be subject to restricted access to certain company systems until the agreement is completed."
