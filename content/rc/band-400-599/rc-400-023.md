# Client Data Security Policy

To: All Department Heads
From: Karen Fischer, Chief Information Officer
Date: March 21, 2026
Subject: Mandatory Client Data Security Training

Following a recent industry-wide increase in data breaches, the company is implementing enhanced data security protocols effective immediately. All employees who handle client information must complete a mandatory online training module by April 18, 2026.

The training covers four key areas: password management and two-factor authentication, recognizing phishing emails and social engineering attempts, proper handling and storage of sensitive client documents, and incident reporting procedures. The module takes approximately 90 minutes to complete and is available on the Learning Portal under "Compliance Training."

Department heads are responsible for ensuring that every team member completes the training by the deadline. A completion report will be generated automatically and sent to HR. Employees who do not complete the training by April 18 will have their network access restricted until the requirement is fulfilled.

Additionally, all client files stored on personal devices must be transferred to the secure company server by April 11. The IT department will conduct random audits starting May 1 to verify compliance. For questions about the policy, contact the IT Security team at security@company.com.

## Q1. What is the deadline for completing the security training?
- A. March 31, 2026
- B. April 11, 2026
- C. April 18, 2026
- D. May 1, 2026
- Answer: C
- Explanation: The memo states the training must be completed by April 18, 2026.

## Q2. What will happen to employees who miss the training deadline?
- A. They will receive a written warning
- B. Their salary will be reduced
- C. Their network access will be restricted
- D. They will be placed on probation
- Answer: C
- Explanation: Employees who do not complete the training will have their network access restricted.

## Q3. When will IT audits begin?
- A. April 11
- B. May 1
- C. April 25
- D. April 18
- Answer: B
- Explanation: The IT department will conduct random audits starting May 1.
