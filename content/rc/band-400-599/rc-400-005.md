# Updated Remote Work Policy

To: All Employees
From: Human Resources Department
Date: March 15, 2026
Subject: Updated Remote Work Policy

Effective April 1, the company will implement a revised remote work policy. Employees in all departments may now work from home up to three days per week, an increase from the previous limit of two days. To participate, employees must submit a remote work schedule to their direct supervisor by the 25th of each month for the following month.

All remote workers are required to be available during core business hours from 10:00 a.m. to 3:00 p.m. and must attend all scheduled video meetings with their cameras turned on. Employees who fail to meet these requirements may have their remote work privileges reduced or revoked.

Please note that certain positions in customer service and facilities management are exempt from this policy due to the nature of their duties. Questions should be directed to the HR helpdesk at extension 4500.

## Q1. How many days per week can employees now work remotely?
- A. One day
- B. Two days
- C. Three days
- D. Four days
- Answer: C
- Explanation: The memo states employees may work from home "up to three days per week," which is an increase from the previous two-day limit.

## Q2. What must employees do to participate in the remote work program?
- A. Get approval from the CEO
- B. Submit a remote work schedule to their supervisor
- C. Complete a training course
- D. Purchase their own equipment
- Answer: B
- Explanation: The memo requires employees to "submit a remote work schedule to their direct supervisor by the 25th of each month."

## Q3. Which department is mentioned as exempt from the new policy?
- A. Marketing
- B. Finance
- C. Customer service
- D. Research
- Answer: C
- Explanation: The memo states that "certain positions in customer service and facilities management are exempt from this policy due to the nature of their duties."
