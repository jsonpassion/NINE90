# Supplier Contract Negotiation

From: Angela Morrison, Procurement Manager
To: Board of Directors
Date: March 22, 2026
Subject: Recommended Supplier Contract — Premium Paper Products

Dear Board Members,

Following a competitive bidding process involving five qualified suppliers, I am recommending that we enter into a two-year supply agreement with Northland Paper Co. for our premium packaging and stationery needs.

Northland submitted the most favorable overall proposal with a unit cost of $0.42 per sheet for premium cardstock, compared to the current supplier's rate of $0.51 per sheet. This represents an 18 percent cost reduction that would save approximately $156,000 annually based on our current consumption volume. Their quality samples met or exceeded our specifications in all seven testing categories.

Northland has proposed the following terms: a guaranteed fixed price for the first 12 months with a maximum increase of 3 percent in year two, delivery within 5 business days for standard orders and 48-hour turnaround for rush orders at a 15 percent surcharge, a minimum order quantity of 10,000 sheets per order, and a dedicated account manager for our company.

I should note that Northland's facility is located in Wisconsin, approximately 200 miles further from our distribution center than our current supplier in Illinois. However, Northland has committed to absorbing all shipping costs within the contract price, so this distance will not affect our landed cost.

I request board approval to proceed with the contract. The proposed signing date is April 7, with first deliveries beginning April 21.

Respectfully,
Angela Morrison

## Q1. How much will the company save annually with the new supplier?
- A. $156,000
- B. $120,000
- C. $96,000
- D. $198,000
- Answer: A
- Explanation: The cost reduction would save approximately $156,000 annually.

## Q2. What is the surcharge for rush orders?
- A. 10 percent
- B. 15 percent
- C. 12 percent
- D. 20 percent
- Answer: B
- Explanation: Rush orders have a 48-hour turnaround at a 15 percent surcharge.

## Q3. Why is the supplier's distance not a concern?
- A. The company will use a different shipping method
- B. A new distribution center is being built closer
- C. Northland will absorb all shipping costs
- D. Orders will be placed less frequently
- Answer: C
- Explanation: Northland has committed to absorbing all shipping costs within the contract price.
