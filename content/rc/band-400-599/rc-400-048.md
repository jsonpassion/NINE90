# Office Space Utilization Study

To: Executive Leadership Team
From: Sandra Choi, Facilities Director
Date: March 22, 2026
Subject: Office Space Utilization Study — Findings and Recommendations

I have completed the three-month office space utilization study conducted from January through March 2026. Using sensor data, badge swipe records, and employee surveys, we gathered comprehensive information about how our 45,000-square-foot office space is being used.

Key findings: Average daily office occupancy is 58 percent on Tuesdays and Wednesdays (our busiest days) and only 31 percent on Mondays and Fridays. Conference rooms are booked for an average of 6.2 hours per day but are actually occupied for only 3.8 hours, indicating a 39 percent no-show rate. Individual workstations are occupied an average of 4.1 hours per day during an 8-hour workday, and 22 percent of assigned desks are unused on any given day.

Based on these findings, I recommend the following: convert 30 percent of individual workstations to hot-desking stations that can be reserved through a booking app, reduce the number of conference rooms from 12 to 8 and convert the remaining space into collaboration zones and quiet focus rooms, implement a conference room check-in system that automatically releases bookings if the room is not occupied within 15 minutes, and sublease approximately 8,000 square feet of underutilized space on the fourth floor.

The estimated annual savings from subleasing and reduced maintenance costs would be approximately $320,000. Implementation would require an initial investment of $85,000 for furniture reconfiguration and technology upgrades.

I welcome the opportunity to discuss these recommendations at the next executive meeting.
