# Intellectual Property and Confidentiality Policy

INTELLECTUAL PROPERTY AND CONFIDENTIALITY AGREEMENT

Effective Date: April 1, 2026
Issued by: Legal Department
Applicable to: All employees, contractors, and temporary staff

This agreement supersedes all previous intellectual property and confidentiality policies. By continuing employment after the effective date, all covered individuals agree to the following terms.

Intellectual Property: All inventions, designs, software, documentation, processes, and other works created by employees during the course of their employment, or using company resources, are the sole property of the company. This includes work created outside of normal business hours if it relates to the company's current or reasonably anticipated business activities. Employees must promptly disclose all potentially patentable inventions to the Legal Department using the Invention Disclosure Form.

Confidential Information: Employees shall not disclose, publish, or otherwise reveal any confidential information belonging to the company to any third party during or after employment. Confidential information includes but is not limited to: client lists and contact details, pricing strategies and financial projections, proprietary algorithms and source code, business plans and strategic initiatives, and employee compensation data.

Upon termination of employment, all confidential materials must be returned within 5 business days. Former employees remain bound by the confidentiality obligations for a period of 3 years after their departure date. The non-compete clause restricts former employees from working for a direct competitor within a 50-mile radius for 12 months after leaving the company.

Violations of this agreement may result in immediate termination and legal action, including injunctive relief and damages. Employees with questions about this policy should consult the Legal Department at legal@company.com.

Each employee must sign the updated agreement and return it to HR by April 15, 2026.

## Q1. Who owns work created using company resources outside business hours?
- A. The company
- B. The employee
- C. Both share ownership
- D. It depends on the project
- Answer: A
- Explanation: Work created using company resources is the sole property of the company, including outside business hours if it relates to company activities.

## Q2. How long do confidentiality obligations last after leaving the company?
- A. 1 year
- B. 2 years
- C. 3 years
- D. 5 years
- Answer: C
- Explanation: Former employees remain bound by confidentiality obligations for 3 years after departure.
