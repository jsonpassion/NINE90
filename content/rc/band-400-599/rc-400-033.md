# Quarterly Budget Adjustments

To: All Department Managers
From: Nathan Brooks, Chief Financial Officer
Date: March 21, 2026
Subject: Q2 Budget Adjustments

Following the quarterly financial review, I am writing to inform all department managers of adjustments to the Q2 operating budget. The board has approved a company-wide cost reduction target of 8 percent for the second quarter due to lower-than-expected revenue in the enterprise software division.

Each department's discretionary spending budget will be reduced by 10 percent compared to Q1 allocations. However, budgets for customer-facing activities, including sales travel and client entertainment, will remain unchanged to protect revenue-generating operations. Additionally, all non-essential capital expenditures exceeding $5,000 must receive approval from the Finance Committee before purchase.

To offset these reductions, the company will implement the following measures: consolidating software subscriptions to eliminate redundant tools (estimated savings of $45,000 per quarter), renegotiating contracts with three major vendors, and temporarily suspending the office renovation project scheduled for the third floor.

I recognize these adjustments require difficult decisions. To support this process, each department manager should submit a revised Q2 budget proposal to the Finance Department by April 8. The Finance team will schedule individual meetings during the week of April 14 to review and finalize each department's budget.

Please feel free to reach out if you need guidance on prioritizing expenditures during this period.

## Q1. What is the company-wide cost reduction target?
- A. 8 percent
- B. 5 percent
- C. 10 percent
- D. 12 percent
- Answer: A
- Explanation: The board approved a company-wide cost reduction target of 8 percent.

## Q2. Which budgets will NOT be reduced?
- A. Marketing and advertising
- B. Office supplies and equipment
- C. Customer-facing activities
- D. Training and development
- Answer: C
- Explanation: Budgets for customer-facing activities will remain unchanged to protect revenue.

## Q3. What is the estimated quarterly savings from consolidating software subscriptions?
- A. $25,000
- B. $35,000
- C. $55,000
- D. $45,000
- Answer: D
- Explanation: Consolidating software subscriptions has an estimated savings of $45,000 per quarter.
