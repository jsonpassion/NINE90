# New Mixed-Use Development Approved for Downtown

The city council voted 7-2 on Tuesday to approve the construction of Meridian Place, a mixed-use development in the downtown core. The $45 million project will include 120 residential units, 15,000 square feet of retail space on the ground floor, and an underground parking garage with 200 spaces.

Construction is expected to begin in the third quarter of this year and take approximately 18 months to complete. The developer, Clearview Properties, has committed to making 20 percent of the residential units affordable housing, with rents set at below-market rates for qualifying residents.

Local business owners have expressed mixed reactions. Some welcome the expected increase in foot traffic, while others are concerned about construction disruptions and increased competition for parking during the building phase. The city has agreed to provide temporary street parking permits for affected businesses.

Clearview Properties plans to hold a community information session on April 10 at the downtown library to address residents' questions about the project timeline and design.
