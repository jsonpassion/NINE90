# Electric Vehicle Charging Stations Coming to City Parking

The city's Department of Transportation announced plans to install 50 electric vehicle charging stations in public parking lots across the downtown area. The project, funded by a $2.5 million federal grant, is expected to be completed by the end of next year.

The charging stations will be Level 2 chargers, capable of fully charging most electric vehicles in four to six hours. Ten fast-charging stations that can provide an 80 percent charge in approximately 30 minutes will also be installed at two high-traffic locations.

Drivers will pay $2 per hour for standard charging and $8 per session for fast charging. A mobile app will be available for locating available stations, starting and stopping charging sessions, and processing payments. The app will be free to download.

The city hopes the initiative will encourage more residents to switch to electric vehicles by addressing one of the main barriers to adoption: limited charging infrastructure.
