# Data Retention and Disposal Policy

DATA RETENTION AND DISPOSAL POLICY

Effective Date: April 1, 2026
Issued by: Legal and Compliance Department
Applies to: All employees, contractors, and third-party service providers

This policy establishes the requirements for retaining and disposing of company records and data in compliance with applicable laws, regulations, and business needs.

Record retention periods are as follows: Financial records and tax documents must be retained for 7 years from the end of the fiscal year. Employment records must be maintained for 5 years after the employee's departure from the company. Client contracts and related correspondence must be kept for 6 years after the contract's expiration or termination. Marketing materials and internal communications may be disposed of after 2 years unless subject to a legal hold.

Electronic data stored on company servers will be automatically archived after 3 years of inactivity and permanently deleted after the applicable retention period expires. The IT department will execute quarterly data purges in January, April, July, and October of each year.

Physical documents that have exceeded their retention period must be shredded using cross-cut shredders. Collection bins for confidential shredding are located on each floor near the copy rooms. A certified shredding service collects materials every two weeks on alternating Fridays.

Employees who become aware of pending or potential litigation must immediately notify the Legal Department, which will issue a litigation hold preventing the destruction of any potentially relevant records. Failure to comply with a litigation hold may result in serious legal consequences for both the individual and the company.

Questions about this policy should be directed to compliance@company.com.

## Q1. How long must financial records be retained?
- A. 3 years
- B. 5 years
- C. 7 years
- D. 6 years
- Answer: C
- Explanation: Financial records and tax documents must be retained for 7 years.

## Q2. How often are quarterly data purges executed?
- A. February, May, August, November
- B. January, April, July, October
- C. March, June, September, December
- D. Monthly
- Answer: B
- Explanation: Quarterly data purges occur in January, April, July, and October.

## Q3. What must employees do if they learn of potential litigation?
- A. Immediately shred related documents
- B. Notify their department manager
- C. Archive all documents to the cloud
- D. Immediately notify the Legal Department
- Answer: D
- Explanation: Employees must immediately notify the Legal Department, which will issue a litigation hold.
