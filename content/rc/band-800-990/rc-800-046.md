# Annual Report Letter to Shareholders — Everest Reinsurance Group

Dear Shareholders,

The property and catastrophe reinsurance market experienced extraordinary conditions during 2025, and I am writing to report on how Everest Reinsurance Group navigated this environment and the strategic positioning decisions we made to capitalize on favorable market dynamics while maintaining disciplined underwriting standards.

Gross written premiums reached $14.8 billion, a 16 percent increase over the prior year, driven by rate increases averaging 18 percent in our property catastrophe book and meaningful growth in our specialty lines segment. Net income was $1.92 billion, compared with a loss of $340 million in 2024, which was dominated by the unprecedented catastrophe losses from Hurricane Marcus. The combined ratio improved to 88.4 percent from 112.6 percent, reflecting both the reduced catastrophe activity in 2025 and the cumulative benefit of rate increases secured over the past three renewal cycles.

Our investment portfolio generated total return of 7.2 percent on an asset base of $42 billion, with fixed income investments benefiting from the stabilization of interest rates at levels not seen in over a decade. The embedded book yield of our fixed income portfolio has increased from 2.8 percent three years ago to 4.9 percent, representing a structural improvement in recurring investment income that will persist even if market rates moderate from current levels.

Capital management was a priority during the year. We returned $2.4 billion to shareholders through dividends and share repurchases while maintaining our financial strength ratings from all major rating agencies at their current levels. Our capital position remains robust, with total shareholders' equity of $18.6 billion and estimated excess capital of $3.2 billion above the level required to support our current book of business at target return thresholds.

Looking ahead, we observe early signs of rate deceleration in certain property catastrophe markets as new capital, attracted by the sector's recent profitability, enters the market. We are prepared to reduce writings in lines where pricing no longer meets our return requirements, consistent with our longstanding commitment to underwriting discipline over premium volume. History has demonstrated that the reinsurance companies that sustain superior long-term returns are those that maintain pricing discipline through full market cycles rather than pursuing growth when margins are compressing.

## Q1. What caused the dramatic improvement in net income from 2024 to 2025?
- A. A new government subsidy program for reinsurance companies
- B. The company's decision to exit the property catastrophe business
- C. Reduced catastrophe activity, rate increases, and improved investment returns
- D. A one-time gain from the sale of a subsidiary company
- Answer: C
- Explanation: The improvement reflects "reduced catastrophe activity in 2025," "cumulative benefit of rate increases," and "total return of 7.2 percent" on investments, compared to 2024's Hurricane Marcus losses.

## Q2. What is the company's strategic response to signs of rate deceleration?
- A. Reduce writings in lines where pricing no longer meets return requirements.
- B. Aggressively increase premium volume to maintain revenue growth.
- C. Exit the reinsurance market entirely and focus on direct insurance.
- D. Acquire a competitor to gain market share.
- Answer: A
- Explanation: "We are prepared to reduce writings in lines where pricing no longer meets our return requirements, consistent with our longstanding commitment to underwriting discipline."

## Q3. What can be inferred about the cyclical nature of the reinsurance market?
- A. Reinsurance pricing is permanently fixed and never changes.
- B. The reinsurance market always becomes more profitable over time.
- C. Periods of high profitability attract new capital that compresses margins, requiring discipline to navigate effectively.
- D. New capital entering the market has no effect on pricing.
- Answer: C
- Explanation: "New capital, attracted by the sector's recent profitability, enters the market," causing rate deceleration — a cyclical pattern requiring the discipline of maintaining pricing through "full market cycles."

## Q4. The term "combined ratio" in reinsurance most likely measures:
- A. The sum of incurred losses and expenses as a percentage of earned premiums
- B. The ratio of investment income to total assets
- C. The proportion of premiums ceded to other reinsurers
- D. The number of policies written relative to claims filed
- Answer: A
- Explanation: A combined ratio of 88.4 percent (below 100) indicates profitability, while 112.6 percent indicates losses exceeding premiums — it measures total costs (losses plus expenses) relative to premium income.
