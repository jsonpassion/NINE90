# Annual ESG Performance Report — Cederholm Global Industries

Cederholm Global Industries is pleased to present its annual Environmental, Social, and Governance (ESG) Performance Report, prepared in accordance with the Global Reporting Initiative (GRI) Standards and the Sustainability Accounting Standards Board (SASB) framework. This report has been subjected to limited assurance by Ernst & Whitmore LLP, an independent third-party auditor, to enhance the credibility and reliability of the disclosed metrics.

Environmental Performance: The company achieved a 17.4% reduction in Scope 1 and Scope 2 greenhouse gas emissions relative to the 2022 baseline, surpassing the interim target of 15% established in the company's Climate Transition Plan. This reduction was accomplished primarily through the conversion of three manufacturing facilities to renewable energy sources, the implementation of advanced heat recovery systems, and the electrification of 40% of the company's commercial vehicle fleet. Scope 3 emissions, which constitute approximately 72% of the company's total carbon footprint, declined by 8.6%, reflecting progress in supplier engagement initiatives though remaining below the 12% interim target. The company has committed to achieving net-zero emissions across all scopes by 2045.

Social Performance: Employee engagement survey participation reached 89%, with an overall engagement score of 4.1 out of 5.0, representing a year-over-year improvement of 0.3 points. Gender diversity at the senior leadership level increased to 38%, advancing toward the company's 2028 target of 45%. The company invested $28 million in workforce development programs, including the launch of a comprehensive digital skills academy that enrolled over 6,200 employees across fourteen countries. Community investment initiatives totaled $15 million, directed primarily toward STEM education programs in underserved regions where the company maintains significant operational presences.

Governance Performance: The board of directors completed its annual governance effectiveness assessment, resulting in the establishment of a dedicated Sustainability Committee with direct reporting to the full board. Executive compensation structures were revised to incorporate ESG performance metrics representing 25% of annual incentive calculations, up from 15% in the prior year. The company's supply chain due diligence program was expanded to cover 92% of tier-one suppliers and 64% of tier-two suppliers, with assessments encompassing labor practices, environmental compliance, and ethical business conduct standards.

## Q1. How did Cederholm's actual emissions reduction compare to its interim targets?
- A. It fell short of targets across all emission scopes
- B. It exceeded its Scope 1 and 2 target but fell short on Scope 3
- C. It exceeded targets across all emission scopes
- D. It met its Scope 3 target but failed to reach its Scope 1 and 2 target
- Answer: B
- Explanation: The company achieved 17.4% reduction in Scope 1 and 2 (surpassing the 15% target) but Scope 3 declined only 8.6% (below the 12% interim target).

## Q2. What can be inferred about the company's approach to ESG reporting credibility?
- A. The company self-certifies all ESG metrics without external verification
- B. The company engages an independent auditor to provide assurance on reported data
- C. The company relies exclusively on industry benchmarks for its ESG disclosures
- D. The company does not follow any recognized reporting framework
- Answer: B
- Explanation: The report states it "has been subjected to limited assurance by Ernst & Whitmore LLP, an independent third-party auditor, to enhance the credibility and reliability of the disclosed metrics."

## Q3. The phrase "net-zero emissions" as used in the Environmental Performance section most likely means:
- A. Reducing all industrial activity to zero output
- B. Balancing total emissions produced with an equivalent amount removed or offset
- C. Eliminating the measurement of greenhouse gas emissions entirely
- D. Transferring emissions responsibility to supply chain partners
- Answer: B
- Explanation: "Net-zero emissions" refers to achieving a balance between the greenhouse gases emitted and those removed from the atmosphere, resulting in no net addition of emissions.
