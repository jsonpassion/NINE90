# Confidential Memorandum — Acquisition Due Diligence Summary: Thornfield BioSciences

TO: Executive Committee and Board of Directors
FROM: Corporate Development and Legal Affairs
DATE: October 28, 2025
RE: Preliminary Due Diligence Findings — Proposed Acquisition of Thornfield BioSciences Inc.

This memorandum presents a summary of preliminary due diligence findings regarding the proposed acquisition of Thornfield BioSciences Inc. ("Target") for a consideration of approximately $2.8 billion. The due diligence review covered financial performance, intellectual property, regulatory status, pending litigation, and key personnel retention risks.

Financial Overview: Thornfield reported revenue of $640 million and EBITDA of $148 million for the twelve months ended June 30, 2025, implying an acquisition multiple of approximately 18.9 times EBITDA. Revenue has grown at a compound annual rate of 22 percent over the past three years, driven primarily by the commercial success of its flagship diagnostic platform, ThoraDx, which accounts for 71 percent of total revenue. The concentration of revenue in a single product line represents a material risk factor that should be weighed against the platform's strong competitive position and expanding clinical applications.

Intellectual Property: Thornfield's IP portfolio comprises 42 granted patents and 18 pending applications across the United States, European Union, Japan, and South Korea. Our patent counsel's preliminary assessment indicates that the core ThoraDx technology patents are valid, enforceable, and provide meaningful competitive protection through 2034. However, two pending patent applications critical to the next-generation ThoraDx-2 platform face significant prior art challenges that could result in narrower claims than originally filed.

Regulatory Status: ThoraDx holds FDA 510(k) clearance for its current commercial applications and CE marking for European distribution. The ThoraDx-2 platform, currently in clinical validation, is expected to require a de novo classification pathway rather than the 510(k) route, which typically involves longer review timelines and greater regulatory uncertainty.

Litigation: Thornfield is currently defending against a patent infringement claim filed by NovaTech Diagnostics alleging that certain ThoraDx components infringe NovaTech's patents. Outside counsel's preliminary assessment rates the probability of an adverse outcome at 30 to 40 percent, with potential damages exposure of $80 to $120 million.

Key Personnel: The departure of either the Chief Scientific Officer or the VP of Regulatory Affairs within 24 months of closing would represent a significant integration risk. We recommend retention agreements with guaranteed compensation packages for both individuals.

## Q1. What is the acquisition multiple based on EBITDA?
- A. 12.4 times
- B. 18.9 times
- C. 22 times
- D. 71 times
- Answer: B
- Explanation: The memorandum states the deal implies "an acquisition multiple of approximately 18.9 times EBITDA."

## Q2. What is the most significant financial risk identified in the due diligence?
- A. Revenue concentration in a single product that accounts for 71 percent of total revenue
- B. Declining revenue growth over the past three years
- C. The company's inability to achieve profitability
- D. Excessive debt levels on Thornfield's balance sheet
- Answer: A
- Explanation: "The concentration of revenue in a single product line represents a material risk factor."

## Q4. The phrase "prior art challenges" in the IP assessment most likely means:
- A. Legal challenges filed by artists claiming copyright infringement
- B. Previously published information or inventions that may prevent patent approval
- C. Manufacturing difficulties encountered during early-stage product development
- D. Challenges in training prior employees on the new technology
- Answer: B
- Explanation: "Prior art" in patent law refers to existing knowledge or published work that may render a patent claim not novel, potentially resulting in "narrower claims than originally filed."
