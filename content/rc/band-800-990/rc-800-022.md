# Consolidated Financial Results — Third Quarter Fiscal Year 2025

Alderton Holdings PLC today announced consolidated financial results for the three months ended September 30, 2025. The Group reported revenue of £4.28 billion, representing a 6 percent increase on a reported basis and 8 percent growth on a constant currency basis compared with the prior-year quarter. The discrepancy between reported and constant currency growth reflects the adverse impact of sterling appreciation against the U.S. dollar and several emerging market currencies during the period.

Operating profit increased 11 percent to £612 million, with the operating margin expanding 70 basis points to 14.3 percent. This margin improvement was achieved despite a £38 million increase in restructuring charges related to the ongoing consolidation of the Group's European manufacturing footprint, which involves the closure of two legacy facilities in Belgium and the transfer of production to the recently expanded high-efficiency plant in Poland. Excluding restructuring charges, the adjusted operating margin reached 15.2 percent, a record level for the Group.

Segmental performance diverged materially. The Specialty Chemicals division delivered revenue growth of 14 percent and operating margins of 18.6 percent, benefiting from strong demand in the electronics and electric vehicle sectors and the favorable pricing environment for advanced materials. Conversely, the Industrial Solutions division reported a 3 percent revenue decline and margin compression of 120 basis points to 9.1 percent, reflecting weakness in the European construction sector and the loss of a significant customer contract in the Middle East that had contributed approximately £45 million in annual revenue.

Free cash flow generation was robust at £384 million, representing a conversion ratio of 63 percent of operating profit. The Group deployed £220 million in share repurchases during the quarter, reflecting management's confidence in the underlying business trajectory. Net debt to EBITDA improved to 1.4 times from 1.7 times at year-end, providing significant balance sheet capacity for strategic investments.

The Board has declared an interim dividend of 24 pence per share, an increase of 8 percent over the prior year, payable on December 15, 2025, to shareholders of record as of November 15.

## Q1. Why did reported revenue growth differ from constant currency growth?
- A. Sterling strengthened against several trading currencies, reducing the value of overseas revenues when converted.
- B. Accounting errors in the previous year's results required restatement.
- C. The Group acquired a new business that was not included in prior-year comparisons.
- D. A one-time asset sale inflated reported revenue beyond underlying growth.
- Answer: A
- Explanation: The report attributes the gap to "the adverse impact of sterling appreciation against the U.S. dollar and several emerging market currencies."

## Q2. What was the adjusted operating margin excluding restructuring charges?
- A. 9.1 percent
- B. 14.3 percent
- C. 18.6 percent
- D. 15.2 percent
- Answer: D
- Explanation: "Excluding restructuring charges, the adjusted operating margin reached 15.2 percent, a record level for the Group."

## Q3. What can be inferred about the Group's capital allocation priorities?
- A. The Group is focused exclusively on debt reduction and has ceased all shareholder returns.
- B. Management is balancing shareholder returns through dividends and buybacks while maintaining capacity for strategic investments.
- C. The Group plans to acquire the competitor that won the Middle East contract.
- D. All available cash is being invested in the European manufacturing consolidation.
- Answer: B
- Explanation: The Group deployed £220 million in buybacks, increased the dividend 8 percent, and improved its net debt ratio to 1.4x, "providing significant balance sheet capacity for strategic investments."

## Q4. The phrase "conversion ratio" in the context of free cash flow most likely refers to:
- A. The rate at which foreign currencies are exchanged into British pounds
- B. The efficiency of converting raw materials into finished products
- C. The percentage of customers who convert from trial to paid subscriptions
- D. The proportion of operating profit that is converted into actual cash generation
- Answer: D
- Explanation: Free cash flow of £384 million representing "63 percent of operating profit" describes how much of the reported profit was realized as cash.
