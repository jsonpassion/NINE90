# Joint Venture Agreement — Strategic Partnership for Advanced Battery Manufacturing

This Joint Venture Agreement ("Agreement") is executed as of September 15, 2025, by and between Consolidated Energy Systems Inc. ("CES"), a corporation organized under the laws of the State of Michigan, and Takeda Materials Technology Co., Ltd. ("TMT"), a corporation organized under the laws of Japan, for the purpose of establishing and operating a joint venture entity to be known as NextCell Advanced Batteries LLC ("Joint Venture" or "JV").

Article 2 — Purpose and Scope: The Joint Venture shall engage exclusively in the development, manufacture, and sale of solid-state lithium battery cells and modules for electric vehicle and grid-scale energy storage applications within North America. The JV shall construct and operate a manufacturing facility in Tennessee with an initial annual production capacity of 30 gigawatt-hours, expandable to 60 gigawatt-hours upon mutual agreement of the parties.

Article 4 — Capital Contributions: CES shall contribute $1.8 billion in cash and the perpetual, royalty-free license to its proprietary solid-state electrolyte technology (valued at $400 million). TMT shall contribute $1.4 billion in cash and production equipment valued at $800 million, including cathode and anode coating machinery manufactured to TMT's proprietary specifications. Each party shall hold a 50 percent equity interest in the Joint Venture regardless of the differing composition of their respective contributions.

Article 6 — Governance: The Board of Managers shall consist of six members, three appointed by each party. The Board Chairperson shall rotate annually between the parties. Decisions requiring supermajority approval (five of six votes) include: annual budgets exceeding $500 million, capital expenditures exceeding $100 million not contemplated in the approved business plan, the admission of new equity partners, amendments to the JV's scope of business, and the initiation or settlement of litigation with potential exposure exceeding $50 million.

Article 9 — Deadlock Resolution: In the event of a deadlock on any matter requiring supermajority approval that persists for more than 90 days, either party may invoke the escalation procedure set forth in Section 9.2, which requires submission of the dispute to the respective Chief Executive Officers for resolution within 60 days. If the CEO escalation fails to resolve the deadlock, either party may initiate the buy-sell mechanism described in Section 9.4, whereby the invoking party submits a binding offer to purchase the other party's interest at a specified price, and the receiving party must either accept the offer or purchase the invoking party's interest at the same price.

Article 11 — Non-Competition: During the term of this Agreement and for a period of three years following any termination or dissolution, neither party shall directly or indirectly engage in the manufacture or sale of solid-state battery cells in North America outside the Joint Venture, except for research and development activities conducted independently.

## Q1. What is the total value of both parties' combined contributions?
- A. $4.4 billion
- B. $3.2 billion
- C. $4.0 billion
- D. $2.6 billion
- Answer: A
- Explanation: CES contributes $1.8B cash + $400M technology = $2.2B; TMT contributes $1.4B cash + $800M equipment = $2.2B; total is $4.4 billion.

## Q2. What mechanism resolves governance deadlocks if CEO escalation fails?
- A. Binding arbitration before an international tribunal
- B. A vote by the Tennessee state government
- C. Automatic dissolution of the Joint Venture
- D. A buy-sell mechanism where one party must either buy or sell at the proposed price
- Answer: D
- Explanation: The buy-sell mechanism requires the invoking party to submit "a binding offer to purchase," and the receiving party "must either accept the offer or purchase the invoking party's interest at the same price."

## Q3. What can be inferred about the rationale for equal equity despite unequal cash contributions?
- A. Both parties' total contributions are valued equally at $2.2 billion, with non-cash assets balancing the cash difference.
- B. TMT insisted on equal ownership as a precondition for participating.
- C. CES agreed to a disadvantageous arrangement due to financial difficulties.
- D. Equal equity is required by law for all joint ventures involving foreign companies.
- Answer: A
- Explanation: CES contributes $2.2B ($1.8B cash + $400M IP) and TMT contributes $2.2B ($1.4B cash + $800M equipment), creating equal total contributions despite different cash amounts.

## Q4. The non-competition clause would allow either party to:
- A. Manufacture and sell solid-state batteries freely in Europe during the JV's term
- B. License the JV's technology to competitors in North America
- C. Conduct independent R&D on solid-state batteries without violating the agreement
- D. Establish a competing battery manufacturing facility in Canada
- Answer: C
- Explanation: The non-competition clause prohibits manufacturing or sales in North America "except for research and development activities conducted independently."
