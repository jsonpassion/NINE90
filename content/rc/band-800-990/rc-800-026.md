# Antitrust Scrutiny Intensifies for Technology Platform Acquisitions

Global antitrust regulators are applying increasingly rigorous scrutiny to acquisitions by dominant technology platforms, with several jurisdictions adopting new analytical frameworks that depart significantly from traditional merger review standards. The shift reflects growing concern among competition authorities that conventional market definition and competitive effects analysis may be inadequate to address the unique dynamics of digital platform markets.

The European Commission's revised Digital Markets Merger Guidelines, published in July 2025, introduce the concept of "ecosystem entrenchment" as a primary analytical framework for evaluating platform acquisitions. Under this approach, regulators assess not merely whether a proposed acquisition would reduce competition in a specific product market, but whether it would strengthen the acquiring platform's ability to leverage its existing ecosystem to foreclose competition across adjacent markets. The guidelines establish a rebuttable presumption that acquisitions by designated "gatekeepers" of companies with more than 10 million active users or annual revenue exceeding €250 million are likely to raise competitive concerns.

The United States Federal Trade Commission has adopted a complementary but distinct approach. In its September 2025 Policy Statement on Platform Acquisitions, the FTC announced that it would evaluate such transactions through the lens of "nascent competition preservation," focusing on whether acquisition targets represent potential future competitors that could disrupt the acquiring platform's market position if allowed to develop independently. This framework effectively shifts the burden of proof, requiring acquiring platforms to demonstrate that proposed transactions would not eliminate significant competitive threats.

Critics of the new regulatory approaches argue that they risk deterring beneficial acquisitions that provide startups with lucrative exit opportunities and facilitate the integration of innovative technologies into widely used platforms. They contend that the rebuttable presumption and shifted burden of proof standards create a de facto prohibition on large platform acquisitions, regardless of their competitive merits.

Proponents counter that traditional antitrust analysis has consistently failed to prevent the accumulation of market power through serial acquisitions of potential competitors, resulting in durable monopolies that reduce innovation incentives and consumer welfare over time. They argue that the new frameworks appropriately calibrate regulatory intervention to the distinctive characteristics of digital markets.

## Q1. What is the key innovation in the European Commission's revised guidelines?
- A. The introduction of "ecosystem entrenchment" as a primary analytical framework
- B. The elimination of all merger review for technology companies
- C. A requirement that all acquisitions be approved by consumer referendum
- D. The creation of a state-owned technology platform to replace private companies
- Answer: A
- Explanation: The guidelines "introduce the concept of 'ecosystem entrenchment' as a primary analytical framework for evaluating platform acquisitions."

## Q2. How does the FTC's approach differ from the European Commission's?
- A. The FTC has no interest in regulating technology acquisitions.
- B. The FTC focuses on preserving nascent competition, while the EC evaluates ecosystem entrenchment.
- C. The FTC applies stricter financial penalties than the European Commission.
- D. The FTC framework only applies to acquisitions exceeding $10 billion.
- Answer: B
- Explanation: The FTC focuses on "nascent competition preservation" — whether targets could become future competitors — while the EC evaluates ecosystem leverage across adjacent markets.

## Q3. What can be inferred about the likely impact on startup ecosystem dynamics?
- A. Startups will find it easier to sell to large technology platforms.
- B. All startups will be required to remain independent indefinitely.
- C. Venture capital investment in startups will be completely eliminated.
- D. The regulatory changes may reduce the attractiveness of acquisition as a startup exit strategy.
- Answer: D
- Explanation: Critics argue the new frameworks "risk deterring beneficial acquisitions that provide startups with lucrative exit opportunities," suggesting reduced acquisition-based exits.

## Q4. The phrase "rebuttable presumption" in the context of the EC guidelines means:
- A. An absolute prohibition that cannot be challenged or overturned
- B. A regulatory assumption that the acquisition raises concerns, which the parties can challenge with evidence
- C. A recommendation that has no legal binding force
- D. An automatic approval process for acquisitions meeting certain criteria
- Answer: B
- Explanation: A "rebuttable presumption" is a legal assumption that acquisitions meeting certain criteria are problematic, but which the acquiring party can challenge and potentially overcome by presenting contrary evidence.
