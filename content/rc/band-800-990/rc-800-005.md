# Revised Master Service Agreement — Clause 14 Amendment

The following constitutes an amendment to the Master Service Agreement ("MSA") entered into between Thornton-Blake Consulting Group ("Provider") and Meridian Pacific Holdings, Ltd. ("Client"), effective as of the first day of the calendar quarter following written acceptance by both parties. This amendment supersedes and replaces Clause 14 of the original MSA dated March 15, 2024, in its entirety.

Clause 14.1 — Limitation of Liability: Notwithstanding any provision to the contrary contained herein, the aggregate liability of the Provider arising out of or in connection with this Agreement, whether in contract, tort (including negligence), breach of statutory duty, or otherwise, shall not exceed an amount equal to one hundred and fifty percent (150%) of the total fees paid by the Client during the twelve-month period immediately preceding the event giving rise to the claim. This cap shall not apply to liabilities arising from willful misconduct, gross negligence, or breaches of confidentiality obligations set forth in Clause 9.

Clause 14.2 — Indemnification: The Provider shall indemnify, defend, and hold harmless the Client and its affiliates, officers, directors, and employees against any third-party claims, losses, damages, or expenses (including reasonable legal fees) arising directly from the Provider's material breach of its representations and warranties under Clause 7, provided that the Client promptly notifies the Provider in writing of any such claim and grants the Provider sole authority to manage the defense and any settlement negotiations.

Clause 14.3 — Consequential Damages Waiver: Neither party shall be liable to the other for any indirect, incidental, special, consequential, or punitive damages, including but not limited to loss of revenue, loss of profits, loss of business, or loss of data, regardless of the theory of liability, even if such party has been advised of the possibility of such damages. This exclusion shall not extend to damages resulting from unauthorized disclosure of proprietary information as defined in Schedule B.

## Q1. What is the primary purpose of this document?
- A. To establish a new contractual relationship between two parties
- B. To replace a specific section of an existing agreement with updated terms
- C. To terminate the Master Service Agreement in its entirety
- D. To outline the scope of consulting services to be provided
- Answer: B
- Explanation: The document explicitly states it "supersedes and replaces Clause 14 of the original MSA," indicating it is an amendment to an existing agreement rather than a new contract.

## Q2. Under Clause 14.1, in which situation does the liability cap NOT apply?
- A. When the total fees exceed a predetermined threshold
- B. When claims are filed after the twelve-month reference period
- C. When the liability stems from willful misconduct or gross negligence
- D. When the Client fails to provide written notice of the claim
- Answer: C
- Explanation: Clause 14.1 explicitly states the cap "shall not apply to liabilities arising from willful misconduct, gross negligence, or breaches of confidentiality obligations."

## Q3. The word "supersedes" in the first paragraph is closest in meaning to:
- A. supplements
- B. replaces
- C. references
- D. precedes
- Answer: B
- Explanation: "Supersedes" means to take the place of or replace something, which aligns with the context of the amendment replacing the original Clause 14.

## Q4. What condition must the Client meet to receive indemnification under Clause 14.2?
- A. The Client must pay additional fees to activate indemnification coverage
- B. The Client must resolve all claims independently before seeking reimbursement
- C. The Client must notify the Provider in writing and allow the Provider to control the defense
- D. The Client must demonstrate that the Provider acted with willful misconduct
- Answer: C
- Explanation: Clause 14.2 specifies that indemnification is "provided that the Client promptly notifies the Provider in writing of any such claim and grants the Provider sole authority to manage the defense."
