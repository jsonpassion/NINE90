# Crestfield Industries — Annual Financial Performance Review

Crestfield Industries reported consolidated revenues of $4.87 billion for the fiscal year ending December 31, representing an 8.3% increase over the prior year. This growth was driven predominantly by the company's Advanced Materials Division, which contributed $1.92 billion in revenue — a 14.6% year-over-year increase attributable to heightened demand in the aerospace and semiconductor sectors. The Infrastructure Solutions Division, by contrast, experienced a modest decline of 2.1%, generating $1.68 billion, largely due to project deferrals in the Middle Eastern and Southeast Asian markets.

Operating income rose to $612 million, yielding an operating margin of 12.6%, compared to 11.4% in the preceding fiscal year. The margin expansion was facilitated by the successful implementation of the company's operational efficiency program, designated "Apex 2025," which delivered approximately $85 million in annualized cost savings through supply chain optimization and the consolidation of manufacturing facilities in the Midwestern United States. However, these gains were partially offset by a $47 million increase in research and development expenditures, reflecting the company's strategic commitment to next-generation polymer composites and sustainable material technologies.

Net income attributable to common shareholders was $389 million, or $5.72 per diluted share, compared to $341 million, or $4.98 per diluted share, in the prior year. The effective tax rate declined to 22.8% from 24.1%, primarily as a result of research and development tax credits and the favorable resolution of certain international tax disputes. Free cash flow generation remained robust at $478 million, enabling the company to reduce net debt by $220 million while simultaneously returning $195 million to shareholders through dividends and share repurchases.

Looking ahead, management has issued guidance for the forthcoming fiscal year projecting revenue growth of 5% to 7%, with operating margins anticipated to expand by an additional 40 to 60 basis points as the full benefits of the Apex 2025 program are realized.

- Answer: B
- Explanation: The passage states growth was "driven predominantly by the company's Advanced Materials Division," which saw a 14.6% year-over-year increase.
