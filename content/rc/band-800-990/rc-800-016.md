# Cross-Border M&A Activity Surges Amid Shifting Antitrust Paradigms

The global mergers and acquisitions landscape has experienced a notable resurgence in the first quarter, with aggregate deal value reaching $1.2 trillion — a 34% increase over the same period last year and the highest quarterly figure since the pre-pandemic peak. Remarkably, cross-border transactions accounted for 58% of total deal value, driven by strategic imperatives ranging from technology capability acquisition to geographic diversification and vertical supply chain integration.

However, this surge in dealmaking is occurring against a backdrop of increasingly assertive antitrust enforcement across multiple jurisdictions. Regulatory authorities in the United States, European Union, and several Asian economies have signaled a willingness to scrutinize transactions more rigorously and to challenge deals that they perceive as potentially anticompetitive, even when the acquiring and target entities operate in ostensibly distinct market segments. The US Federal Trade Commission, for instance, has expanded its analytical framework to evaluate not merely traditional market concentration metrics but also the potential for acquisitions to entrench dominant platforms, eliminate nascent competitors, or accumulate aggregations of data that confer durable competitive advantages.

This heightened scrutiny has introduced significant uncertainty into deal timelines and completion probabilities. According to data compiled by Mergerstat, the average regulatory review period for transactions exceeding $5 billion has increased from 7.2 months to 11.4 months over the past three years, and the proportion of proposed transactions ultimately abandoned due to regulatory opposition has risen from 4.8% to 8.3%. These dynamics have compelled acquirers to adopt more sophisticated regulatory risk assessment methodologies and to incorporate reverse break-up fees and regulatory condition provisions into transaction agreements with greater frequency.

Despite these headwinds, seasoned dealmakers contend that the fundamental strategic rationale for transformative acquisitions remains compelling. Victoria Chen, Global Head of M&A at Berenson Capital Partners, observes that "the companies that will define the next decade of their respective industries are those with the foresight and conviction to pursue strategically accretive acquisitions today, even if the regulatory path to completion has become more circuitous and demanding."

## Q1. What is the main trend described in this article?
- A. A decline in global M&A activity due to economic recession
- B. Increased cross-border deal activity coinciding with more aggressive antitrust enforcement
- C. The elimination of regulatory oversight for international transactions
- D. A shift away from cross-border transactions toward domestic consolidation
- Answer: B
- Explanation: The article describes a "notable resurgence" in M&A with cross-border deals at 58% of total value, occurring "against a backdrop of increasingly assertive antitrust enforcement across multiple jurisdictions."

## Q2. How has the FTC's approach to evaluating acquisitions evolved?
- A. It now focuses exclusively on traditional market share analysis
- B. It has reduced its scrutiny of technology sector transactions
- C. It now considers factors such as platform entrenchment, nascent competitor elimination, and data accumulation
- D. It has delegated all review authority to individual state regulators
- Answer: C
- Explanation: The FTC "has expanded its analytical framework to evaluate not merely traditional market concentration metrics but also the potential for acquisitions to entrench dominant platforms, eliminate nascent competitors, or accumulate aggregations of data."

## Q3. The word "circuitous" as used by Victoria Chen is closest in meaning to:
- A. straightforward
- B. impossible
- C. indirect and lengthy
- D. voluntary
- Answer: C
- Explanation: "Circuitous" means longer than the most direct way, roundabout or indirect. Chen acknowledges the regulatory path has become "more circuitous and demanding," meaning more complicated and time-consuming.

- Explanation: The article states these dynamics "have compelled acquirers to... incorporate reverse break-up fees and regulatory condition provisions into transaction agreements with greater frequency."
