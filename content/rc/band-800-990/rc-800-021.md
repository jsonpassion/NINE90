# Technology Licensing Agreement — Exclusive Rights and Performance Obligations

This Technology Licensing Agreement ("Agreement") is entered into as of November 1, 2025, by and between Quantum Dynamics Corporation ("Licensor"), a Delaware corporation with principal offices at 4200 Innovation Drive, San Jose, California, and Meridian Applied Sciences GmbH ("Licensee"), a company incorporated under the laws of Germany with registered offices in Munich.

WHEREAS, Licensor is the sole owner of certain proprietary semiconductor fabrication processes, including but not limited to the QD-NanoEtch Process and the QD-ThinFilm Deposition Method, protected under United States patents 11,482,301 through 11,482,309 and corresponding international filings (collectively, the "Licensed Technology"); and WHEREAS, Licensee desires to obtain an exclusive license to utilize the Licensed Technology for the manufacture and sale of semiconductor components in the European Economic Area ("Territory");

NOW, THEREFORE, in consideration of the mutual covenants and obligations set forth herein, the parties agree as follows. Article 3 — License Grant: Licensor hereby grants to Licensee an exclusive, non-transferable license to use the Licensed Technology within the Territory for a period of ten years from the Effective Date, subject to the performance obligations specified in Article 5. The exclusivity of this license is conditioned upon Licensee's continuous satisfaction of the minimum performance thresholds established herein; failure to meet such thresholds for two consecutive fiscal quarters shall entitle Licensor to convert the license to a non-exclusive basis upon ninety days' written notice.

Article 5 — Performance Obligations: Licensee shall achieve the following milestones: (a) commence commercial production utilizing the Licensed Technology within eighteen months of the Effective Date; (b) generate minimum annual royalty payments of €8 million beginning in the third year of the Agreement; and (c) invest not less than €15 million in research and development activities directly related to the advancement of the Licensed Technology over the initial five-year period. Article 7 — Royalties: Licensee shall pay Licensor a royalty of 4.5 percent of net sales revenue derived from products manufactured using the Licensed Technology, payable quarterly within thirty days of each fiscal quarter end.

Article 12 — Termination: Either party may terminate this Agreement upon material breach by the other party that remains uncured for sixty days following written notice specifying the nature of the breach. Upon termination, Licensee shall cease all use of the Licensed Technology within ninety days and shall destroy or return all proprietary technical documentation.

## Q1. Under what condition can the exclusive license be converted to non-exclusive?
- A. If the Licensee requests a reduction in royalty payments
- B. If the Licensee fails to meet minimum performance thresholds for two consecutive quarters
- C. If a competitor offers the Licensor more favorable terms
- D. If the Licensed Technology becomes obsolete within the Territory
- Answer: B
- Explanation: The Agreement states "failure to meet such thresholds for two consecutive fiscal quarters shall entitle Licensor to convert the license to a non-exclusive basis."

## Q2. What is the minimum R&D investment required over the first five years?
- A. €15 million
- B. €8 million
- C. €12 million
- D. €4.5 million
- Answer: A
- Explanation: The Licensee must "invest not less than €15 million in research and development activities directly related to the advancement of the Licensed Technology over the initial five-year period."

## Q3. What can be inferred about the Licensor's strategic intent regarding the performance obligations?
- A. The Licensor is primarily concerned with minimizing its own R&D expenditures.
- B. The Licensor expects the Licensee to sublicense the technology to third parties.
- C. The Licensor intends to terminate the agreement as soon as possible.
- D. The Licensor has structured obligations to ensure the Licensee actively commercializes and advances the technology rather than merely holding exclusive rights.
- Answer: D
- Explanation: The milestones requiring commercial production, minimum royalties, and R&D investment collectively ensure the Licensee actively exploits the technology rather than sitting on exclusive rights, with conversion to non-exclusive as the consequence for inactivity.

## Q4. The phrase "remains uncured" in Article 12 most likely means:
- A. The violation has not been corrected or remedied within the specified period
- B. The breaching party has been unable to find a medical remedy
- C. The breach has been acknowledged but not yet investigated
- D. The non-breaching party has waived its right to terminate
- Answer: A
- Explanation: In legal context, "uncured" means a breach that has not been corrected or remedied; the sixty-day period gives the breaching party an opportunity to fix the problem before termination can proceed.
