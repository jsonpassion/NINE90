# Algorithmic Trading Governance Framework

ALGORITHMIC AND HIGH-FREQUENCY TRADING GOVERNANCE FRAMEWORK — RISK CONTROLS AND REGULATORY COMPLIANCE

Prepared by the Trading Risk Committee for the Board Risk Oversight Subcommittee

This framework establishes the governance structure, risk control requirements, and regulatory compliance obligations applicable to all algorithmic and high-frequency trading ("HFT") activities conducted by the firm and its subsidiaries across global execution venues. The framework has been developed to satisfy the requirements of the Markets in Financial Instruments Directive II ("MiFID II") in the European Union, the SEC's Regulation Systems Compliance and Integrity ("Reg SCI") in the United States, and comparable regulatory frameworks in the Asia-Pacific jurisdictions where the firm operates.

PRE-TRADE RISK CONTROLS: All algorithmic trading strategies must pass through a mandatory multi-layer pre-trade risk control framework before any order is transmitted to an execution venue. Layer 1 controls operate at the individual strategy level, enforcing position limits, order size limits, and price deviation thresholds calibrated to each strategy's historical operating parameters. Layer 2 controls operate at the desk level, monitoring aggregate exposure across all strategies assigned to a trading desk and enforcing desk-level net and gross position limits. Layer 3 controls operate at the firm level, monitoring total firm exposure across all asset classes and enforcing absolute exposure ceilings linked to the firm's regulatory capital. Each layer must be capable of automatically canceling all outstanding orders and unwinding positions if a control threshold is breached — a capability referred to as a "kill switch" — with a maximum activation latency of 50 milliseconds.

ALGORITHM APPROVAL AND CHANGE MANAGEMENT: No algorithmic trading strategy shall be deployed in a production environment without the documented approval of the Algorithm Approval Committee, comprising the Head of Quantitative Research, the Chief Risk Officer, the Head of Technology, and a representative of the Compliance Department. The approval process requires submission of a comprehensive strategy document including the mathematical model specification, backtesting results covering at least five years of historical data or three years for strategies in asset classes with limited data history, stress test results under a minimum of 12 predefined market scenarios, and a detailed assessment of the strategy's potential market impact and contribution to systemic risk.

REAL-TIME MONITORING AND CIRCUIT BREAKERS: The firm's real-time surveillance system shall monitor all algorithmic trading activity for anomalous behavior patterns, including but not limited to: order-to-trade ratios exceeding 10:1, rapid position reversals within defined time windows, and order patterns that may constitute market manipulation under applicable regulations. Firm-level circuit breakers shall automatically suspend all algorithmic trading activity if aggregate daily profit-and-loss exceeds a loss of $25 million, if total order volume exceeds three standard deviations above the trailing 30-day average, or if execution venue connectivity latency exceeds 500 milliseconds, which may indicate network instability that could cause erroneous order execution.

POST-TRADE REVIEW AND REGULATORY REPORTING: All algorithmic trading activity shall be subject to end-of-day review by the Trading Surveillance team, with comprehensive reports submitted to the Chief Risk Officer. Any identified anomalies must be escalated within two hours. Records of all orders, including canceled and modified orders, must be retained for a minimum of seven years in a format that enables reconstruction of the complete order lifecycle in response to regulatory inquiries.

## Q1. What is the maximum activation latency for the kill switch mechanism?
- A. 10 milliseconds
- B. 50 milliseconds
- C. 100 milliseconds
- D. 500 milliseconds
- Answer: B
- Explanation: The kill switch must have 'a maximum activation latency of 50 milliseconds.'

## Q2. What does the Algorithm Approval Committee require before deployment?
- A. Only a mathematical model specification and backtesting results
- B. A comprehensive document including model specification, backtesting (minimum 5 years), stress tests (12 scenarios), and market impact assessment
- C. A simple risk-reward analysis and management approval
- D. Backtesting results and a 30-day pilot trading period
- Answer: B
- Explanation: Approval requires 'mathematical model specification, backtesting results covering at least five years, stress test results under a minimum of 12 predefined market scenarios, and market impact assessment.'

## Q3. What triggers a firm-level circuit breaker?
- A. Any single trade resulting in a loss exceeding $1 million
- B. An aggregate daily loss exceeding $25 million, excessive order volume, or venue connectivity latency exceeding 500 milliseconds
- C. A regulatory inquiry about the firm's trading activities
- D. Client requests to suspend trading during market volatility
- Answer: B
- Explanation: Circuit breakers activate if 'daily profit-and-loss exceeds a loss of $25 million, total order volume exceeds three standard deviations above average, or connectivity latency exceeds 500 milliseconds.'

## Q4. How long must order records be retained?
- A. Three years
- B. Five years
- C. Seven years
- D. Ten years
- Answer: C
- Explanation: Records must be 'retained for a minimum of seven years.'
