# Private Equity Fund Governance

LIMITED PARTNERSHIP AGREEMENT — GOVERNANCE AND INVESTOR PROTECTION PROVISIONS (EXCERPTED)

Meridian Growth Partners Fund IV, L.P.

ARTICLE VII — ADVISORY COMMITTEE AND INVESTOR GOVERNANCE RIGHTS

Section 7.1: The General Partner shall establish an Advisory Committee composed of representatives of Limited Partners whose aggregate Capital Commitments equal or exceed $75 million individually. The Advisory Committee shall have no fewer than five and no more than nine members, appointed for the duration of the Fund's term. Advisory Committee members shall serve without compensation from the Fund but shall be reimbursed for reasonable expenses incurred in connection with Committee activities.

Section 7.2: The Advisory Committee shall be consulted, and its prior approval obtained, with respect to the following matters: (a) any transaction between the Fund and any Related Person of the General Partner, as defined in Section 1.28; (b) any material modification to the Fund's valuation policies or methodologies; (c) any extension of the Investment Period beyond the initial five-year term; (d) the allocation of co-investment opportunities among Limited Partners when demand exceeds the available allocation; and (e) any waiver of the Key Person Provisions set forth in Section 8.4.

Section 7.3: KEY PERSON PROVISIONS — The continued active involvement of Marcus Reiner (Managing Partner) and Elena Vasquez (Chief Investment Officer) is deemed material to the Fund's investment strategy and operations. If either Key Person ceases to devote substantially all of their professional time to Fund activities for any reason ("Key Person Event"), the Investment Period shall be automatically suspended. During the suspension, no new portfolio investments may be made, and the General Partner shall have 120 days to propose a remediation plan to the Advisory Committee. If the Advisory Committee does not approve a remediation plan within the 120-day period, the Investment Period shall terminate, and the Fund shall enter its harvesting phase. Existing portfolio investments shall continue to be managed in accordance with the General Partner's fiduciary obligations during the harvesting phase.

Section 7.4: DISTRIBUTION WATERFALL — Distributions of available cash shall be made in the following order of priority: (a) first, 100 percent to Limited Partners until they have received cumulative distributions equal to their Capital Contributions; (b) second, 100 percent to Limited Partners until they have received a Preferred Return equal to an annualized internal rate of return of 8 percent on their unreturned Capital Contributions, compounded quarterly; (c) third, 80 percent to the General Partner and 20 percent to Limited Partners until the General Partner has received cumulative distributions equal to 20 percent of cumulative profits distributed under clauses (b) and (c) (the "GP Catch-Up"); (d) thereafter, 80 percent to Limited Partners and 20 percent to the General Partner (the "Carried Interest").

Section 7.5: CLAWBACK — In the event that the General Partner has received cumulative Carried Interest distributions in excess of 20 percent of the Fund's aggregate net profits as calculated upon final liquidation, the General Partner shall return such excess to the Fund within 90 days of the final distribution. This clawback obligation is secured by a letter of credit equal to 50 percent of cumulative Carried Interest distributions, maintained at a financial institution with a credit rating of not less than A by Standard & Poor's.

## Q1. What triggers a suspension of the Investment Period?
- A. A decline in portfolio valuations exceeding 20 percent
- B. A Key Person ceasing to devote substantially all professional time to Fund activities
- C. A vote by a majority of Limited Partners
- D. The Fund reaching its maximum investment capacity
- Answer: B
- Explanation: A Key Person Event — either Key Person ceasing to devote substantially all professional time — causes the Investment Period to be 'automatically suspended.'

## Q2. What is the Preferred Return for Limited Partners?
- A. 5 percent annualized, compounded annually
- B. 8 percent annualized, compounded quarterly
- C. 10 percent annualized, compounded semi-annually
- D. 12 percent annualized, compounded monthly
- Answer: B
- Explanation: The Preferred Return equals 'an annualized internal rate of return of 8 percent on their unreturned Capital Contributions, compounded quarterly.'

## Q3. What secures the General Partner's clawback obligation?
- A. A personal guarantee from the Managing Partner
- B. Escrowed fund assets equal to the clawback amount
- C. A letter of credit equal to 50 percent of cumulative Carried Interest distributions
- D. An insurance policy purchased by the Fund
- Answer: C
- Explanation: The clawback is 'secured by a letter of credit equal to 50 percent of cumulative Carried Interest distributions.'

## Q4. Which matter requires Advisory Committee approval?
- A. Day-to-day portfolio management decisions
- B. Transactions between the Fund and any Related Person of the General Partner
- C. The General Partner's selection of legal counsel
- D. Individual investment decisions within approved parameters
- Answer: B
- Explanation: The Advisory Committee's prior approval must be obtained for 'any transaction between the Fund and any Related Person of the General Partner.'
