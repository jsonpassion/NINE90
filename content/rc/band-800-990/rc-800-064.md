# Intellectual Property Licensing Framework

TECHNOLOGY LICENSING AND INTELLECTUAL PROPERTY RIGHTS AGREEMENT — MASTER FRAMEWORK

This Master Technology Licensing Agreement ("Agreement") is executed as of February 1, 2026, between Quantum Dynamics Corporation ("Licensor"), a Delaware corporation and the sole owner of the proprietary technologies enumerated in Schedule A, and Apex Manufacturing Solutions, Inc. ("Licensee"), a corporation organized under the laws of the State of Michigan.

GRANT OF LICENSE: The Licensor hereby grants to the Licensee a non-exclusive, non-transferable, revocable license to use, reproduce, and create derivative works based upon the Licensed Technology (as defined in Schedule A) solely for the purpose of manufacturing the products specified in Exhibit B (the "Authorized Products") within the territory of the United States, Canada, and Mexico (the "Licensed Territory"). The Licensee shall not sublicense, assign, or otherwise transfer any rights granted hereunder to any third party without the prior written consent of the Licensor, which consent may be withheld in the Licensor's sole and absolute discretion.

ROYALTY STRUCTURE: In consideration of the rights granted herein, the Licensee shall pay to the Licensor a royalty equal to 4.5 percent of Net Sales Revenue attributable to the Authorized Products. For purposes of this Agreement, "Net Sales Revenue" is defined as gross revenue from sales of Authorized Products less applicable returns, allowances, shipping charges, and sales taxes. A minimum annual royalty of $2.8 million shall be payable regardless of actual sales volume, with the minimum applied as a credit against earned royalties. Royalty payments shall be made quarterly within 45 days of each quarter-end, accompanied by a detailed royalty report certified by the Licensee's chief financial officer.

INTELLECTUAL PROPERTY PROTECTION: The Licensee acknowledges that all intellectual property rights in the Licensed Technology, including all patents, trade secrets, copyrights, and know-how, remain the exclusive property of the Licensor. Any improvements, modifications, or derivative works developed by the Licensee using or based upon the Licensed Technology ("Improvements") shall be owned jointly by the parties, with the Licensor retaining an irrevocable, worldwide, royalty-free license to use such Improvements for any purpose. The Licensee shall implement commercially reasonable security measures to protect the confidentiality of the Licensed Technology, including restricting access to employees with a demonstrable need-to-know and requiring all such employees to execute confidentiality agreements conforming to the standards set forth in Exhibit C.

AUDIT RIGHTS AND REMEDIES: The Licensor shall have the right, upon 30 days' written notice, to audit the Licensee's books, records, and accounting systems relevant to the calculation of royalties. If any audit reveals an underpayment of royalties exceeding 5 percent of the amount actually due for the audited period, the Licensee shall bear the full cost of the audit and shall pay interest on the underpaid amount at a rate of SOFR plus 4 percent from the date such payment was originally due. Persistent underpayment, defined as underpayment exceeding 5 percent in two consecutive audit periods, shall constitute a material breach entitling the Licensor to terminate this Agreement immediately.

TERM AND TERMINATION: This Agreement shall have an initial term of seven years, with automatic renewal for successive two-year periods unless either party provides written notice of non-renewal at least 180 days before the expiration of the then-current term.

## Q1. What type of license is granted to the Licensee?
- A. An exclusive, worldwide, perpetual license
- B. A non-exclusive, non-transferable, revocable license limited to a defined territory
- C. An irrevocable sublicensable right covering all product categories
- D. A temporary evaluation license for research purposes only
- Answer: B
- Explanation: The Agreement grants 'a non-exclusive, non-transferable, revocable license' within 'the United States, Canada, and Mexico.'

## Q2. How is the minimum annual royalty applied?
- A. It is paid in addition to earned royalties.
- B. It replaces the percentage-based royalty structure entirely.
- C. It serves as a credit against earned royalties, ensuring a floor payment.
- D. It is refunded if earned royalties exceed the minimum.
- Answer: C
- Explanation: The minimum annual royalty of $2.8 million is 'payable regardless of actual sales volume, with the minimum applied as a credit against earned royalties.'

## Q3. Who owns improvements developed by the Licensee?
- A. Exclusively the Licensee
- B. Exclusively the Licensor
- C. Jointly, with the Licensor retaining a royalty-free license to use them
- D. A neutral third-party trust
- Answer: C
- Explanation: Improvements 'shall be owned jointly by the parties, with the Licensor retaining an irrevocable, worldwide, royalty-free license to use such Improvements.'

## Q4. What constitutes a material breach related to royalty payments?
- A. Any single late payment regardless of amount
- B. Underpayment exceeding 5 percent in two consecutive audit periods
- C. Failure to submit quarterly royalty reports
- D. Total royalties falling below the minimum annual amount
- Answer: B
- Explanation: Persistent underpayment, defined as 'underpayment exceeding 5 percent in two consecutive audit periods, shall constitute a material breach.'
