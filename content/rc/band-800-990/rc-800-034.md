# Annual Letter to Limited Partners — Crestview Capital Partners Fund VI

Dear Limited Partners,

I am writing to provide you with a comprehensive review of Fund VI's performance and activity during fiscal year 2025. This was a year of significant portfolio development, marked by two new platform investments, three strategic add-on acquisitions, and the successful exit of one portfolio company.

Fund VI has deployed $3.2 billion of its $4.5 billion in committed capital across eight platform investments since its inception in 2023. The portfolio's aggregate enterprise value has grown from $7.8 billion at acquisition to an estimated $10.4 billion as of September 30, 2025, reflecting a gross multiple on invested capital (MOIC) of 1.42 times and a gross internal rate of return (IRR) of 24 percent. These metrics are consistent with or exceed the performance of our prior funds at comparable stages of their investment periods.

Our most notable achievement during the year was the exit of Lighthouse Healthcare Services, which we acquired in 2023 for $680 million and sold to a strategic buyer for $1.12 billion, representing a 1.65 times MOIC and a gross IRR of 31 percent. The value creation was driven by a combination of revenue growth through geographic expansion into three new states, margin improvement through operational efficiency initiatives, and multiple expansion resulting from the broader market re-rating of healthcare services companies during the period.

Among active portfolio companies, Pinnacle Industrial Technologies has been our strongest performer, with EBITDA growing 38 percent since acquisition through a combination of organic growth and two bolt-on acquisitions that expanded its product offering into adjacent industrial markets. Conversely, Atlantic Consumer Brands has underperformed our initial investment thesis, with revenue growth of only 4 percent against our base case projection of 12 percent. The underperformance is attributable to intensified private-label competition and a slower-than-anticipated consumer spending recovery. We have installed a new management team and are executing a revised operating plan focused on product innovation and digital commerce capabilities.

Looking ahead, we intend to deploy the remaining $1.3 billion of committed capital over the next 18 to 24 months, with a focus on the business services, healthcare, and technology-enabled industrial sectors where we see the most attractive risk-adjusted return opportunities.

Sincerely,
Richard Alderton
Managing Partner, Crestview Capital Partners

## Q1. What is Fund VI's current gross MOIC?
- A. 1.42 times
- B. 1.12 times
- C. 1.65 times
- D. 2.4 times
- Answer: A
- Explanation: "The portfolio's gross multiple on invested capital (MOIC) of 1.42 times."

## Q2. What drove value creation in the Lighthouse Healthcare exit?
- A. Revenue growth, margin improvement, and multiple expansion from market conditions
- B. Cost reduction through workforce downsizing exclusively
- C. A government subsidy that inflated the company's valuation
- D. A bidding war between multiple private equity buyers
- Answer: A
- Explanation: Value creation was driven by "revenue growth through geographic expansion, margin improvement through operational efficiency initiatives, and multiple expansion resulting from the broader market re-rating."

## Q3. What can be inferred about Crestview's approach to underperforming investments?
- A. They immediately sell companies that fall below projections.
- B. They merge underperforming companies with successful portfolio companies.
- C. They accept underperformance and wait for market conditions to improve.
- D. They take active operational measures including management changes to improve performance.
- Answer: D
- Explanation: For underperforming Atlantic Consumer Brands, they "installed a new management team" and are "executing a revised operating plan."

## Q4. The term "bolt-on acquisitions" most likely refers to:
- A. The sale of a portfolio company's non-core assets
- B. Government contracts awarded to portfolio companies
- C. The initial purchase of a platform company by the private equity fund
- D. Smaller acquisitions made by portfolio companies to expand their capabilities or market presence
- Answer: D
- Explanation: Pinnacle's "two bolt-on acquisitions expanded its product offering into adjacent industrial markets," indicating smaller acquisitions that complement and enhance an existing platform company.
