# Long-Term Supply Agreement — Critical Raw Materials

This Long-Term Supply Agreement ("Agreement") is entered into as of October 15, 2025, by and between Nordland Mining Corporation ("Supplier"), a Norwegian corporation, and Kensington Battery Technologies Ltd. ("Buyer"), a United Kingdom corporation, for the supply of battery-grade lithium hydroxide.

Article 2 — Supply Commitment: Supplier agrees to deliver to Buyer 12,000 metric tons of battery-grade lithium hydroxide per annum for a period of ten years commencing January 1, 2026, with volumes escalating by 8 percent annually from the third year through the tenth year. Buyer agrees to purchase not less than 85 percent of the contracted annual volume in each year ("Minimum Offtake Obligation"). Failure to meet the Minimum Offtake Obligation shall obligate Buyer to pay a shortfall penalty calculated at 30 percent of the contract price multiplied by the volume deficit.

Article 4 — Pricing: The base price for the initial year shall be $24,500 per metric ton, adjusted annually by a formula comprising 40 percent linked to the London Metal Exchange lithium hydroxide reference price, 30 percent linked to the European Producer Price Index, and 30 percent fixed. This hybrid pricing structure is intended to balance the parties' respective exposure to market price volatility while providing a degree of cost predictability for both producer and consumer.

Article 6 — Quality Specifications: All delivered material must conform to the quality specifications set forth in Appendix B, including a minimum lithium hydroxide purity of 99.5 percent, maximum moisture content of 0.5 percent, and specific limits on iron, sodium, calcium, and other metallic impurities. Buyer shall have the right to inspect and test each delivery within 15 business days of receipt. Material failing to meet specifications may be rejected, and Supplier shall replace rejected material at its own cost within 30 days.

Article 8 — Force Majeure: Neither party shall be liable for failure to perform its obligations under this Agreement to the extent that such failure results from causes beyond its reasonable control, including but not limited to natural disasters, governmental actions, war, sanctions, or pandemic-related restrictions, provided that the affected party notifies the other party within 72 hours and uses commercially reasonable efforts to mitigate the impact. Force majeure events exceeding 180 consecutive days shall entitle either party to terminate the Agreement without penalty.

Article 10 — Exclusivity and Right of First Refusal: During the term of this Agreement, Supplier grants Buyer a right of first refusal on any additional battery-grade lithium hydroxide production capacity developed at Supplier's Norwegian facilities, on terms no less favorable than those offered to any third party.

## Q1. What happens if the Buyer does not purchase the minimum required volume?
- A. The Agreement is automatically terminated.
- B. The Supplier may sell the excess to any other buyer without notice.
- C. The Buyer must pay a shortfall penalty of 30 percent of the contract price times the volume deficit.
- D. The Buyer receives a credit applied to the following year's purchases.
- Answer: C
- Explanation: "Failure to meet the Minimum Offtake Obligation shall obligate Buyer to pay a shortfall penalty calculated at 30 percent of the contract price multiplied by the volume deficit."

## Q2. How is the annual price adjustment calculated?
- A. A blend of 40 percent market price, 30 percent producer price index, and 30 percent fixed
- B. Entirely based on the London Metal Exchange reference price
- C. Negotiated annually between the parties without a formula
- D. Fixed at $24,500 for the entire ten-year period
- Answer: A
- Explanation: The price is "40 percent linked to the LME lithium hydroxide reference price, 30 percent linked to the European Producer Price Index, and 30 percent fixed."

## Q3. What can be inferred about the strategic importance of this agreement to the Buyer?
- A. Lithium hydroxide is a minor input with many readily available substitute materials.
- B. The Buyer is purchasing the material solely for resale at a profit.
- C. The Buyer is entering the mining industry and plans to produce its own lithium.
- D. Securing long-term supply of this critical raw material is essential for the Buyer's battery manufacturing operations.
- Answer: D
- Explanation: A ten-year commitment with escalating volumes, minimum offtake obligations, and right of first refusal indicates the material is strategically critical to the Buyer's operations.

## Q4. What is the threshold for force majeure termination?
- A. 30 days of continuous inability to perform
- B. 72 hours after notification
- C. One full year of affected operations
- D. 180 consecutive days of force majeure event
- Answer: D
- Explanation: "Force majeure events exceeding 180 consecutive days shall entitle either party to terminate the Agreement without penalty."
