# Compliance Notification — Enhanced Anti-Money Laundering Obligations

TO: All Regulated Entities Operating Under the Jurisdiction of the Financial Conduct Authority
FROM: Compliance Enforcement Division, Financial Conduct Authority
DATE: March 15, 2026

This notification serves to inform all regulated entities of material amendments to the Anti-Money Laundering (AML) Regulations that will become effective on July 1, 2026. These amendments reflect the Authority's commitment to strengthening the integrity of the financial system and are informed by the recommendations of the Financial Action Task Force's most recent mutual evaluation of the United Kingdom's AML framework.

Enhanced Customer Due Diligence (ECDD): Under the revised regulations, the threshold for mandatory enhanced due diligence has been expanded to encompass all commercial relationships involving politically exposed persons (PEPs), their family members, and known close associates, regardless of the assessed risk level. Previously, enhanced due diligence for domestic PEPs was required only when the overall business relationship was determined to present a higher risk. This change eliminates the discretionary element and establishes an absolute obligation for enhanced scrutiny in all PEP-related engagements.

Beneficial Ownership Transparency: Regulated entities are now required to verify the identity of all ultimate beneficial owners (UBOs) holding a direct or indirect ownership interest of 10% or more in any corporate client, reduced from the previous threshold of 25%. Verification must be conducted using at least two independent, reliable sources, and the resulting documentation must be retained for a minimum of seven years following the termination of the business relationship. The Authority recognizes that this lower threshold will significantly increase the volume of verification activities required and has accordingly established a six-month transitional period for existing client portfolios.

Transaction Monitoring: All regulated entities must implement automated transaction monitoring systems capable of detecting patterns indicative of layering, structuring, and trade-based money laundering. These systems must generate alerts based on dynamic, risk-calibrated parameters rather than static thresholds, and must be subject to annual model validation by an independent qualified party. Entities currently relying exclusively on manual monitoring processes must submit a remediation plan to the Authority within ninety days of the effective date.

Non-compliance with these amended regulations may result in enforcement actions including monetary penalties, public censure, and restrictions on business activities.

## Q1. What is the main change regarding due diligence for politically exposed persons?
- A. PEPs are no longer subject to any enhanced scrutiny requirements
- B. Enhanced due diligence is now mandatory for all PEP relationships regardless of assessed risk
- C. Only foreign PEPs require enhanced due diligence under the new rules
- D. Enhanced due diligence is required only for PEPs in the banking sector
- Answer: B
- Explanation: The notification states ECDD now encompasses "all commercial relationships involving politically exposed persons... regardless of the assessed risk level," eliminating the previous discretionary approach.

## Q2. How has the beneficial ownership verification threshold changed?
- A. It has been increased from 10% to 25%
- B. It has been eliminated entirely
- C. It has been reduced from 25% to 10%
- D. It remains unchanged at 25%
- Answer: C
- Explanation: The notification states entities must verify UBOs "holding a direct or indirect ownership interest of 10% or more, reduced from the previous threshold of 25%."

## Q3. What can be inferred about the Authority's approach to the implementation timeline?
- A. The Authority expects immediate compliance with no transitional accommodations
- B. The Authority has provided some flexibility for existing clients while maintaining firm deadlines for system upgrades
- C. The Authority has indefinitely postponed the effective date
- D. The Authority allows each entity to set its own compliance timeline
- Answer: B
- Explanation: A six-month transitional period is provided for existing client portfolios (beneficial ownership), while entities using manual monitoring must submit remediation plans within ninety days, showing targeted flexibility within firm deadlines.

## Q4. The phrase "dynamic, risk-calibrated parameters" in the Transaction Monitoring section most likely describes:
- A. Fixed numerical thresholds that remain constant over time
- B. Monitoring criteria that adjust based on evolving risk assessments and patterns
- C. Manual review processes conducted by compliance officers
- D. Industry-wide standardized detection rules
- Answer: B
- Explanation: "Dynamic, risk-calibrated parameters" are contrasted with "static thresholds," indicating monitoring criteria that adapt and adjust based on changing risk profiles and patterns rather than remaining fixed.
