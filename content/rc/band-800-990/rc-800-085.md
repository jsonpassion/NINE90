# Restructuring the Global Operations Division: Strategic Rationale and Implementation Timeline

To: Executive Leadership Team
From: Jonathan Price, Chief Operating Officer
Date: April 22

This memorandum outlines the proposed restructuring of the Global Operations Division, which has been under review since the completion of our strategic planning process in January. The restructuring is designed to address three interconnected challenges: redundant administrative functions across regional offices, inconsistent service delivery standards, and the need to accelerate decision-making in response to rapidly changing market conditions.

Under the proposed structure, the current four regional offices in New York, London, Singapore, and São Paulo will be consolidated into two operational hubs: one in London serving Europe, the Middle East, and Africa, and one in Singapore covering the Asia-Pacific and Americas regions. The New York and São Paulo offices will be retained as client-facing satellite locations but will no longer house operational management functions. This consolidation is projected to reduce annual operating costs by approximately $8.5 million while enabling more consistent global process standardization.

The restructuring will affect approximately 85 positions across the division. Of these, an estimated 50 roles will be eliminated through a combination of natural attrition and voluntary separation packages. The remaining 35 positions will be redesigned to align with the new operational model, with affected employees given priority consideration for the redesigned roles. A comprehensive transition support program, including career counseling, resume assistance, and extended healthcare benefits, will be made available to all impacted employees for a period of six months following their separation date.

Implementation will proceed in three phases over 12 months, with Phase 1 focusing on organizational design and stakeholder communication from May through July, Phase 2 addressing systems integration and process harmonization from August through November, and Phase 3 completing personnel transitions and final operational handoffs from December through April of next year. I have requested detailed implementation plans from each regional director by May 15.
