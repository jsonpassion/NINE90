# Regulatory Compliance Report

To: Executive Committee
From: Dr. Evelyn Marsh, General Counsel
Date: February 27
Re: Assessment of Compliance Readiness — Revised Environmental Protection Standards (EPS-2026)

Following the publication of the revised Environmental Protection Standards (EPS-2026) by the Federal Regulatory Commission on January 15, the Legal Department has conducted a comprehensive assessment of Thornton Chemical's current compliance posture. This report summarizes our findings and outlines the remedial actions necessary to achieve full compliance prior to the September 1 enforcement date.

Our assessment reveals that Thornton Chemical is currently in substantial compliance with approximately 78% of the revised standards. The remaining provisions pertain primarily to emissions monitoring protocols and waste disposal documentation requirements that were either absent from or less stringent under the previous regulatory framework. Failure to address these gaps before the enforcement date could expose the company to penalties ranging from $250,000 to $1.5 million per violation, in addition to potential operational restrictions.

To mitigate these risks, we propose a three-phase remediation plan. Phase I (March–April) will encompass the installation of continuous emissions monitoring equipment at all four production facilities. Phase II (May–June) will focus on the development and implementation of revised waste tracking and documentation procedures. Phase III (July–August) will consist of internal audits and mock inspections to corroborate our readiness prior to the enforcement date. The estimated cost for the full remediation program is $3.2 million, which we believe is indispensable given the magnitude of the potential penalties.

It should be noted that two of our competitors — Brennan Industries and Coastal Synthetics — have publicly disclosed that they have already commenced their own compliance programs. While this does not directly affect our obligations, it underscores the industry's recognition that the new standards represent an unprecedented shift in regulatory expectations. Subsequent to the enforcement date, the Commission has indicated that it intends to conduct facility inspections on an unannounced basis, which further reinforces the urgency of our preparations.

I recommend that the Executive Committee authorize the proposed remediation budget at its next meeting and designate a cross-functional compliance task force to oversee the implementation.

- A. Outdated production equipment at manufacturing facilities
- B. Insufficient employee training on environmental regulations
