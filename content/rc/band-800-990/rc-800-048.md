# Quarterly Investor Report — Harborview Real Estate Investment Trust

Harborview REIT reports operating results for the quarter ended September 30, 2025. Funds from operations ("FFO"), the primary performance metric for real estate investment trusts, was $186 million or $1.42 per diluted share, representing increases of 8 percent and 6 percent, respectively, compared with the prior-year quarter. The difference between total FFO growth and per-share FFO growth reflects the dilutive effect of 3.2 million shares issued in April 2025 to partially fund the acquisition of a six-property industrial portfolio.

Same-store net operating income ("NOI") grew 4.2 percent, driven by rental rate increases averaging 12 percent on new and renewed leases, partially offset by a 90 basis point decline in occupancy to 94.6 percent as two large office tenants consolidated their spaces at lease expiration. The mark-to-market leasing spread — the difference between new lease rates and the expiring lease rates they replace — was positive 15 percent, indicating strong embedded growth as below-market leases roll to current market rates over the coming years.

The portfolio's composition continued to shift toward industrial and logistics properties, which now represent 48 percent of total NOI, up from 38 percent two years ago. Office properties have declined to 32 percent of NOI, and retail comprises the remaining 20 percent. This strategic rotation reflects management's conviction that secular demand drivers in e-commerce fulfillment and supply chain modernization will support superior growth and valuation multiples for industrial assets relative to traditional office and retail properties.

Balance sheet metrics remain conservative. Net debt to EBITDA stands at 5.4 times, within the REIT's stated target range of 5.0 to 6.0 times. The weighted average interest rate on outstanding debt is 4.1 percent, with a weighted average maturity of 5.8 years. No significant debt maturities occur before 2027, providing insulation from near-term refinancing risk.

The Board declared a quarterly dividend of $0.92 per share, representing a 4 percent increase and an annualized yield of approximately 4.8 percent based on the current share price. The FFO payout ratio of 65 percent provides substantial retained cash flow for reinvestment.

## Q1. Why did per-share FFO growth lag total FFO growth?
- A. New shares issued to fund an acquisition diluted the per-share calculation.
- B. The REIT reduced its dividend, decreasing the amount available per share.
- C. Several properties were sold at a loss during the quarter.
- D. Operating expenses increased faster than revenue.
- Answer: A
- Explanation: "The difference reflects the dilutive effect of 3.2 million shares issued in April 2025 to partially fund the acquisition."

## Q2. What does the 15 percent mark-to-market leasing spread indicate?
- A. Rental income will decrease as old leases expire.
- B. Market rents have declined 15 percent from their peak.
- C. The REIT is losing tenants at a rate of 15 percent annually.
- D. New lease rates are significantly higher than expiring rates, indicating embedded future growth.
- Answer: D
- Explanation: A positive 15 percent spread means "new lease rates" exceed "expiring lease rates they replace," indicating "strong embedded growth as below-market leases roll to current market rates."

## Q3. What can be inferred about management's view of the office property sector?
- A. Management views office properties as the strongest growth opportunity.
- B. Management plans to increase office exposure to 50 percent of the portfolio.
- C. Office properties are the REIT's most profitable segment.
- D. Management is strategically reducing office exposure in favor of industrial properties with stronger growth drivers.
- Answer: D
- Explanation: Office has declined from a larger share to 32 percent while industrial grew to 48 percent, reflecting management's conviction about superior industrial property fundamentals.

## Q4. The term "FFO payout ratio" refers to:
- A. The percentage of total debt that is paid down each quarter
- B. The ratio of capital expenditures to total revenue
- C. The proportion of funds from operations distributed to shareholders as dividends
- D. The percentage of vacant properties in the portfolio
- Answer: C
- Explanation: A 65 percent FFO payout ratio means 65 percent of FFO is paid as dividends, with the remaining 35 percent retained for reinvestment.
