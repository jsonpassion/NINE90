# Contract Renewal Brief

To: Procurement Committee Members
From: Sandra Whitfield, Director of Vendor Relations
Date: February 14
Re: Renewal of Maintenance Services Agreement — Hargrave Technical Solutions

As the current three-year maintenance services agreement with Hargrave Technical Solutions is set to expire on March 31, I am writing to recommend that the committee authorize the commencement of renewal negotiations. Notwithstanding the minor service disruptions documented in the Q3 performance review, Hargrave has consistently met or exceeded the key performance indicators stipulated in the original contract over the duration of the engagement.

A provision in the existing agreement grants Dalton Industries the right to renegotiate pricing terms upon renewal, provided that written notice is delivered no fewer than 60 days prior to the expiration date. Given this timeline, I urge the committee to expedite its review so that we may issue formal notice by January 30 at the latest.

It should be noted that Hargrave has proposed a revised fee structure that reflects a 4.5% increase over the current rates. While this exceeds the 3% threshold outlined in our procurement guidelines, the increase is commensurate with current market rates for comparable services. Furthermore, transitioning to an alternative vendor at this juncture would entail substantial onboarding costs and a projected three-month integration period, during which service continuity cannot be guaranteed.

I recommend that we accept the revised terms, subject to the inclusion of more stringent service-level commitments and a penalty clause for response times exceeding the agreed-upon benchmarks. A detailed comparison of the proposed terms against two alternative bids is attached for your reference.

## Q161. What is the primary purpose of this memo?
- A. To report on Hargrave Technical Solutions' quarterly performance
- B. To recommend proceeding with contract renewal negotiations
- C. To announce a change in the company's procurement guidelines
- D. To introduce an alternative vendor for maintenance services
- Answer: B
- Explanation: The memo's opening paragraph states that Ms. Whitfield is writing "to recommend that the committee authorize the commencement of renewal negotiations," making B the correct answer.

## Q162. According to the memo, what must happen by January 30?
- A. The revised fee structure must be finalized.
- B. Hargrave must submit its performance review.
- C. Formal written notice of intent to renegotiate must be issued.
- D. The procurement committee must select an alternative vendor.
- Answer: C
- Explanation: The memo states that a provision requires written notice "no fewer than 60 days prior to the expiration date" of March 31, and urges the committee to act so that "we may issue formal notice by January 30 at the latest."

## Q163. Why does Ms. Whitfield suggest accepting the fee increase despite it exceeding company guidelines?
- A. Hargrave has threatened to terminate the agreement otherwise.
- B. The increase aligns with market rates and switching vendors would be costly and disruptive.
- C. The procurement guidelines have recently been revised to allow higher increases.
- D. Alternative vendors have proposed even higher rates for the same services.
- Answer: B
- Explanation: The memo explains that the increase is "commensurate with current market rates" and that transitioning to another vendor would involve "substantial onboarding costs" and a "three-month integration period" with no guarantee of service continuity.
