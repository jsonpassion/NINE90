# Investment Proposal — Sovereign Infrastructure Partners Fund III

Presented to: Investment Committee, Northgate Pension Trust
Prepared by: Brookdale Capital Advisors

Executive Summary: Brookdale Capital Advisors respectfully submits this proposal recommending that Northgate Pension Trust allocate $250 million to the Sovereign Infrastructure Partners Fund III ("the Fund"), a closed-end infrastructure investment vehicle targeting essential-service assets in developed markets across North America, Western Europe, and Australasia. The Fund's investment thesis is predicated on the structural undersupply of critical infrastructure and the growing imperative for governments to mobilize private capital to address aging transportation networks, energy transition requirements, and digital connectivity gaps.

Fund Strategy and Structure: The Fund targets a diversified portfolio of 15 to 20 core-plus infrastructure assets, including regulated utilities, renewable energy generation facilities, transportation concessions, and digital infrastructure platforms. The target gross internal rate of return is 10% to 12%, with an expected net distribution yield of 5% to 6% commencing in the third year of the fund's twelve-year term. The general partner, Sovereign Infrastructure Partners, has deployed over $18 billion across two predecessor funds, generating a weighted average net IRR of 11.3% and a 1.6x net multiple of invested capital.

Risk Considerations: While infrastructure investments generally exhibit lower volatility than traditional equity allocations, the Fund's strategy is not without material risks. Regulatory and political risk is inherent in assets subject to government oversight, particularly regulated utilities where tariff-setting mechanisms may be altered to the detriment of private operators. Currency risk is a consideration given the Fund's multi-jurisdictional mandate, though the general partner employs a systematic hedging program targeting 75% to 85% coverage of non-domestic currency exposures. Additionally, the illiquid nature of infrastructure investments necessitates a long-term commitment horizon, and early redemption provisions are deliberately restrictive to protect portfolio stability.

Alignment with Investment Policy: The proposed allocation is consistent with Northgate Pension Trust's strategic asset allocation framework, which permits infrastructure investments of up to 15% of total assets under management. The current infrastructure allocation stands at 8.2%, and the proposed commitment would bring this to approximately 10.7%, well within the established parameters and consistent with the Trust's objective of enhancing portfolio diversification and securing predictable long-term cash flows to meet actuarial liabilities.

## Q1. What is the primary investment rationale for the Fund?
- A. Speculative returns from emerging market infrastructure development
- B. Structural demand for private capital to address critical infrastructure needs in developed markets
- C. Short-term trading profits from infrastructure-related securities
- D. Tax avoidance opportunities in the renewable energy sector
- Answer: B
- Explanation: The Fund's thesis is "predicated on the structural undersupply of critical infrastructure and the growing imperative for governments to mobilize private capital to address aging transportation networks, energy transition requirements, and digital connectivity gaps."
