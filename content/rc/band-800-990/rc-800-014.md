# Due Diligence Summary Report — Proposed Acquisition of Stellarise Biotech, Inc.

Prepared for: Acquisition Review Committee, Rathmore Pharmaceuticals PLC
Prepared by: Whitfield & Crane Advisory Partners

This report summarizes the findings of the comprehensive due diligence investigation conducted in connection with the proposed acquisition of Stellarise Biotech, Inc. ("Target") by Rathmore Pharmaceuticals PLC ("Acquirer"). The investigation encompassed financial, legal, regulatory, intellectual property, and operational dimensions and was conducted over a sixty-day period concluding on March 15.

Financial Assessment: The Target reported revenues of $385 million for the most recent fiscal year, with a compound annual growth rate of 22% over the preceding three-year period. However, approximately 64% of total revenue is derived from a single oncology therapeutic, Velantra, whose patent protection expires in thirty-one months. Post-patent-expiry revenue projections indicate a potential decline of 40% to 55% within the first two years of generic competition, unless the Target's pipeline candidates achieve timely regulatory approval. EBITDA margins of 34% are above industry median but are expected to compress significantly as patent cliff effects materialize.

Intellectual Property Review: The Target maintains a portfolio of 47 granted patents and 23 pending applications across four therapeutic areas. The cornerstone patent covering Velantra's formulation is currently the subject of two inter partes review proceedings filed by generic manufacturers. Outside counsel has assessed the probability of the patent being upheld as approximately 65%, though an adverse ruling could accelerate generic entry by up to eighteen months.

Regulatory and Pipeline Analysis: The Target has three candidates in late-stage clinical development. The most advanced, a next-generation immuno-oncology compound designated SBT-4100, is currently in Phase III trials with top-line data expected within nine months. Regulatory advisors have assessed the probability of approval at 58%, based on interim efficacy data and the current competitive landscape. The remaining two candidates are in Phase II, with estimated timelines to potential approval of three to four years.

Recommendation: The advisory team recommends proceeding with the acquisition, subject to a revised valuation that incorporates a risk-adjusted discount reflecting patent vulnerability and pipeline uncertainty. We further recommend structuring the transaction with contingent consideration provisions tied to specific regulatory milestones.

## Q1. What is the most significant financial risk identified in the due diligence report?
- A. The Target's revenues have been declining for three consecutive years
- B. The majority of revenue depends on a single product facing imminent patent expiration
- C. The Target has no intellectual property portfolio
- D. The Target's EBITDA margins are below industry standards
- Answer: B
- Explanation: The report highlights that "approximately 64% of total revenue is derived from a single oncology therapeutic, Velantra, whose patent protection expires in thirty-one months," representing the most critical financial risk.

- A. They recommend abandoning the acquisition entirely

- C. They believe the acquisition should proceed at the originally proposed valuation
- D. They recommend acquiring only the Target's intellectual property portfolio
- Answer: B
- Explanation: The team "recommends proceeding with the acquisition" but "subject to a revised valuation that incorporates a risk-adjusted discount" and contingent consideration provisions, indicating qualified support.

## Q3. The phrase "patent cliff" in the Financial Assessment section refers to:
- A. A legal dispute over patent ownership between competing companies
- B. A sharp decline in revenue when patent protection expires and generic competition begins
- C. An increase in patent filing fees imposed by regulatory authorities
- D. The process of extending patent protection through regulatory applications
- Answer: B
- Explanation: In pharmaceutical context, "patent cliff" refers to the sharp revenue decline that occurs when a key drug's patent expires and lower-cost generic alternatives enter the market, which the passage describes with projected declines of 40% to 55%.
