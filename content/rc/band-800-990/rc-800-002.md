# Merger Integration Update

To: Senior Leadership Team
From: Richard Calloway, Chief Integration Officer
Date: March 3
Re: Weston-Greer Merger — 90-Day Integration Progress Report

This memorandum provides an update on the integration of Weston Dynamics and Greer Manufacturing following the completion of the merger on December 1. As we approach the 90-day milestone, I am pleased to report that the majority of critical workstreams are proceeding on or ahead of schedule, though certain areas require continued attention.

The consolidation of the IT infrastructure, which encompasses the migration of over 14,000 user accounts and the unification of enterprise resource planning systems, has been completed two weeks ahead of the projected timeline. This achievement is attributable to the meticulous planning undertaken by the joint technology task force during the pre-merger phase. I wish to commend both teams for their exemplary collaboration throughout this process.

Workforce integration, however, presents an ongoing challenge. Of the 340 positions identified as redundant during the due diligence phase, 285 have been resolved through voluntary separation packages and internal redeployment. The remaining 55 positions are concentrated primarily in the finance and legal departments, where functional overlap is most pronounced. We anticipate that these will be fully addressed by the end of Q1, subject to the completion of the departmental restructuring plans currently under review.

Notwithstanding these workforce complexities, the projected cost synergies of $23 million annually remain achievable. Preliminary financial modeling indicates that we may, in fact, realize an additional $4 million in savings from the earlier-than-anticipated IT consolidation. A comprehensive financial reconciliation will be presented to the board at the April meeting.

I recommend that the leadership team convene a dedicated session to review the departmental restructuring proposals before they are finalized, as the decisions made in this phase will have lasting implications for the organizational structure of the combined entity.

## Q164. What can be inferred about the IT consolidation?
- A. It was delayed due to unexpected technical difficulties.
- B. It was the most expensive component of the integration.
- C. It was completed faster than originally planned.
- D. It required hiring additional external consultants.
- Answer: C
- Explanation: The memo states the IT infrastructure consolidation "has been completed two weeks ahead of the projected timeline," indicating it finished faster than originally planned.
