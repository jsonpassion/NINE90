# Confidential Memorandum — Organizational Restructuring Plan: Phase II Implementation

TO: Board of Directors
FROM: Chief Restructuring Officer
DATE: November 10, 2025
RE: Phase II Implementation Plan and Financial Impact Assessment

This memorandum provides the Board with a comprehensive overview of the Phase II restructuring plan, which builds upon the operational consolidation measures completed in Phase I during the first half of 2025. Phase I achieved $92 million in annualized cost savings through the closure of three redundant manufacturing facilities and the consolidation of regional sales offices, but incurred $145 million in one-time charges including severance payments, asset impairments, and lease termination costs.

Phase II addresses the organizational structure and spans three workstreams. Workstream A involves the reorganization of business units from a geographic structure to a product-line structure, which is expected to eliminate duplicative management layers and improve strategic alignment. This change will reduce the number of senior vice president and vice president positions from 48 to 31, with estimated annual savings of $34 million in compensation and related overhead. Affected executives will be offered either reassignment to available roles at the senior director level or enhanced severance packages valued at 18 to 24 months of total compensation.

Workstream B addresses the centralization of support functions. Finance, human resources, legal, and procurement operations currently maintained independently in each business unit will be consolidated into shared services centers. This consolidation is projected to reduce support function headcount by 280 positions, representing 22 percent of the current support function workforce, and generate annual savings of $48 million. The transition will be executed in three stages over 15 months to maintain operational continuity.

Workstream C focuses on the divestiture of non-core business lines. The committee has identified three business units — Specialty Coatings, Regional Distribution Services, and Marine Equipment — that do not align with the company's refocused strategic priorities. Preliminary valuations suggest aggregate divestiture proceeds of $650 to $780 million, which would be allocated to debt reduction ($400 million), investment in core businesses ($200 million), and share repurchases (remainder).

The total one-time cost of Phase II implementation is estimated at $180 million, with projected annualized savings of $82 million achieving full run-rate by the fourth quarter of 2026.

## Q1. What were the results of Phase I restructuring?
- A. $145 million in annualized savings at a one-time cost of $92 million
- B. $82 million in annualized savings with no one-time charges
- C. $92 million in annualized savings with $145 million in one-time charges
- D. $48 million in savings from shared services consolidation
- Answer: C
- Explanation: "Phase I achieved $92 million in annualized cost savings" but "incurred $145 million in one-time charges."

## Q2. How will affected senior executives be treated under Workstream A?
- A. They will be offered reassignment or enhanced severance of 18 to 24 months.
- B. All will be immediately terminated without severance.
- C. They will retain their current positions with reduced responsibilities.
- D. They will be transferred to the divested business units.
- Answer: A
- Explanation: Executives "will be offered either reassignment to available roles at the senior director level or enhanced severance packages valued at 18 to 24 months of total compensation."

## Q3. What can be inferred about the rationale for the phased transition of support functions?
- A. Budget constraints prevent simultaneous implementation across all functions.
- B. The company has not yet decided which functions to consolidate.
- C. Government regulations require a minimum transition period for workforce changes.
- D. A gradual approach reduces the risk of operational disruption during the consolidation.
- Answer: D
- Explanation: "The transition will be executed in three stages over 15 months to maintain operational continuity," indicating the phased approach is designed to minimize disruption.

## Q4. How would divestiture proceeds be prioritized?
- A. Share repurchases first, then debt reduction, then investment
- B. Debt reduction first ($400M), then core business investment ($200M), then share repurchases
- C. Equal allocation across all three uses
- D. All proceeds would be retained as cash reserves
- Answer: B
- Explanation: Proceeds "would be allocated to debt reduction ($400 million), investment in core businesses ($200 million), and share repurchases (remainder)."
