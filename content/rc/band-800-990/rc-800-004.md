# Strategic Partnership Proposal

To: Board of Directors
From: James Harrington, Chief Strategy Officer
Date: March 5
Re: Proposed Strategic Alliance with Meridian Logistics International

I am writing to present for the Board's consideration a strategic partnership proposal from Meridian Logistics International (MLI), a leading provider of integrated supply chain solutions operating across 42 countries. If approved, this alliance would represent a transformative opportunity for Ashford Consumer Products to expedite its international expansion while circumventing the substantial capital expenditures typically associated with building proprietary logistics infrastructure in new markets.

Under the proposed terms, Ashford would gain exclusive access to MLI's warehousing and distribution network across Southeast Asia and Latin America — two regions identified as high-priority growth markets in our five-year strategic plan. In return, MLI would receive preferential pricing on Ashford's product portfolio for resale through its established retail channels, along with a revenue-sharing arrangement on all goods distributed through the partnership. The initial term of the agreement would be five years, with provisions for renewal subject to mutually agreed-upon performance benchmarks.

The financial projections are compelling. Our analysis indicates that this partnership could generate incremental revenue of approximately $85 million over the initial five-year term, while reducing our projected market-entry costs by an estimated 40%. These figures supersede our earlier internal estimates, which assumed that Ashford would establish its own distribution infrastructure — a scenario that would have required capital investment of approximately $120 million and a timeline of 18 to 24 months before operations could commence.

Notwithstanding these advantages, the proposal does present certain risks that warrant careful deliberation. MLI's exclusive distribution rights in the designated regions would limit our flexibility to engage alternative logistics providers during the term of the agreement. Additionally, the revenue-sharing model, while feasible in the current pricing environment, could compress our margins if raw material costs increase substantially. To ameliorate these concerns, our legal team has drafted supplementary clauses that would allow for renegotiation of the revenue-sharing percentages in the event of material cost fluctuations exceeding 15%.

I recommend that the Board authorize the formation of a due diligence committee to evaluate the proposal in detail and report its findings within 45 days.

## Q170. What is the primary advantage of the proposed partnership over Ashford's original expansion plan?
- A. It would allow Ashford to enter more markets than originally planned.
- B. It would significantly reduce costs and accelerate the timeline for international expansion.
- C. It would give Ashford ownership of logistics infrastructure in new markets.
- D. It would eliminate all financial risks associated with international expansion.
- Answer: B
- Explanation: The memo states the partnership could reduce "projected market-entry costs by an estimated 40%" compared to building proprietary infrastructure, which "would have required capital investment of approximately $120 million and a timeline of 18 to 24 months." The partnership would expedite this process significantly.

## Q171. What risk mitigation measure has been proposed for the revenue-sharing arrangement?
- A. Capping the total revenue shared with MLI at a fixed annual amount
- B. Allowing renegotiation of revenue-sharing terms if material costs rise more than 15%
- C. Requiring MLI to absorb all increases in raw material costs
- D. Limiting the revenue-sharing arrangement to the first two years of the agreement
- Answer: B
- Explanation: The memo states that "our legal team has drafted supplementary clauses that would allow for renegotiation of the revenue-sharing percentages in the event of material cost fluctuations exceeding 15%."

## Q172. What is Mr. Harrington's recommended next step?
- A. Immediately sign the partnership agreement with Meridian Logistics International
- B. Reject the proposal and proceed with building proprietary logistics infrastructure
- C. Establish a due diligence committee to evaluate the proposal within 45 days
- D. Negotiate a shorter initial term of three years instead of five
- Answer: C
- Explanation: The final paragraph recommends that "the Board authorize the formation of a due diligence committee to evaluate the proposal in detail and report its findings within 45 days," indicating a thorough review rather than immediate acceptance or rejection.
