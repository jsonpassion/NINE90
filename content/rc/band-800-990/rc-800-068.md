# Antitrust Compliance Program

ANTITRUST AND COMPETITION LAW COMPLIANCE PROGRAM — GLOBAL OPERATING GUIDELINES

Issued by the Office of the General Counsel, Harrington Industrial Group

This compliance program establishes binding rules of conduct for all officers, directors, employees, and agents of Harrington Industrial Group and its subsidiaries worldwide ("Covered Persons") regarding interactions with competitors, customers, suppliers, and trade association participants. Violations of antitrust and competition laws carry severe consequences for both the corporation and individuals involved, including criminal fines of up to $100 million per offense under US law, imprisonment for up to 10 years for individual offenders, and administrative fines of up to 10 percent of worldwide annual turnover under European Union competition rules.

PROHIBITED CONDUCT: Covered Persons shall not, under any circumstances, engage in the following with any competitor or potential competitor: (i) agreements, understandings, or concerted practices to fix, stabilize, or coordinate prices, discounts, rebates, or other terms of sale; (ii) allocation of markets, territories, customers, or product lines; (iii) coordination of bids in public or private procurement processes, including agreements to submit artificially high bids, to refrain from bidding, or to rotate winning bids; (iv) agreements to restrict production output or supply capacity; or (v) exchanges of competitively sensitive information, including current or future pricing, cost structures, production volumes, capacity utilization, strategic plans, or customer-specific commercial terms. These prohibitions apply regardless of whether the conduct is initiated by the Covered Person or by the competitor, and regardless of whether any formal agreement is reached — the mere exchange of competitively sensitive information may constitute a violation.

TRADE ASSOCIATION PARTICIPATION: Participation in trade associations, industry conferences, and benchmarking groups requires prior written approval from the Legal Department. Approved participants must adhere to the following protocols: attend only meetings with pre-circulated agendas reviewed by legal counsel; leave any meeting immediately if competitively sensitive topics arise and report the incident to the Legal Department within 24 hours; and refrain from participating in any data collection or benchmarking activity that involves the sharing of disaggregated, company-specific commercial data unless the activity is administered by an independent third party using protocols that prevent the identification of individual company data.

DAWN RAID PREPAREDNESS: All facilities must maintain a current dawn raid response protocol, which shall be tested through simulation exercises at least annually. In the event of an unannounced inspection by competition authorities, the facility manager shall immediately contact the General Counsel's office, cooperate with inspectors' lawful requests while preserving the company's legal privileges, and ensure that a qualified attorney is present before providing access to any documents or electronic records that may be subject to legal professional privilege.

ENFORCEMENT AND REPORTING: Any Covered Person who becomes aware of conduct that may constitute a violation of this program must report it immediately through the confidential compliance hotline or directly to the Chief Compliance Officer. The company operates a strict non-retaliation policy for good-faith reporters. Violations of this compliance program will result in disciplinary action up to and including termination of employment and referral to law enforcement authorities, regardless of the seniority of the individual involved.

## Q1. What is the maximum criminal fine per offense under US antitrust law?
- A. $10 million
- B. $50 million
- C. $100 million
- D. 10 percent of worldwide annual turnover
- Answer: C
- Explanation: US law provides for 'criminal fines of up to $100 million per offense.'

## Q2. Which of the following is explicitly prohibited even without a formal agreement?
- A. Attending industry conferences with competitors present
- B. The mere exchange of competitively sensitive information with competitors
- C. Submitting competitive bids in public procurement
- D. Discussing general industry trends at trade associations
- Answer: B
- Explanation: The prohibitions apply 'regardless of whether any formal agreement is reached — the mere exchange of competitively sensitive information may constitute a violation.'

## Q3. What must participants do if competitively sensitive topics arise at a trade association meeting?
- A. Request that the topic be tabled for a future meeting
- B. Continue participating but take detailed notes for the Legal Department
- C. Leave the meeting immediately and report the incident to the Legal Department within 24 hours
- D. Ask the meeting organizer to change the subject
- Answer: C
- Explanation: Participants must 'leave any meeting immediately if competitively sensitive topics arise and report the incident to the Legal Department within 24 hours.'

## Q4. How often must dawn raid response protocols be tested?
- A. Monthly
- B. Quarterly
- C. At least annually through simulation exercises
- D. Only after an actual dawn raid occurs
- Answer: C
- Explanation: The protocol 'shall be tested through simulation exercises at least annually.'
