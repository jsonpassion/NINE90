# Enterprise Risk Management Committee Report to the Board of Directors

This report presents the findings and recommendations of the Enterprise Risk Management Committee for the fiscal year ended September 30, 2025. The committee conducted a comprehensive review of the organization's risk landscape, incorporating quantitative modeling, scenario analysis, and assessments from each business unit's risk owner.

The committee has identified five principal risks that warrant board-level attention. The first and most significant is geopolitical supply chain risk. Our analysis indicates that 34 percent of critical raw material inputs are sourced from jurisdictions currently subject to elevated geopolitical tensions. Scenario modeling projects that a severe trade disruption affecting any two of our five primary sourcing countries could reduce production capacity by 18 to 25 percent for a period of three to six months, with an estimated financial impact of $280 to $420 million. Mitigation measures currently in progress include qualifying alternative suppliers in lower-risk jurisdictions and increasing strategic inventory buffers from 45 to 90 days for critical materials.

The second principal risk is cybersecurity, where the threat landscape has intensified significantly. Attempted intrusions against our network infrastructure increased 62 percent year-over-year, and the sophistication of attacks — particularly those employing AI-generated social engineering techniques — has escalated materially. While our security operations center successfully contained all attempted breaches during the period, the committee recommends an incremental investment of $18 million in advanced threat detection capabilities and zero-trust architecture implementation.

The third risk relates to regulatory compliance in the evolving ESG reporting landscape. New mandatory climate disclosure regulations taking effect in multiple jurisdictions in 2026 require audited emissions data across Scope 1, 2, and 3 categories. Our current data collection and verification capabilities are not sufficient to meet these requirements, and the committee recommends accelerating the implementation of the enterprise ESG data management platform at an additional cost of $12 million.

The fourth and fifth principal risks — talent retention in critical technical disciplines and interest rate exposure on the floating-rate portion of our debt portfolio — are addressed in the detailed appendices to this report.

## Q1. What is the most significant risk identified by the committee?
- A. Geopolitical supply chain risk affecting critical raw material sourcing
- B. Cybersecurity threats from AI-generated social engineering
- C. Regulatory compliance with ESG reporting requirements
- D. Talent retention in technical disciplines
- Answer: A
- Explanation: The committee identifies geopolitical supply chain risk as "the first and most significant" principal risk.

## Q2. What is the projected financial impact of a severe trade disruption scenario?
- A. $280 million to $420 million
- B. $120 million to $180 million
- C. $18 million to $62 million
- D. $450 million to $600 million
- Answer: A
- Explanation: The scenario projects "an estimated financial impact of $280 to $420 million."

## Q3. What can be inferred about the organization's current ESG reporting capabilities?

- Explanation: "Our current data collection and verification capabilities are not sufficient to meet these requirements" for new regulations in 2026.

## Q4. The term "zero-trust architecture" in the cybersecurity context most likely refers to:
- A. A building design that prevents unauthorized physical access to servers
- B. A policy of refusing to work with any external cybersecurity vendors
- C. A security model that requires verification for every user and device, assuming no inherent trust
- D. An insurance product that provides zero deductible for cyber incidents
- Answer: C
- Explanation: "Zero-trust architecture" is a cybersecurity framework that assumes no user or device should be automatically trusted, requiring continuous verification — appropriate context given the discussion of advanced threat detection.
