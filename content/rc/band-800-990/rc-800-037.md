# Executive Employment Agreement — Chief Financial Officer

This Executive Employment Agreement ("Agreement") is entered into as of October 1, 2025, by and between Harrington Global Industries, Inc. ("Company") and Victoria Chen ("Executive"), establishing the terms and conditions of Executive's employment as Chief Financial Officer of the Company.

Section 3 — Compensation: Executive shall receive an annual base salary of $850,000, subject to annual review by the Compensation Committee of the Board of Directors. In addition, Executive shall be eligible for an annual cash bonus targeted at 100 percent of base salary, with actual payout ranging from 0 to 200 percent of target based on achievement of performance metrics established annually by the Compensation Committee. For fiscal year 2026, the performance metrics shall be weighted as follows: 40 percent based on consolidated adjusted EBITDA growth, 30 percent based on free cash flow generation, and 30 percent based on individual strategic objectives.

Section 4 — Equity Awards: Upon commencement of employment, Executive shall receive an initial equity grant valued at $3.2 million, consisting of 50 percent performance-based restricted stock units ("PSUs") and 50 percent time-based restricted stock units ("RSUs"). RSUs shall vest ratably over four years from the grant date. PSUs shall vest based on the Company's total shareholder return relative to a peer group of 20 companies over a three-year performance period, with threshold vesting at the 25th percentile (50 percent of target units), target vesting at the 50th percentile (100 percent of target units), and maximum vesting at the 75th percentile (200 percent of target units).

Section 7 — Restrictive Covenants: For a period of 24 months following termination of employment for any reason, Executive shall not: (a) accept employment with or provide services to any Competing Business, defined as any company deriving more than 25 percent of its revenue from the same business lines in which the Company operates; (b) solicit or recruit any employee of the Company who held a position at the director level or above during the 12 months preceding termination; or (c) solicit business from any customer or client of the Company with whom Executive had material contact during the 24 months preceding termination.

Section 9 — Termination Provisions: In the event of termination without Cause or resignation for Good Reason, Executive shall receive: (i) 24 months of base salary continuation; (ii) a prorated annual bonus for the year of termination based on actual performance; (iii) acceleration of time-based RSUs that would have vested during the 24-month severance period; and (iv) 18 months of continued health insurance coverage.

## Q1. What is the maximum possible annual cash bonus?
- A. $1,700,000
- B. $1,275,000
- C. $850,000
- D. $3,200,000
- Answer: A
- Explanation: The bonus target is 100 percent of $850,000 base salary, with maximum payout at 200 percent of target: $850,000 × 200% = $1,700,000.

## Q2. At what percentile of peer group TSR does maximum PSU vesting occur?
- A. 25th percentile
- B. 50th percentile
- C. 75th percentile
- D. 100th percentile
- Answer: C
- Explanation: "Maximum vesting at the 75th percentile (200 percent of target units)."

## Q3. What can be inferred about the Company's approach to retaining key executives?
- A. The Company uses a comprehensive approach combining competitive pay, equity with multi-year vesting, and strong severance protections.
- B. The Company relies solely on cash compensation with no equity incentives.
- C. The Company offers minimal severance to discourage executives from leaving.
- D. The Company does not include any performance-based compensation elements.
- Answer: A
- Explanation: The agreement combines base salary, performance bonuses, equity with 3-4 year vesting periods, 24-month severance, and continued benefits — a comprehensive retention approach.

## Q4. The phrase "Good Reason" for resignation most likely refers to:
- A. The executive's desire to pursue a different career opportunity
- B. Personal health reasons requiring the executive to stop working
- C. Company actions such as material reduction in duties, compensation, or other adverse changes that effectively force the executive's departure
- D. The executive's dissatisfaction with the company's stock price performance
- Answer: C
- Explanation: "Good Reason" is a legal term in executive agreements typically covering adverse actions by the employer (demotion, pay cuts, relocation) that constructively force departure, qualifying the executive for severance benefits.
