# Fiduciary Duty and Investment Management Agreement

INVESTMENT MANAGEMENT AGREEMENT — FIDUCIARY STANDARDS AND PERFORMANCE OBLIGATIONS

This Investment Management Agreement ("Agreement") is entered into as of January 15, 2026, by and between Caldwell Pension Trust ("Client"), a defined benefit pension plan established under the Employee Retirement Income Security Act of 1974, as amended ("ERISA"), and Meridian Capital Advisors, LLC ("Advisor"), a registered investment advisory firm duly authorized under the Investment Advisers Act of 1940.

The Client hereby appoints the Advisor as a discretionary investment manager for the portion of the Client's assets designated in Schedule A (the "Managed Assets"), currently valued at approximately $840 million. The Advisor accepts this appointment and acknowledges that it shall act as a fiduciary with respect to the Managed Assets, subject to the duties of loyalty, prudence, and diversification prescribed by ERISA Sections 404(a)(1)(A) and (B). The Advisor shall manage the Managed Assets in accordance with the Investment Policy Statement ("IPS") attached hereto as Exhibit 1, which establishes the strategic asset allocation, return objectives, risk tolerance parameters, and liquidity requirements of the Client.

The Advisor's compensation shall be calculated as an annual management fee of 0.45 percent of the average daily net asset value of the Managed Assets, payable quarterly in arrears. In addition, the Advisor shall be eligible for a performance fee equal to 15 percent of the net investment return in excess of the benchmark return specified in the IPS, subject to a high-water mark provision that prevents the Advisor from earning performance fees on gains that merely recover prior losses. The total annual compensation, inclusive of both management and performance fees, shall not exceed 0.85 percent of the average daily net asset value.

Either party may terminate this Agreement upon 90 days' written notice. Upon termination, the Advisor shall cooperate in the orderly transition of the Managed Assets to a successor manager and shall provide all records, reports, and documentation necessary to facilitate the transition within 30 business days. The Advisor shall not engage in any transaction designed to realize short-term gains or generate increased fees during the termination notice period, and any such transactions shall be subject to disgorgement of associated profits.

This Agreement shall be governed by and construed in accordance with the laws of the State of New York, and any disputes arising hereunder shall be resolved through binding arbitration administered by the American Arbitration Association under its Commercial Arbitration Rules.

## Q1. Under what regulatory framework does the Advisor accept fiduciary duties?
- A. The Securities Exchange Act of 1934
- B. The Employee Retirement Income Security Act of 1974 (ERISA)
- C. The Dodd-Frank Wall Street Reform and Consumer Protection Act
- D. The Sarbanes-Oxley Act of 2002
- Answer: B
- Explanation: The Advisor 'acknowledges that it shall act as a fiduciary' under 'the duties of loyalty, prudence, and diversification prescribed by ERISA Sections 404(a)(1)(A) and (B).'

## Q2. What is the purpose of the high-water mark provision?
- A. To ensure the Advisor maintains a minimum portfolio value
- B. To cap the total annual compensation at 0.85 percent
- C. To prevent the Advisor from earning performance fees on gains that merely recover prior losses
- D. To require quarterly rebalancing of the portfolio
- Answer: C
- Explanation: The high-water mark 'prevents the Advisor from earning performance fees on gains that merely recover prior losses.'

## Q3. What restriction applies during the termination notice period?
- A. The Advisor must immediately liquidate all positions.
- B. The Client cannot appoint a successor manager.
- C. The Advisor cannot engage in transactions designed to realize short-term gains or generate increased fees.
- D. Both parties must renegotiate the fee structure.
- Answer: C
- Explanation: The Advisor 'shall not engage in any transaction designed to realize short-term gains or generate increased fees during the termination notice period.'

## Q4. How are disputes under this Agreement resolved?
- A. Through litigation in federal court
- B. Through mediation by a neutral third party
- C. Through binding arbitration administered by the American Arbitration Association
- D. Through an internal review committee of both parties
- Answer: C
- Explanation: Disputes 'shall be resolved through binding arbitration administered by the American Arbitration Association.'
