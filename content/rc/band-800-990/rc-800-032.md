# Credit Analysis Report: Westfield Industrial Group — Senior Unsecured Debt Rating Review

Standard Rating Services is initiating a review of Westfield Industrial Group's BBB+ senior unsecured debt rating, with a negative outlook, reflecting concerns about the company's leverage trajectory and the deteriorating performance of its European operations. This report summarizes the key factors under consideration.

Westfield's credit profile has been supported historically by its diversified industrial portfolio, strong free cash flow generation, and conservative financial policies. However, several developments over the past 18 months have introduced material risk to the company's credit standing. Total debt has increased by $1.2 billion to $6.8 billion, primarily to fund the $2.1 billion acquisition of Eurotech Automation GmbH in January 2025. The resulting leverage ratio of 3.8 times net debt to EBITDA exceeds the 3.0 times threshold that the rating agency considers consistent with the current rating.

Management has committed to deleveraging to 3.0 times within 24 months through a combination of EBITDA growth from the integrated Eurotech operations and the application of free cash flow to debt reduction. However, the deleveraging plan faces execution risk. Eurotech's EBITDA contribution of €180 million for the first three quarters has fallen 15 percent below the acquisition-case projections, attributable to weaker-than-anticipated demand in the European industrial automation market and integration-related operational disruptions.

Additionally, Westfield's core North American operations, while fundamentally sound, face cyclical headwinds. The manufacturing PMI has contracted for four consecutive months, suggesting potential demand weakness in the near term. Free cash flow for the trailing twelve months was $820 million, down from $940 million in the prior period, reflecting both higher interest expenses and increased capital expenditures related to the integration program.

The rating review will be resolved within 90 days. A downgrade to BBB would be triggered by failure to demonstrate a credible path to 3.0 times leverage within the 24-month timeframe, continued underperformance of the Eurotech operations, or a material decline in free cash flow generation below $700 million. Conversely, the rating could be affirmed at BBB+ with a stable outlook if Eurotech performance stabilizes and Westfield achieves leverage below 3.4 times within the next two quarters.

## Q1. Why is the credit rating under review?
- A. Westfield filed for bankruptcy protection.
- B. Increased leverage from an acquisition and deteriorating European operations raised concerns.
- C. The company voluntarily requested a rating downgrade.
- D. A fraud investigation was initiated by regulators.
- Answer: B
- Explanation: The review reflects "concerns about the company's leverage trajectory and the deteriorating performance of its European operations" after the $2.1 billion Eurotech acquisition.

- A. 3 percent
- B. 10 percent
- C. 24 percent
- D. 15 percent
- Answer: D
- Explanation: "Eurotech's EBITDA contribution has fallen 15 percent below the acquisition-case projections."

## Q3. What can be inferred about the relationship between acquisition strategy and credit risk?
- A. Acquisitions always strengthen a company's credit profile.
- B. Credit rating agencies do not consider acquisition-related debt in their assessments.
- C. Debt-funded acquisitions that underperform projections can meaningfully weaken credit standing.
- D. Westfield's acquisition was too small to affect its credit rating.
- Answer: C
- Explanation: The $2.1 billion acquisition increased debt by $1.2 billion, pushed leverage to 3.8x (above the 3.0x threshold), and underperformed projections — all directly threatening the credit rating.

- Explanation: The rating "could be affirmed at BBB+ with a stable outlook if Eurotech performance stabilizes and Westfield achieves leverage below 3.4 times within the next two quarters."
