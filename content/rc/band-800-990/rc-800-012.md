# Internal Memorandum — Revised Corporate Governance Framework

TO: All Members of the Board of Directors and Senior Leadership Team
FROM: Office of the General Counsel, Ashford-Wentworth Corporation
RE: Adoption of Enhanced Corporate Governance Protocols

This memorandum outlines the principal amendments to the Ashford-Wentworth Corporation Corporate Governance Charter, which were unanimously approved by the Governance and Nominating Committee on February 28. These amendments are responsive to evolving best practices in corporate governance, recent regulatory guidance from the Securities and Exchange Commission, and feedback received during the most recent institutional shareholder engagement cycle.

Board Composition and Independence: Effective immediately, the minimum threshold for independent directors shall be raised from 60% to 75% of total board membership. Additionally, no individual shall serve as an independent director for more than twelve consecutive years, after which a mandatory two-year cooling-off period must elapse before reappointment eligibility. This tenure limitation is designed to ensure the continuous infusion of fresh perspectives while preserving institutional knowledge through staggered rotation schedules.

Executive Compensation Oversight: The Compensation Committee's mandate has been expanded to encompass not only traditional financial performance metrics but also non-financial indicators, including employee engagement scores, diversity and inclusion benchmarks, and environmental sustainability targets. Henceforth, a minimum of 20% of executive variable compensation shall be tied to the achievement of measurable ESG objectives, as independently verified by a third-party auditor. This change aligns with the growing consensus among institutional investors that long-term value creation is inextricably linked to responsible corporate citizenship.

Risk Oversight Enhancement: The board has established a dedicated Enterprise Risk Committee, separate from the Audit Committee, to provide focused oversight of emerging risk categories including cybersecurity threats, climate-related financial risks, and geopolitical supply chain vulnerabilities. The Enterprise Risk Committee shall convene no fewer than six times annually and shall have direct access to the Chief Risk Officer and external risk advisory consultants without requiring management intermediation.

Shareholder Engagement: The board has formalized a proactive shareholder engagement program requiring that independent directors participate in a minimum of two direct engagement sessions annually with the company's twenty largest institutional shareholders.

## Q1. What prompted the amendments to the Corporate Governance Charter?
- A. A hostile takeover attempt by a competing corporation
- B. Evolving best practices, regulatory guidance, and institutional shareholder feedback
- C. A significant decline in the company's stock price
- D. The resignation of multiple board members due to governance failures
- Answer: B
- Explanation: The memo states the amendments are "responsive to evolving best practices in corporate governance, recent regulatory guidance from the Securities and Exchange Commission, and feedback received during the most recent institutional shareholder engagement cycle."

## Q2. What is the purpose of the twelve-year tenure limitation for independent directors?
- A. To reduce the total number of directors on the board
- B. To ensure continuous introduction of new perspectives while maintaining institutional knowledge
- C. To comply with a mandatory SEC regulation
- D. To allow executives to transition into board roles more quickly
- Answer: B
- Explanation: The memo states the limitation "is designed to ensure the continuous infusion of fresh perspectives while preserving institutional knowledge through staggered rotation schedules."

## Q3. How does the revised compensation framework reflect broader investor expectations?
- A. It eliminates all variable compensation for executives
- B. It ties a portion of executive pay to independently verified ESG performance metrics
- C. It bases compensation exclusively on short-term stock price performance
- D. It removes the Compensation Committee's oversight authority
- Answer: B
- Explanation: The memo requires "a minimum of 20% of executive variable compensation shall be tied to the achievement of measurable ESG objectives, as independently verified by a third-party auditor," aligning with institutional investor expectations.

## Q4. The phrase "without requiring management intermediation" in the Risk Oversight section most likely means:
- A. Without the need for additional financial resources
- B. Without bypassing standard audit procedures
- C. Without management serving as a go-between or gatekeeper
- D. Without prior approval from external regulators
- Answer: C
- Explanation: "Without requiring management intermediation" means the Enterprise Risk Committee can access the Chief Risk Officer and external consultants directly, without management acting as an intermediary or controlling the flow of information.
