# The Economic Impact of International Student Enrollment on Regional Economies

A longitudinal study released by the Higher Education Economic Research Center has quantified the significant economic contribution of international student enrollment to regional economies in university towns across the country. The research, spanning a 10-year period and encompassing data from 45 institutions, concludes that each international student contributes an average of $58,000 annually to the local economy through tuition, housing, food, transportation, and discretionary spending.

Beyond direct spending, the study employs a multiplier analysis to estimate the broader economic impact. For every dollar spent by an international student, an additional $1.40 is generated in the local economy through secondary effects such as employment creation, tax revenue, and increased demand for goods and services from local businesses. In aggregate, the 45 institutions studied generated an estimated $14.2 billion in total economic impact during the most recent academic year, supporting approximately 92,000 jobs in their surrounding communities.

The research also examines the long-term economic benefits that accrue when international graduates remain in the region after completing their studies. Approximately 38 percent of international graduates at the surveyed institutions obtained employment within 50 miles of their university within two years of graduation. These graduates tend to work in high-skilled occupations, earn above-median salaries, and contribute to entrepreneurial activity at rates significantly higher than the general population, with 12 percent starting their own businesses within five years.

However, the study raises concerns about the sustainability of current enrollment levels, citing increasing global competition for international students, rising geopolitical tensions, and immigration policy uncertainty as potential headwinds. The researchers recommend that universities and regional economic development agencies collaborate more closely to create welcoming environments and post-graduation career pathways that enhance their competitiveness in the international student market.
