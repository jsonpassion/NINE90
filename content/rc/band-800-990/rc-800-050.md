# Chapter 11 Reorganization Trends Among Mid-Market Companies

The number of Chapter 11 bankruptcy filings by mid-market companies — defined as businesses with liabilities between $50 million and $500 million — increased 34 percent in 2025 compared with the prior year, reaching the highest level since 2020. The surge reflects the cumulative impact of elevated interest rates on companies that refinanced or incurred floating-rate debt during the low-rate period of 2020-2021 and are now confronting debt service obligations that have increased by 60 to 100 percent.

Analysis of the filings reveals distinct sectoral patterns. Healthcare services companies accounted for 22 percent of mid-market filings, the largest single sector, driven by a combination of reduced reimbursement rates, labor cost inflation, and the wind-down of pandemic-era government financial support programs. Retail and consumer services companies represented 18 percent of filings, while technology and software companies, many of which had relied on venture debt and revenue-based financing, comprised 15 percent.

The restructuring process itself is evolving. Pre-packaged and pre-negotiated Chapter 11 cases, in which the debtor and its principal creditors agree on the terms of a reorganization plan before the bankruptcy filing, now account for 45 percent of mid-market cases, up from 28 percent five years ago. These expedited proceedings typically achieve confirmation of a reorganization plan within 60 to 120 days, compared with 12 to 18 months for traditional contested cases. The efficiency gains benefit all stakeholders by preserving enterprise value that would otherwise be consumed by prolonged professional fees and operational disruption.

Creditor recoveries have varied significantly depending on the nature of the debtor's capital structure and the viability of the underlying business. Secured creditors have achieved average recoveries of 72 percent, while unsecured creditors received an average of 24 percent — a figure that has declined from 31 percent five years ago, reflecting the increasing prevalence of heavily leveraged capital structures with multiple layers of secured debt.

Market participants expect the current elevated level of filings to persist through 2026, as approximately $1.2 trillion in leveraged loans and high-yield bonds are scheduled to mature between 2025 and 2027, creating refinancing pressure for borrowers whose creditworthiness has deteriorated since the original issuance.

## Q1. What is the primary driver of increased Chapter 11 filings?
- A. New regulations making it easier for companies to file bankruptcy
- B. International trade disputes forcing companies to cease operations
- C. A wave of fraud investigations triggering corporate failures
- D. The impact of elevated interest rates on companies with debt incurred during the low-rate period
- Answer: D
- Explanation: The surge reflects "elevated interest rates on companies that refinanced or incurred floating-rate debt during the low-rate period of 2020-2021."

## Q2. What advantage do pre-packaged Chapter 11 cases offer?
- A. They allow companies to avoid repaying any debt whatsoever.
- B. They guarantee full recovery for all creditors.
- C. They achieve faster confirmation (60-120 days), preserving enterprise value and reducing costs.
- D. They eliminate the need for court supervision entirely.
- Answer: C
- Explanation: Pre-packaged cases "achieve confirmation within 60 to 120 days" and "preserving enterprise value that would otherwise be consumed by prolonged professional fees and operational disruption."

## Q3. What can be inferred about the trend in unsecured creditor recoveries?
- A. Unsecured creditors are recovering more money than in previous years.
- B. All unsecured debt is being forgiven without any recovery.
- C. Unsecured creditors now receive priority over secured creditors.
- D. Increasingly leveraged capital structures are leaving less value available for unsecured creditors.
- Answer: D
- Explanation: Unsecured recoveries declined from 31 to 24 percent, "reflecting the increasing prevalence of heavily leveraged capital structures with multiple layers of secured debt."

## Q4. The term "enterprise value" in the restructuring context refers to:
- A. The market price of a company's stock on the day of filing
- B. The liquidation value of a company's physical assets
- C. The total economic value of a business as a going concern
- D. The amount of government subsidies received by the company
- Answer: C
- Explanation: In restructuring, "enterprise value" represents the total value of the business as an operating entity, which stakeholders seek to preserve through efficient reorganization processes.
