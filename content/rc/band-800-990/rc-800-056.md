# Board Memorandum — CEO Succession Planning Framework

TO: Board of Directors, Nominating and Governance Committee
FROM: Lead Independent Director
DATE: December 5, 2025
RE: Comprehensive CEO Succession Planning Framework and Readiness Assessment

This memorandum presents the Nominating and Governance Committee's recommended CEO succession planning framework for adoption by the full Board. While succession planning has been a standing agenda item for the Committee, the Board has recognized the need for a more formalized and rigorous approach, particularly given that our current CEO, who has served with distinction for nine years, has indicated a desire to transition from the role within the next 24 to 36 months.

The recommended framework addresses three succession scenarios. The Emergency Succession Protocol establishes immediate contingency arrangements in the event that the CEO becomes unexpectedly unable to serve. The protocol designates the Chief Operating Officer as interim CEO, with authority to exercise all CEO powers for an initial period of 90 days while the Board conducts a deliberative process to determine permanent succession. The interim appointment does not create any presumption that the COO will be selected as permanent CEO.

The Planned Succession Process, which is the scenario most directly relevant to our current circumstances, establishes a structured evaluation methodology for internal candidates. The Committee has identified four internal candidates currently occupying positions within two reporting levels of the CEO. Each candidate will be assessed against a competency framework comprising strategic vision, operational execution capability, stakeholder management effectiveness, cultural leadership, and financial acumen. The assessment process will incorporate 360-degree feedback, external psychological evaluations, and performance in expanded developmental assignments designed to test candidates' capabilities in areas outside their current functional expertise.

The External Search Contingency provides a framework for engaging an executive search firm in the event that the Board determines none of the internal candidates meets the required competency thresholds. The Committee recommends pre-selecting and retaining two search firms under standby engagement agreements to ensure immediate mobilization capacity if needed.

The Committee further recommends that the Board conduct formal candidate progress reviews on a semi-annual basis, with the first review scheduled for March 2026. The target timeline for announcing a CEO designate is 12 to 18 months before the anticipated transition date.

## Q1. What is the interim CEO arrangement under the Emergency Succession Protocol?
- A. The Board Chair automatically becomes CEO permanently.
- B. The COO serves as interim CEO for up to 90 days while the Board determines permanent succession.
- C. All executive committee members share CEO responsibilities equally.
- D. An external interim CEO is immediately recruited from the market.
- Answer: B
- Explanation: "The protocol designates the Chief Operating Officer as interim CEO, with authority to exercise all CEO powers for an initial period of 90 days."

## Q2. How many internal candidates have been identified?
- A. Two
- B. Three
- C. Six
- D. Four
- Answer: D
- Explanation: "The Committee has identified four internal candidates currently occupying positions within two reporting levels of the CEO."

## Q3. What can be inferred about the Board's approach to ensuring the best CEO selection?
- A. The Board has already decided which internal candidate will be selected.
- B. The Board is maintaining rigorous internal evaluation while preserving the option to search externally if needed.
- C. The Board considers only financial performance metrics in its evaluation.
- D. External candidates are preferred over internal candidates in all circumstances.
- Answer: B
- Explanation: The framework includes comprehensive internal assessment using multiple evaluation methods, plus an "External Search Contingency" with pre-selected firms, demonstrating a thorough dual-track approach.

## Q4. The phrase "developmental assignments" in the assessment process refers to:
- A. Educational courses at business schools for executive development
- B. Administrative tasks assigned to candidates as routine work
- C. Stretch roles or projects designed to test candidates' capabilities beyond their current expertise
- D. Assignments given to junior employees to evaluate future leadership potential
- Answer: C
- Explanation: The assignments are "designed to test candidates' capabilities in areas outside their current functional expertise" — deliberately expanding their roles to evaluate readiness.
